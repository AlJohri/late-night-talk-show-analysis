Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
More bad news for Obama from the polls
"A new Gallup poll says just 41% of Americans now approve of the job President Obama is doing. Talking Points believes the primary reason Barack Obama is falling in the polls is that independent voters have lost confidence in him. Apparently even some Democrats are wavering, and that is because the President has no big wins on his resume. The economy remains awful, Afghanistan is in trouble, the oil spill was chaos, and the President is spending a record amount of money. So rather than running on his record, the President is saying the other guys are worse. Will that stem the tide for President Obama? Doubtful. The major achievement of the Obama administration has been the passage of national health care, but polls show most Americans don't really want it, and it will take years to see if Obamacare is a positive for America. At this point there is only one thing that can save the President: A surge in the economy. Talking Points believes that if Mr. Obama falls below a 40% approval rating, Hillary Clinton may challenge him for the Democratic nomination in 2012."

The Factor invited FNC political analyst Karl Rove to evaluate the Talking Points Memo. "I'm giving you a score of 6 out of a possible 7," Rove joked, "on the FTPMRAQ (Factor Talking Points Memo Rove Agreement Quotient.) You're absolutely right that independents are deserting President Obama in droves and he has no big wins on his resume. The only thing on which I disagree with you is Hillary Clinton - even if Obama is under 40%, I don't think Secretary Clinton would be so disloyal as to challenge him in the primary. But I wouldn't be surprised to see Howard Dean challenge him from the left." Rove also criticized the administration's current strategy of deflecting blame. "They're playing a hand that will do the Obama presidency long-term damage. You want to maintain the view that a president is a strong and credible leader, but by adopting the attitude that 'it's not my fault and those guys would be worse,' he's making himself look weak."

Obama-watchers Alan Colmes and Monica Crowley also analyzed President Obama's falling approval ratings. "These day-to-day polls don't tell the whole story," Colmes insisted. "He has health care reform, he's reformed Wall Street, and I disagree with your take on stimulus - look how well the stock market is doing, and we've seen a resurgence of the economy in many sectors." But Crowley painted a far bleaker picture for the President and his party. "There is a trend that has been spiraling downward for months and, when you look historically, a 43% or lower job approval rating is a real danger zone. Republicans don't have to put a compelling agenda out now, they can win by default." The Factor argued that things could be far worse for the Democrats: "Mainstream Republicans don't have anyone articulating their belief system. If there were, Obama would be in worse trouble."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Ground Zero mosque controversy continues
New York City has approved plans for a $100-million Islamic mosque to be built near Ground Zero in lower Manhattan. The Factor invited Nihad Awad, head of the Council for American-Islamic Relations, to weigh in on the controversy. "I'm really disturbed about the misinformation," Awad said, "that has been incorporated into this. There is a false linkage between Islam and 9/11. I and others have condemned 9/11 and the terrorists, and there is no linkage between our faith and 9/11. There are 9/11 families who support the Islamic cultural center." The Factor reminded Awad that most 9/11 families vehemently oppose the mosque: "My community on Long Island has scores of people who lost family members in the World Trade Center. Wouldn't it be wise for the imam in charge of this to say, in deference to and respect for the families, I'm going to drop this project and put it five or ten blocks away? That's what should happen."
Personal Story Segment 
Newt Gingrich on the Ground Zero mosque
Former Speaker Newt Gingrich entered the No Spin Zone with his take on the proposed mosque. "It is a political act to create this facility close to Ground Zero," Gingrich declared, "and it's being led by an imam who said America was an accomplice in 9/11 and who has refused to condemn Hezbollah for terrorism. I have never condemned Islam, but I do oppose radical Islamists and Sharia law, which has horrific consequences. Why not turn the building into a genuine interfaith facility with a synagogue and a church and a mosque?" The Factor laid out the other side of the argument: "The Muslim community says it wants to have this display near 9/11 because they want to send a message that Muslims are peaceful. But I don't think they're going to get the money and I don't think they're going to build the mosque."
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Anchor babies and the Fourteenth Amendment
Some conservatives want to change the law that grants citizenship to any baby born in the United States, an issue The Factor explored with legal wizards Lis Wiehl and Kimberly Guilfoyle. "The 14th Amendment says if you are born in this country," Wiehl explained, "you are automatically a citizen, and the Supreme Court has upheld this. It's not just poor illegal aliens who are coming here - there are also rich people coming here for that purpose." Guilfoyle added that overturning the law would require a constitutional amendment. "It's not going to happen - this a political move because there is frustration over people being here illegally. The 14th Amendment is sacrosanct and they're not going to mess with it."
Back of Book Segment 
Obama and Don't Ask, Don't Tell
President Obama has been criticized by some gays who want him to overturn the military's "don't ask, don't tell" policy and allow homosexuals to serve openly. The Factor welcomed Fox Business anchor John Stossel, who denounced "don't ask, don't tell" as immoral and nonsensical. "It's a dumb policy," Stossel said. "The Democrats want to let gays in the military and I agree. Most countries allow gays in the military - the ones that don't are Cuba, China, India, Egypt, Iran, and North Korea. We're not in good company." But The Factor countered that "don't ask, don't tell" is a good compromise: "The military says openly gay soldiers pose a morale problem, so Barack Obama does what he usually does - nothing! He could get rid of the policy by signing an executive order."
