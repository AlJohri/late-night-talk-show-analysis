Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Ends Justify the Means
Watch more video in the Video Center...
"I have known Donald Trump for about 25 years and I can tell you he is a unique individual. When he first started running for president, he was about 4% in the polls and had virtually no chance to win if he conducted himself as a standard politician. But Trump saw the campaign as a mission. He sincerely believes most politicians are phonies who are not looking out for you. He also thinks the federal government is screwed up beyond belief and only an outsider can correct things quickly. So his campaign theme, in my humble opinion, is that the end justifies the means. His strategy is to get attention 24-7, starving the other candidates out. With the media paying so much attention to Trump, you don't know that much about John Kasich or Rand Paul or Carly Fiorina. We see them at the debates and that's pretty much it. At this point it's not important to the Trump campaign to discuss things like the nuclear triad or macroeconomics or how to conduct foreign diplomacy. It's enough for Trump to say he's a good deal maker, he'll make Putin his friend, and he'll kick some serious butt overseas. Some intellectual Americans despise that approach and some pundits here at Fox News are among those folks. For some reason that rankles Donald Trump and, as I told him last night, I don't know why. Trump's strategy is clearly working as many of his supporters simply want an avenger, someone to blow up what they consider a corrupt system He understands the anger of the American people and he calculates that he can run on emotion and possibly win. That is Donald Trump's strategy."

Eric Bolling and Geraldo Rivera joined The Factor with their analyses of the Trump phenomenon. "Trump is a star and a celebrity," Rivera began, "and he owns Jeb Bush. Bush knows that even if he comes at Trump with full force, Trump will belittle and diminish him. Trump does not know what the nuclear triad is, but he was able to fix the skating rink in Central Park." Bolling argued that Trump's ignorance of specifics is not a problem. "He should not change anything, everyone is tuning in to see what he has to say. Let's first get the White House in Republican hands and then when he gets there the perception is that things will get done." The Factor compared Trump to another charismatic politician: "President Obama ran on 'hope and change.' I listened to a speech where he did not give one specific at all, but the crowd loved him and elected him twice. We could get the same thing with Donald Trump."
Impact Segment 
Dana Traces Obama's Missteps and Upcoming California Trip
Dana Perino looked ahead to President Obama's upcoming visit to San Bernardino and his attitude towards terrorism. "I think he wanted to calm the nation by not over-reacting," she said, "but he made the nation more nervous by under-reacting. Terrorism is the number one issue and the Republicans have shown a lot of seriousness about it." Perino also evaluated the GOP debate in Las Vegas. "The debates are now facing the law of diminishing returns and I don't think anybody broke out. Some people are highly entertained by Donald Trump, but I am not. I think his gender gap with women is a big problem." The Factor boldly predicted, "Cruz will win Iowa, Trump will win New Hampshire and South Carolina."
Truth Serum 
The Truth About Obamacare Costs
Congress is extending health coverage for 9/11 first responders who became sick after working at Ground Zero. The Factor was joined by FNC anchor Eric Shawn, who has a personal stake in the legislation. "I reported from Ground Zero for weeks after the attack," Shawn said, "and in November of 2001 I was diagnosed with 'World Trade Center Cough' that turned into bronchitis. Doctors tell me it's chronic, but this is nothing compared to the suffering and sacrifices of the first responders." Shannon Bream then administered some truth serum to President Obama's oft-repeated claim that health care reform would lower costs for Americans. "According to a new report," she said, "there has been an increase in premiums in 45 states and an increase in deductibles in 39 states. Costs are up across the board, that's the reality of it."
Unresolved Problems 
Deadlocked Jury In Baltimore
Watch more video in the Video Center...
A racially-mixed jury failed to reach a verdict in the trial of William Porter, the first of six Baltimore police officers charged in the death of Freddie Gray. Billy Murphy, the high-profile attorney representing the Gray family, opined on the hung jury. "Both sides got an adequate opportunity to present their cases," he said, "and the jury was a representative cross-section of the Baltimore community. What more can you ask? A majority of the time when prosecutors re-try these cases they win a conviction." The Factor criticized protesters who say they will only be satisfied if the cops are found guilty: "It disturbs me when people get out there demanding a conviction when they are not on the jury and they don't know what they're talking about. I certainly have no clue whether or not Officer Porter is guilty and we have to accept the jury's verdict."
Weekdays with Bernie 
Can Trump Do No Wrong With His Followers?
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Bernie Goldberg, obviously no fan of Donald Trump, entered the No Spin Zone and took aim at Trump's rabid fans. "Most Trump supporters are not like most Americans," he theorized. "They are angrier than most Americans, they are more frustrated and more alienated than most Americans. They are so wrapped up in his magic that they not only think he will win the White House, they think most Americans think like they do. They are so desperate to get out of living in modern America that they believe Trump's cynical nonsense. He says all these things that he can't deliver on, but they don't care!"
Watters' World 
Florida Christmas Controversy
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Jesse Watters headed south to Plantation, Florida and paid a visit to Mark and Kathy Hyatt, whose elaborate and ostentatious Christmas display has them at odds with local officials. "A lot of these things we make by hand," Mark said, "and people can come through the display and interact with it. A few thousand people to see this, many of them kids." One neighbor heartily endorsed the garish display, saying, "This has never been anything but a joy, it's brought so much happiness to so many families." Jesse then hunted down Plantation Mayor Diane Veltri Bendekovic, who refused to comment on her beef with the Hyatts. Watters warned the mayor that, even though coal is quite scarce in Florida, she is very likely to find a lump of the stuff in her Christmas stocking this year.
