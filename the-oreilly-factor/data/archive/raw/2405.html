Talking Points Memo 
NBC News comes to Obama's aid... again
"As everybody knows, NBC News, owned by the General Electric Company, has been extremely favorable to Barack Obama ever since he launched his Presidential campaign. Now the President's in trouble. All the polls show Americans turning against his health care vision. There is no question that the health care chaos is hurting the Democratic Party and President Obama. To the rescue: NBC News, which is now using its power to attack the health care protestors. Now there is something very disturbing about a major corporation GE allowing its news division to brand regular Americans racist, fascist, stupid because they oppose a public policy. In fact, I've never seen this before in my 35 years of journalism. Folks are using their Constitutional rights to protest a health care policy they believe will harm them and the country. A powerful corporation GE, which just received more than one billion dollars in low cost government loans (that's taxpayer money), demonizes the very people that provided them the cash. Is that unbelievable? And the folks are noticing. So far in August, MSNBC network, the most radical left arm of NBC News is down 24% in total audience while Fox News is up 21%. At 8:00 pm, the Factor is up 56% in the key 25-54 demo; MSNBC is down 15%. So the battle lines have been drawn as the nation is deeply involved in the most intense political debate in years. President Obama has the power of his office and most of the media squarely behind him. The folks have the folks. We'll see who prevails."
Top Story 
New poll reveals growing discomfort with Obama-care
The Factor invited pollster Scott Rasmussen and Dr. Caroline Heldman from Occidental College to discuss the latest poll numbers showing 49% of Americans opposing health care reform legislation, 61% of Americans unsatisfied with how things are going in the country today, and President Obama's job approval rating at its lowest point ever. Rasmussen said that Obama's slipping approval rating may not be solely due to the health care debate. "Voters were overwhelmingly opposed to bailouts that began in the Bush years, they were opposed to them when they continued in the Obama years. They also were opposed to the GM and Chrysler takeovers. And there is just this frustration that people are feeling and its coming out into play in the town halls we're seeing in the form of debating health care, but really it's a bigger issue," he said. Heldman agreed with Rasmussen: "We are seeing really a lot of partisan dissatisfaction, although some of it amongst independents, and what's happening at the health care debates is very much a reflection of broader concerns, not just health care," she said. The Factor explained why the health care debate may be politically troubling for President Obama: "I see this as Armageddon for the Obama administration. If this goes down in flames or he tries to ram it through in the fall with so many people against it (the majority of Americans, 58% not wanting it), I don't think he can come back from that."
Top Story II 
The White House's unsolicited health care campaign
Laura Ingraham entered the No Spin Zone to discuss reports from Fox News White House correspondent Major Garrett that the White House is sending unsolicited emails to urge Americans to get involved in the health care reform debate. Ingraham claimed that there is a clear double standard at play. "If the Bush administration had done anything like this, you would have the media up in arms, it wouldn't just be Major Garrett at Fox News," she said. The Factor also asked Ingraham whether she was offended that General Electric and NBC News are using their power to attack individual Americans over the health care debate and town hall meetings. "I think all Americans should be concerned about this. Something odd is going on here and it is clearly an effort by the part of someone at NBC." The Factor responded: "From the jump, NBC adopted Barack Obama as their Presidential candidate and then when he was elected, said 'We're going to get behind his policies and try to convince Americans that what the President does is good for them.' The way that it is unprecedented is that at the same time, General Electric is now benefiting economically from the Obama White House."
The Kelly File Segment 
Obama spokesman talks to Megyn Kelly
Megyn Kelly spoke with the Factor about her recent discussion on "America's Newsroom" with White House Deputy Press Secretary Bill Burton; Kelly questioned Burton about reports that Americans, who were encouraged to forward suspicious health care emails to the White House, would have their email addresses retained by government personnel as part, perhaps, of an "enemies list." Kelly was concerned that the White House was encouraging the submission of "fishy emails." "There is a real question of whether the White House, asking people to snitch on their neighbors essentially, chills free speech in this country. Even the ACLU has come out and said this is a bad idea, that the White House shouldn't be doing this." The Factor agreed: "The perception is bad, they [the White House] don't need people to inform on other people." Kelly also addressed a story about a police officer in Ohio that is filing a lawsuit because his fellow police officers are making causing him 'emotional pain' and making his life a 'living hell' since his wife posed for Playboy magazine. "What's so hard about it; is it so hard to anticipate that the other cops are going to say, 'Hey, mind getting me an autographed copy of your wife's pictorial?"
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Did neglect kill Michael Jackson?
The Los Angeles times recently reported that Dr. Conrad Murray, Michael Jackson's personal doctor, left the pop singer alone under the influence of a powerful drug in order to make phone calls. When Murray returned, Jackson was dead or near death. Geraldo entered the No Spin Zone to discuss the latest updates. "We've been reporting that right along," Rivera said. "He gave Jackson the Propofol, gave him the powerful antisthetic that knocked him out. Michael had been using Demerol and OxyContin to such extent that his family wanted to do an intervention on him. What Conrad Murray did when walking away from Michael was such reckless conduct that it does amount, we believe, to if not manslaughter, than murder charges." The Factor agreed with Rivera's analysis of the future felony charges, but took issue with whether the Jackson family ever planned an intervention to stop Michael Jackson's drug use. "I think this is all an attempt by the family to kind of rehab the image after the fact, because this guy was using narcotics for a long time, and if there was going to be an intervention, it should have happened a long time ago." Rivera, however, believed that more of the story is waiting to be uncovered. "I think that there is more to this than one negligent doctor prostituting his art."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Is the GOP capitalizing on Dem difficulties?
Former Speaker of the House Newt Gingrich joined the Factor in a discussion of whether Republicans are taking full advantage of the controversial health care debate and President Obama's falling favorability ratings. "Here is an opportunity for the Republicans to come together with a really bold alternative that fits where the American people are, that offers substantial progress on health reform, but that doesn't have gigantic government or big tax increases or any of the things that are frightening people." Gingrich also felt that while Obama and the Democrats have the option of creating an entirely new health care bill after the summer Congressional recess, they may try to rely on their majorities in the House and Senate to pass legislation currently under discussion. "My hunch is that they will try to ram it through although it is decaying so rapidly and people are getting increasingly skeptical about it." The Factor took issue with Gingrich's prediction. "I disagree. If they did that, I think that would be the end of the Obama administration less than a year into his four year term."
Back of Book Segment 
The dumbest things of the week
Radio host Faith Salie and Fox News Correspondent Juliet Huddy entered the No Spin Zone to share one dumb thing or video that they have seen the past week. On her recent trip to Africa, some reporters claimed that Secretary of State Hillary Clinton was "physically exhausted," "having a bad hair day," and "feeling fat" after examining her appearance on the trip. Huddy took offense to the analysis. "They go immediately for the female things; she's angry at Bill because he's having a party in Las Vegas and she's jealous, or it's PMS, or she's having a bad hair day, why does it immediately go to the female, sexist realm?" Salie addressed how Heather Mills, former wife of Paul McCartney, compared herself to Mahatma Gandhi, Malcolm X, and Martin Luther King because she had to deal reports about her divorce fight. "She had to fight for 40 million dollars of Paul McCartney's money. It's incredibly dumb." The Factor also showed a video of an 11-year-old girl asking President Obama a question at a health care town hall in New Hampshire. The only problem was that the girl's mother ran the "Massachusetts for Obama" campaign. "The Boston Globe, ten minutes later, found out who the girl was and put it in the paper. How did they think they were going to get away with this?" the Factor asked.
Pinheads and Patriots 
Brad Pitt & General Electric
Thursday's Patriot: Brad Pitt, who has relocated to New Orleans with his wife Angelina Jolie, is considering running for mayor of New Orleans. And the Pinhead: General Electric, who posted an exhibition on their website saying that the Factor smeared the company because of allegations that GE sent equipment abroad to kill American soldiers.
