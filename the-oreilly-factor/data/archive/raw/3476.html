Talking Points Memo 
Sticking with the President
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guest: Charles Krauthammer
"In just about an hour President Obama will discuss how to deal with Syria, which is killing thousands of people using poison gas. Most Americans do not want the USA to strike Syria militarily, and now the Russians have proposed a plan in which an international coalition would take control of Syria's chemical arsenal. Many of us are very skeptical about President Putin neutralizing his pal Assad, and Putin has said there will be no deal at all unless Obama backs away from force - not only now, but in the future. Ridiculous! However, the President will tell the nation tonight that he has approved a 'diplomatic pause' in the campaign against Syria. That is, he is slowing down threats of military action and exploring possible diplomatic solutions. For Assad and Putin, that's a good thing, and it's a good thing for President Obama, who faces embarrassment in Congress. There is no question that Mr. Obama is in trouble, and thus he needs some spin. The Democrats will say that without President Obama's saber rattling there would never have been the offer of diplomacy, as bogus as that offer now seems. As Talking Points said last night, there is no way President Obama comes out of this unscathed. Most Americans know he has bungled the Syria situation and foreign policy in general. There's a good chance he will never recover from this, no matter what happens. And what is likely to happen is some debate at the UN that will lead nowhere and maybe an international force that might monitor Assad. That will keep the tyrant in power and Putin will keep pulling his strings. In the meantime, the USA just keeps getting weaker and weaker."

The Factor invited Charles Krauthammer to add his perspective on Syria. "This is entirely a Russian ploy," he declared, "and it wasn't proposed because they were afraid of a U.S. strike. They know there is no support in Congress, they know the President is ambivalent, and they heard Kerry say that any strike would be 'unbelievable small.' The reason Russia made the offer is that they saw a way to give Obama a face-saving way out in which nothing happens in Syria. Russia's major ally Assad stays in power, America doesn't strike, and the rebels are demoralized. This is exactly the situation the Russians want and Obama walked into the trap because it is a political face-saver for him."
Top Story 
Can Obama Do No Wrong?
Guests: Monica Crowley & Alan Colmes
Monica Crowley and Alan Colmes entered the No Spin Zone to assess how much President Obama has been damaged by the Syria debacle. "Poll numbers show President Obama sliding in terms of job approval," Crowley said. "The American people are focused on this and they see him as a weak and indecisive leader. President Putin is eating Obama's lunch, he's running circles around the United States." Even Colmes questioned the administration's inability to put forth a coherent message. "We don't know whether this strike will be one day or two days and now they're talking about three days because Assad is moving weapons around. Is there an exit strategy? The numbers are going down for Obama because people don't understand exactly what the mission is."
Stossel Matters Segment 
A Libertarian View
Guest: John Stossel
The Factor challenged Fox Business host John Stossel, an opponent of military action in Syria, by informing him that most Americans were also against taking action against Hitler in the late 1930's. "I resent that libertarians are smeared as isolationists," Stossel replied, "because we are not. I want to engage with the world, I just don't want to try and run the world." Stossel elaborated on how Americans can engage with murderous and hostile regimes. "We trade with people, we try to get our movies and our music to influence their culture, we smuggle in books. The alternative does more harm than good. If we launch missiles, what if we miss like Clinton did in Libya? Will the new leadership be an improvement?" The Factor insisted to Stossel, "This danger is intense, you have babies being gassed, and if the United States becomes isolationist it will be worse for us down the road."
Personal Story Segment 
Hollywood & Syria
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guest: Ed Asner
Most liberal entertainers, among them Bruce Springsteen, Susan Sarandon, George Clooney, and Sean Penn, have been conspicuously silent as President Obama urges a military attack. The Factor asked actor Ed Asner, who has spoken out against military action, why his left-leaning colleagues are so quiet. "Maybe they feel that a majority of Americans oppose war," Asner theorized, "and their voices don't need to be heard on this issue. I'm opposed to involvement because I'm sick of war, but I certainly think many liberals question how much to condemn a particular action by this President. They tread softly because they know there is a sea of Republicans out there who will do it for them. Barack Obama is not my progressive!" The Factor concluded, "Hollywood did a lot to elect Barack Obama, but now he's in a difficult position where he goes against their orthodoxy."
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Hot Legal Stories!
Guests: Kimberly Guilfoyle & Lis Wiehl
Legal analysts Lis Wiehl and Kimberly Guilfoyle looked at the 1993 international agreement banning the use of chemical weapons. "Syria didn't sign this," Wiehl reported, "so they are not beholden to it. And generally, enforcement of this has no teeth, this was a symbolic effort." Guilfoyle turned to the proposed law in California that would prohibit former spouses from posting photos and videos they had taken together. "Even if both parties consent to photos or recordings," she explained, "if you get divorced and then disseminate this you would be breaking this law. I think this law is going to pass and people will be prosecuted because there will be angry victims who want someone punished."
Reality Check Segment 
John Kerry Telling Tall Tales?
Watch more video in the Video Center...
The Washington Post is calling Secretary of State John Kerry a liar because Kerry recently claimed he was opposed to the war in Iraq in 2003. The Factor's Reality Check: "Kerry voted for the resolution to attack Iraq so the Post has awarded the Secretary of State 'Four Pinocchios,' their highest level of scorn for politicians who do not tell the truth. It's kind of embarrassing for Kerry." On another front, U.S. intelligence officials say that at least 100 former Gitmo detainees have returned to terrorism. The Factor's Check: "When you hear the far left scream about 'innocent' people at Gitmo, please remember that it's just more propaganda that puts all of us in danger."
