Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Romney wins Michigan primary
"Congratulations to Governor Romney for winning in Michigan - he seized upon the shaky economy and drove home his successful business resume. Right now, four Republicans have a shot at the presidential nomination: Romney, McCain, Huckabee and Guiliani. Unlike past campaigns, Super Tuesday on February 5th is the ballgame - all four contenders will stay in until then. Now some believe that the Republican nomination will not be decided until the convention in Minneapolis at the beginning of September; certainly that's possible. That's because for the first time in memory, there is no establishment candidate. It is different on the Democratic side, where the machine is behind Hillary Clinton. But Barack Obama continues to wage an effective campaign. Right now most polls show him leading in South Carolina and Nevada. If I had to bet, I'd take the Patriots over the Chargers, and Hillary over Obama. But - and remember I said this - I now think Senator Obama will be the vice-presidential nominee if he doesn't win outright. Write that down."

To comment, political analyst Dick Morris came aboard. He saw nothing but division for the Republicans. "What's happening really is the party is falling apart. You have the national security wing - people that are mainly concerned about terrorism and the war on terror. They're for Giuliani, and some are for McCain. And then you have the fiscal conservative wing - they want economic deregulation, Club for Growth, low taxes... they're for Romney. And then you have the social conservatives. They're for Huckabee - anti-abortion, pro-life, anti-gay marriarge." The Factor noted that Hillary Clinton as a Democratic nominee would do a good job uniting the GOP. Morris agreed and pointed out that Obama hadn't done a good job of attacking Hillary's weak points in Tuesday's debate. "Maybe Obama should hire you," The Factor suggested to Morris.
Impact Segment 
Celebrities throw big bucks to the candidates
The Factor began the segment by listing several celebs who gave money to political candidates. Jeffrey Ressner, a correspondent for Politico.com, offered his assessment of the situation. "Obviously Hollywood gives a lot more money to the Democrats than they do to the GOP candidates," Ressner began. "But I think there's more of a split between the younger hipper actors and talent giving to Obama, and some of the older, more established folks giving to Hillary." The Factor agreed, and said that there may be peril for someone in Hollywood who openly supports a Republican candidate. "There's no question that Hollywood is left - and it has been since the blacklisting of the 50s. However, if you give money to a Republican, conservative candidate, and you're a Hollywood player - a writer, a producer, a director or a star - that's going to be public, and you may lose work."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
ACLU defends Sen. Larry Craig in sex scandal
When news broke that the ACLU was helping to defend Idaho Senator Larry Craig against charges stemming from his infamous bathroom incident, The Factor wanted to find out why. To help get to the bottom of the situation, Megyn Kelly - a Fox News anchor and an attorney - offered her expertise. Kelly explained that the ACLU was arguing based on a previous ruling on privacy in a public restroom. The Factor saw a familiar pattern: "So what the ACLU did was, they took a logical decision by the Minneapolis courts, and they perverted it - pardon the pun - to advance their own agenda, which is a free for all, you can do anything anytime you want, anywhere you want." Kelly pointed out that there was another flaw in the ACLU's argument: "They're forgetting the fact that Larry Craig allegedly peeped inside of the stall for two whole minutes. There's no protection for that... There's a peeping statute that makes that unacceptable under any circumstances."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Swingers' club in Texas under fire
A small town outside of Dallas is fighting a group of people who set up a swingers' sex club called "The Cherry Pit" in a residential neighborhood. A Factor producer went undercover to expose some of the activity at the club. Ed Klein, the attorney for the sex club, entered the No Spin Zone to defend his clients. The Factor suggested to Klein that the club move out of the residential neighborhood and into a rented facility in a more appropriate area. Klein explained that this wouldn't be possible: "In a rented facility, it then would be [considered] a business - which it isn't. And it would be required to get licenses... and the activities that goes on may not be legal in a commercial establishment." The Factor was skeptical: "That's the whole ruse here - we all know it's a business. If you don't give the $50 [suggested donation] they don't want you there - and they make it quite clear. I can't sympathize with you, and I think you're going to lose your suit. Because this is obviously a business that has loopholes."
Miller Time Segment 
Dennis Miller on Tony Snow
Comedian and radio talk show host Dennis Miller came to talk about former White House press secretary Tony Snow's appearance on Bill Maher's show. On the show, Tony was ganged up on by the liberal Maher and the three liberal panelists. The Factor wanted to know if Miller would have gone on the show."You know, if I'd just kicked cancer and had my own hair back, I could care less. Once you've kicked the big C, the small c in "chattering class" really matters not a twit to Tony... Bill [Maher]'s a good friend of mine, but I don't go on his show anymore; because as far as echo chambers go, it makes Carlsbad look like deep space." Miller was also fascinated by the Texas swinger club story in the previous segment. "Why did I know The Cherry Pit's lawyer was going to be balding with a pony tail? When the "Fantasy Room" has mahogany embossed plywood on the walls, you got to set your fantasies a little higher." The Factor agreed, heartily: "Look, when you have to bring Lysol with you, it's not a good situation - I understand that."
Back of Book Segment 
Policing the 'Net: Internet rumors about Obama
There are persistent but false Internet rumors going around that Barack Obama is a Muslim. Internet watcher Mary Katharine Ham tried to explain why these lies persist: "These are sort of urban legends flying around on the Internet - and obviously this one has the potential to be much more harmful because it can affect a presidential race. Obama needs to get out there and say it's not true." The Factor had a reminder for all viewers who may have believed the ridiculous rumor: "I just want to once again tell everybody that when you read it on the net, assume it's not true."
Pinheads and Patriots 
Who's helping, and who's hurting?
Wednesday's Patriot: Actor Johnny Depp, who entertained kids at a British hospital and donated $2 million after the hospital treated his daughter for E-Coli. And the Pinhead: Sean Penn who's mad at The San Francisco Chronicle for referring to Venezuelan president Hugo Chavez as a dictator.
