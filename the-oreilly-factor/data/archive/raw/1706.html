Talking Points Memo 
Your money and the wobbling economy
"The hard left is happy this evening because the economy is wobbling. For years these people have been wishing economic disaster on America because they despise President Bush. The 'no tax cuts for the rich' crowd fervently believe the federal government should control the money flow, taking from the wealthy and giving to the less well off. Back in 2000 - the last year of the Clinton presidency - there was a mild recession in America, and that helped President Bush get elected. And so the cycle is playing out again; but this time a bad economy will be helpful to the Democrats. So who's to blame here? First off, we are. Americans have not cut back on oil consumption, and millions of us bought homes we could not afford. So we consumers have promoted economic chaos. The oil companies and banks are to blame as well - believe me, there is no recession in the American oil industry. They are exploiting an uncertain world and using artificial mechanisms - like futures trading - to raise prices. So high energy prices and greedy lending have led to the shaky economy we have now. All of us are to blame: the folks, the government and the corporations. Let's hope some powerful person has the guts to fix it."
Top Story 
Evaluating last night's Democratic debate
Last night's Democratic debate in South Carolina featured several tense and testy exchanges between frontrunners Barack Obama and Hillary Clinton. Democratic strategist Liz Chadderdon thought that Obama won the debate. "The reason I think he won is not necessarily because of his performance last night, but because leading up to this debate the Clinton strategy was for Bill to be the attack dog, drag Obama into the mud and for Hillary to remain above the fray. And then last night, Hillary got into the fray. So all of a sudden, she's down there in the mud too." Fox News analyst Juan Williams was sad to say that he thought Obama actually lost. "[Obama and Clinton] had a truce. That's why they didn't have these fireworks at the Nevada debate. But it was like a teakettle... eventually it's going to boil over. And when it blew, it blew on Obama." The Factor ended the segment by summing it up nicely: "So Liz thinks Obama won; Juan thinks Obama lost. And I... I don't care. I just wanted to bring you guys and let the folks decide on this one."
Personal Story Segment 
Actor Heath Ledger dies at 28
28 year old actor Heath Ledger was found dead Tuesday afternoon in his New York City apartment. Fox News entertainment reporter Bill McCuddy interviewed him two years ago for the movie Brokeback Mountain. "The guy was on the edge of becoming a much much bigger star." The Factor wanted to know if McCuddy knew of any history of substance abuse. "He was drinking during the interview," McCuddy admitted "He had a bottle of wine." But, McCuddy continued, the actor was not known for being a heavy partier. Fox News entertainment reporter Jill Dobson had praise for the late actor: "He was someone that got both critical acclaim, and had a lot of fans." The Factor, meanwhile, wanted to tread with caution: "Whenever anybody dies at 28 years old, obviously this is a terrible thing. So we're going to be very careful how we categorize it."
Impact Segment 
Illegal alien charged with 47 criminal counts
Authorities in Arizona have charged an illegal alien with an unbelievable 47 counts stemming from his attacks on six girls ages 12 to 15. The suspect had been deported four times between 1994 and 2003, but obviously managed to make it back into the country. Defense attorney Monica Lindstrom has been following the case from Phoenix. "It clearly shows that our system is broken when it comes to deporting somebody and keeping them out of our country," Lindstrom started. "Since 1988, he was deported four times. And every time, he got back into our country. And not only did he get back in, but he committed even more and more crimes. Now the thing that's on the forefront of people's mind is how could this happen? Why didn't they put him in jail long enough?" The Factor had a simple solution to the problem: "I would have probably put him in jail for 50 years so you never would have seen him again."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
The Catholic church and exorcism
Last month Pope Benedict suggested that bishops all over the world expand personnel trained in exorcisms. The Factor started the segment by warning the guest - Catholic Theologian Anthony Gonzalez - that non-believers would be hard to convince: "You know a lot of people watching tonight think this is a bunch of superstitious mumbo jumbo. They're going to mock you. They're going to say that you're a primitive and all kinds of things." Gonzalez laid out his case: "You have good and you have evil. And evil is the absence of good... it is personified in what we call the fallen angels. And the fallen angels are very active on the earth." The Factor wanted to know what made Gonzalez a believer. "Because I've experienced it myself," Gonzalez replied.
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Two disturbing cases
Fox News legal analysts Megyn Kelly and Lis Wiehl looked at three cases - two of them very disturbing. One of the cases involved a man taking pictures of teenage boys at water polo practice and then posting the photos to a gay porn website. Kelly thought that the guy was - initially, at least - not breaking the law: "It's OK for you to photograph a boy even in a Speedo bathing suit. That is legal, if it's a public place. What's not legal, in my judgment, is to then post it on a gay porn Web site." The Factor warned viewers to be careful: "What parents and everybody else needs to understand is anybody with a camera in any public place can photograph your young child, and it could show up on a porn Web site."
Back of Book Segment 
Great American Culture Quiz
Fox News anchors Steve Doocy and Martha MacCallum returned - after a hiatus of several weeks - to face each other in the Great American Culture Quiz. The Factor asked five questions, including these: "Which politician was the president of Fabian's fan club?" "Uday and Qusay Hussein were killed in a gun battle in 2003 - in what town did they die?" "Oprah's first TV news job was in what city?" Both contestants fared poorly - Doocy squeezed out a win with an anemic score of 2 to 1.

Pinheads and Patriots 
Who's helping, and who's hurting?
Tuesday's Patriot: Young actress Scarlett Johansson, who is visiting troops in Iraq, Kuwait and the Gulf. And the Pinhead: Presidential candidate John Edwards, who keeps trotting out his line about "200,000 veterans sleeping under bridges" even though The Factor has proven him wrong.
