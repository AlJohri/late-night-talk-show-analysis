Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Is America a nation in decline?
"There is no question that most media want to see Barack Obama elected president, and the media has a new theme, that the USA is a 'nation in decline.' The political component is this: if the 'decline' theory takes root, more voters will want significant change. So, let's take a look at it. Years of energy neglect have finally caught up with us, but the good news is that the USA is now being forced to change from fossil fuels to alternatives. Overseas, the US military has almost single-handedly defeated Islamic fascism in Iraq. In Afghanistan, NATO forces rout the Taliban whenever there's a direct confrontation. Back home, there is certainly a gap between rich and poor in America, but that's because liberal policies have led the public education system to collapse in many places. Talking Points believes that Americans who become educated can compete with anyone. So there you have it - a magnificent military winning on all fronts, a painful transition to better fuel, which will happen, and a population that is basically honest and decent. America is not in decline; it remains the world's best hope."

The Factor discussed the state of the nation with FNC's Juan Williams, who also complained about the ubiquitous reports of America's decline. "There are books and television specials," Williams pointed out. "This is coming from the media, and the New York Times is taking the lead. What they're saying is that if you really want change, Barack Obama is going to come to the rescue." Fox News analyst Mary Katherine Ham argued that many voters are turned off by predictions of doom. "People are feeling some pain, but most Americans still feel they can achieve in this economy because America is the land of opportunity. As a candidate you don't want to be all gloom and doom or you turn into John Edwards."
Impact Segment 
Obama's move towards the center
Barack Obama has shifted his position on gun control, free trade, surveillance and other issues. The Factor welcomed anti-war activist Medea Benjamin, who complained about Obama's strategy. "It's a big mistake to criticize the very base that is providing energy for his campaign," Benjamin declared. "The activists are giving contributions and registering voters, and I don't think shifting to the center is good politics." Benjamin also issued a direct threat to Obama: "We do have options - we have Ralph Nader and the Green Party candidate Cynthia McKinney - so he'd better be careful." The Factor countered that Obama has little choice but to swing to the center. "A far-left candidate is not going to be elected president, and Barack Obama knows he has to win the hearts and minds of the working class."

Factor Follow Up Segment 
Feds continue crackdowns on illegal employees
Federal immigration authorities are aggressively targeting illegal aliens and their employers. But in Providence, Rhode Island, 26-year-old illegal Marco Riz is accused of kidnapping and raping a woman last month. The Factor was joined by Rhode Island Governor Donald Carcieri, who denounced city leaders in Providence. "Illegal immigration is a huge issue in our state," Carcieri said. "I passed an executive order to get our law enforcement working with ICE to get control of this issue. But unfortunately, our capital city of Providence has not climbed on board, they're not really interested." The Factor praised Carcieri for his efforts. "You recognize the chaos illegal immigration is causing, but Providence Mayor David Cicilline is on record saying he doesn't care. We've invited him to come on, but he's hiding under his desk."

Body Language Segment 
Christie Brinkley, Alex Rodriguez & more
The Factor asked body language expert Tonya Reiman to watch video of New York Yankees star Alex Rodriguez, who is embroiled in a nasty divorce battle, as he was being pursued by the media. "He was holding his chin tucked down and avoiding eye contact," Reiman pointed out. "I would have advised him to make eye contact and put your head forward, which gives the impression that you're the one in control." Reiman also watched tape of a distraught Christie Brinkley, who insisted that her own nasty divorce case be open to the public. "She came across as somebody who is quite vexed. She might not have realized the extent of what this case was going to be."
Personal Story Segment 
8 states holding out on Jessica's Law
42 states have some form of Jessica's Law mandating tough minimum sentences for child molesters, and Factor producer Jesse Watters caught up with three governors whose states are among the holdouts. Idaho Governor C.L. "Butch" Otter said he would sign Jessica's Law if it reaches his desk; Colorado's Bill Ritter angrily and erroneously claimed his state has "the best sex offender laws in the country;" and Wyoming Governor Dave Freudenthal promised he'd sign a mandatory minimum "in a heartbeat." The Factorwas joined by defense attorney Remy Spencer, who explained why so many of her colleagues oppose Jessica's Law. "Defense attorneys don't like it when discretion is taken away from trial judges," Spencer said. "But as a society we should say that children should be protected and people who commit egregious crimes against young children should be severely punished."

Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Republicans attack Obama on energy
A new Republican TV ad lambastes Barack Obama for opposing nuclear energy, offshore drilling and a gas tax holiday. The Factor provided this reality check: "It's true the Democrats have no clear strategy to fight escalating gas prices, but the GOP doesn't have one either. We need a disciplined plan combining drilling, increased nuclear and coal production, and mandatory alternative fuel development. The first candidate to lay out a specific plan will win the election." The Factor also aimed this reality check squarely at the New York Times: "Times reporter Jacques Steinberg recently wrote an article saying that our competitors were catching up to Fox. The truth is that in the second quarter Fox News was the 6th highest rated cable channel in the country, CNN was 21st, and MSNBC 27th. So Fox & Friends poked a little fun at Steinberg for misreporting the situation and they used an unflattering caricature of him. Folks at the Times are outraged, as are the far-left loons at Media Matters. But this is rich! The New York Times used caricatures of me when they slashed my book 'Culture Warrior.' So let me get this straight, New York Times people: we can't mock you, but you can mock us? If Times editor Bill Keller would wise up, perhaps their business would improve."
