Top Story 
Analyzing the Republican Debate
Watch more video in the Video Center...
The Factor began Wednesday's post-debate analysis with a comment on the coverage: "The CNN moderators made the debate very personal, using Donald Trump's attacks on other Republicans to frame questions. That was a waste of time and obviously a strategy to energize ratings. Instead of incisive questioning, we got 'gotcha.' It was all about the show, not about solving vexing problems." Charles Krauthammer then entered the No Spin Zone to evaluate the participants. Here are Professor K's grades and critiques:

Donald Trump: "I thought he had a C-minus performance. He was on the receiving end of some pretty strong ripostes, and on policy he was not up to speed the way the others were. It's harder for him when he's in an arena with other people who are well versed on the issues."

Ben Carson: "I'd put him in the same range. He was rather weak on the issues, he didn't shine, and this was not his kind of forum."

Jeb Bush: "B-minus. He was on sometimes and did rather well, but he's not a man who is really quick and sharp. He did better than in the first debate and on the issues he was rather strong."

Scott Walker: "Again, he was a bit stronger and more forceful than the first debate, but I don't think he changed his fortunes one way or another."

Carly Fiorina: "She gets an A-minus. She had two great moments - the one where she countered the insult from Trump in a way that was short and electric, and later she took on Iran and Planned Parenthood at the same time. She was extremely strong and carried it off very well."

John Kasich: "I'm not sure if he helped himself. I'd give him a C."

Rand Paul: "He did rather well, but he was sort of on the periphery. He made some points, but they were not points that would rally people to him."

Mike Huckabee: "He didn't get a lot of time and he had to break in. I'm not sure it was his fault, but it wasn't his night and I give him a C."

Chris Christie: "He had a good night, he rose above expectations. He showed himself to be controlled but really strong, and he had a bit of humor. I'd give him a B."

Ted Cruz: "C-plus. He didn't have a signature moment, but I think he put himself in position to inherit the Trump constituency if it is every relinquished.

Marco Rubio: "He came in a close second to Carly Fiorina. He was very strong, particularly in the exchange on what should be done about Russia and Syria. He had a consistently strong performance across the board on every issue."
Top Story 
Bret & Brit Grade the Candidates
The Factor asked Brit Hume and Bret Baier for their reactions to the debate. "I agree with Charles," Hume said, "that Carly Fiorina was the big winner, even though she seemed a bit stern. She was extraordinarily authoritative on the issues and facts. Ben Carson held his own, but there was nothing magical about his performance, and Jeb Bush had a pretty good night." Baier turned to GOP front-runner Donald Trump. "I think he had a decent night, he did not have an implosion moment and there was no attack that really killed him. Ted Cruz had facts and substance, but I thought his addressing the camera was a little strange at times. Scott Walker did himself some good, but he'll have a long uphill battle to get back into this fight." The Factor pointed out that Trump got to speak for about 18 minutes, while Walker only had 7 minutes of air time.

Bernie: Who Impressed Voters Tonight?
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Bernie Goldberg opined on the performance of the CNN moderators. "I thought Jake Tapper did very well," he said, "although I wasn't thrilled that he had to cut some of the answers off. As for Hugh Hewitt and Dana Bash, I'll be looking for their pictures on milk cartons in the morning because they were missing in action. Overall, I thought a lot of the questions were good ones." As for the participants, Goldberg handed out mediocre grades to Scott Walker, Mike Huckabee, and Ted Cruz, higher evaluations to Rand Paul, Ben Carson, John Kasich, Jeb Bush, and Chris Christie. He reserved his very highest praise for Marco Rubio and Carly Fiorina, while he put Donald Trump in detention for his "cheap shots." The Factor disagreed with Goldberg's assessment of the front-runner: "If I had to grade Trump tonight, I would give him a B. He was under so much pressure, CNN wanted to make it all about him, and he had to be reactive rather than proactive."
Top Story 
Senator Ted Cruz Provides An Inside Look at the Debate
Watch more video in the Video Center...
The Factor welcomed Ted Cruz and asked the Senator to grade his own performance. "I thought the debate was terrific," Cruz said, "and I was very happy with where it went. I would have liked more air time, but I'm encouraged because our momentum exploded after the first debate and I'm hopeful we'll see the same thing. The grading will be done by the American people, and I don't think they are interested in the back-and-forth schoolyard insults. They're interested in substance and there is a real difference in the records of the candidates. I think people are looking for a consistent conservative and in this debate they were able to see a stark difference between my record and the record of the other candidates."

The Architect Weighs In

The Factor invited reaction from Karl Rove, who helped engineer George W. Bush's two presidential victories. He began by identifying candidates who performed the best. "Carly Fiorina had a great night," Rove declared, "just as she did in the first debate. She demonstrated a fluency with the issues, and so did Marco Rubio. The 'most improved' was Chris Christie, who was very strong, and I thought Jeb Bush showed that he can take a punch and give a punch." Rove gave middling grades to Scott Walker, John Kasich, and Ted Cruz, then turned to the men he considered the big losers. "Ben Carson comes across well, but there wasn't anything tonight that was memorable. Mike Huckabee was not much of a presence and Rand Paul looked desperate for attention. I also don't think Trump did well." In the interest of fairness, The Factor reminded viewers that Rove and Trump "aren't big fans of each other."
Top Story 
Final Word on the Debate
Finally, Mary Katharine Ham and Juan Williams put forth a few thoughts on the big debate. "Carly Fiorina did very well," Ham asserted. "She was commanding and authoritative - I like a strong woman who makes herself heard when she's surrounded by dudes who are trying to interrupt her." But Williams suggested that the debaters didn't win over many Democrats or independents. "I don't think it was a very good debate for the Republican Party," he theorized. "I think the same response on Planned Parenthood that made Fiorina a winner with Republicans turned off a lot of people." The Factor concluded with this assessment: "I think Marco Rubio won the debate on points, but it was close. I don't think there were any disasters, and Donald Trump was outnumbered ten-to-one."
