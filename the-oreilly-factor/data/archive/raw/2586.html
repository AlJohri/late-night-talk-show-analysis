Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
President Obama and your money
"Speaking in New York City today, President Obama told the world that the U.S. financial system is still largely unaccountable and needs new rules to cut down on corruption and speculation. The financial system is far too easy to game and con men often run wild. But the President has to be careful because the feds can not run Wall Street; in fact the feds may be leading the country into bankruptcy. But back to the basic problem: Maybe you are like me and work hard for your money, maybe you invest, trusting the system is fair. And maybe you got burned as I did when the financial system collapsed in 2008. I had no idea that my brokerage firm, Merrill Lynch, was trading bad paper. You probably trusted financial institutions as well, but we were conned. Reluctantly, I have to support new laws to reform the financial industry. But I have to pray somebody in Washington will finally look out for the folks."

The Factor was joined by radio talk show host Laura Ingraham, who opposes the financial reform bill making its way through Congress. "We all want reforms," Ingraham said, "but we have to remember that what started this downward spiral was Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac pushing bad loans out to the marketplace, and none of that is addressed in this so-called reform bill. We can't allow populist sentiment to override what we know about what the government is doing." The Factor agreed that Fannie and Freddie must be included in any reform effort, declaring "if the final bill has no protections against the federal mortgage agencies, I will not support it." Ingraham also spoke about her radio confrontation with New York Times columnist Charles Blow, who compared tea parties to a "minstrel show." "The most amazing thing about this," Ingraham reported, "is that Blow went to Dallas to cover the tea party, but didn't interview a single person there. The conclusion I drew is that he could have written that piece calling it a 'minstrel show' by watching C-SPAN. He didn't have to go there."
Impact Segment 
ACORN gone, but fragments remain
Bertha Lewis, who heads the ACORN organization, warned an audience that "we are in a time that is going to dwarf the era of Jim Crow and segregation." Lewis also compared the tea party movement to a "bowel movement" and extolled socialism, leading The Factor to this conclusion: "When the tea party is yelling about 'socialism,' Bertha Lewis just admitted that the tea party is right. How illuminating!" Also on the tea party front, professional narrator Lance Baxter, whose voice is heard in Geico commercials, called a conservative organization and posed this question: "What percentage is of people who are mentally retarded work for the organization?" The Factor's reaction: "Mr. Baxter did get a call back, but it was from Geico saying he was fired. Why do these far-left loons fear the tea party so much? Here's the deal: The far left fears a populist uprising against liberal policies and they have no way to combat the tea party other than to smear it. Tea party demonstrations are largely peaceful and the debate is going against the far left, so they're desperate."
Personal Story Segment 
One of three Navy SEALs acquitted
One of three Navy SEALs arrested for roughing up a suspected terrorist in Iraq was acquitted by a military jury this week. The Factor has frequently denounced the charges as bogus, but Geraldo Rivera put forth another perspective. "This happened in 2004," Rivera pointed out, "when there had been the awful murder and mutilation of four Blackwater guards. And earlier that year the Abu Ghraib prison scandal happened and you remember the awful press the military received for not vigorously pursuing the people responsible. So that's the context in which these three Navy SEALs were charged." But The Factor was unswayed by Rivera's reasoning: "Nothing happened to this terrorist, who had a little cut on his lip, and all the eyewitnesses said these people didn't do anything. The charges should be dropped right now against the other SEALs."
Culture Warriors Segment 
Are skinny models scrutinized less on TV?
ABC and Fox TV have both rejected a Lane Bryant lingerie ad featuring "plus-sized" models in skimpy underwear. The Factor asked Culture Warriors Gretchen Carlson and Margaret Hoover whether the networks acted properly. "According to Fox and ABC," Carlson said, "the ad was too salacious for the 8 o'clock hour, and I think it was. And here's the thing - with plus-size models you're going to get more cleavage because they're plus-size." Hoover suggested that Lane Bryant is playing up the controversy in order to get free publicity. "They framed it so it was a double standard, saying the networks were allowing Victoria's Secret models but not Lane Bryant. But that's not what happened - Victoria's Secret is airing in the 9 o'clock hour." The Warriors turned their focus to evangelist Franklin Graham, who has accused Islam of provoking violence and mistreating women. Because of that, the Army has rescinded Graham's invitation to a Pentagon prayer service next month. "He's defending his remarks," Carlson said. "He believes treatment of women within Islam is 'horrid,' and the Pentagon has acquiesced to pressure." Hoover praised Graham for having "the courage to say that a part of Islam is calling for violence, which is accurate."
At Your Beck and Call Segment 
Is Glenn Beck seditious?
Some far-left pundits have accused Glenn Beck of coming close to "sedition," defined as inciting rebellion against the government. Beck entered the No Spin Zone to respond. "I think that was the most amazing thing I've ever heard about me," Beck professed. "I love my country, I love the Constitution and I want it restored." Beck theorized why he's been the subject of such vitriol. "Many of the people in charge of our government are 1960's radicals and they have a very fundamental plan to transform America. They need to make the typical American, the one who says our Founding Fathers were good, look radical." The Factor encouraged Beck to ignore his critics: "This is just dumb - nobody thinks you're trying to overthrow the government. I think they fear you and they fear Sarah Palin."
Back of Book Segment 
Great American News Quiz
Steve Doocy and Martha MacCallum once again faced off in the Great American News Quiz. Among the questions: "How much money did Democrats receive from Goldman Sachs employees during the 2008 election cycle?" ... "Where did Ronald Reagan make his last public appearance?" ... "On which TV show did Conan O'Brien get his start as a writer?" Doocy, perhaps rusty after a few weeks off, fired blanks on the first three questions, and MacCallum cruised to an easy win.
Pinheads and Patriots 
American Idol & Kim Kardashian
Thursday's Patriots: The American Idol franchise, which has donated well over $100 million to charity during its run. And the Pinheads: Both Kim Kardashian, who invited a female Australian TV host to fondle her butt, and the host, who eagerly complied.
