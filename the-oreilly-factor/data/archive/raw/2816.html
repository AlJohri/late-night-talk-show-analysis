Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Far left steps up to help American union workers
"There is no movement in Wisconsin as the union stalemate goes into its fourth week. And Americans remain divided somewhat over Governor Scott Walker whether he is right in trying to diminish union power or the workers are correct in trying to maintain their status. Enter far-left bomb thrower Michael Moore who traveled to Wisconsin over the weekend ... So the question is does Mr. Moore help or hurt the union cause in the court of public opinion? "Talking Points" believes that independent Americans see Moore's presence as evidence that union demonstrators are not mainstream. Especially when Moore says stuff like this:
MOORE: America is not broke. Not by a long shot. Wisconsin is not broke. It's one of the three biggest lies of the last decade.

O'REILLY: Well, if facts mean anything, Moore is not telling the truth. The nation owes more than $14 trillion and Wisconsin has a $3.6 billion short fall... There's no question that public union benefits are breaking the bank. So, on this very intense controversy there is truth, Michael Moore, and it is grizzly."
Liberal Ellis Henican argued Michael Moore is helping the union cause. Ellis reminded O'Reilly that he is not Moore's target demo, but many people actually listen to the filmmaker when he says the middle class is being squeezed, and he's successful at energizing and focusing the base. Conservative Andrea Tantaros disagreed, arguing Michael Moore hurts the union cause.
Impact Segment 
Qaddafi using air strikes to attack opponents
Things are bloody in Libya, as the dictator Qaddafi is using violence against his own people to hang onto power. Today, President Obama said the violence is unacceptable and warned Qaddafi's inner circle that they will be held accountable for the continued violence. Fox News chief political analyst Brit Hume: "The first thing [President Obama] should do is stop making threatening statements without any sort of sense of what he's going to do to back them up." Brit also thinks Obama should set up a no-fly zone over Libya as soon as possible. The Factor worried that the Arab world and al Qaeda will use any military intervention by the USA against us.
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Obama aggressive enough on conflict?
With union conflict raging in the USA and the Middle East in chaos, President Obama is perceived in some quarters as not being aggressive enough in addressing both problems. But is that fair? Fox News analyst Juan Williams cautioned that we don't want to be involved in a third war in Libya or give Qaddafi ammunition against the "imperialist" Americans. Fox News analyst Mary Katharine Ham accused the President of being all talk: "Barack Obama, since the campaign, has been a guy who makes grand pronouncements, and then he's sort of done."

On the subject of Michael Moore, the Factor asserted the documentary filmmaker is hurting the unions by showing up in Wisconsin. Juan thought the move makes the unions happy because they're desperate for media attention; Mary Katharine said Moore is making the union protesters look like the fringe rather than the mainstream.
Personal Story Segment 
Why the big jump in gas prices?
Gas prices are up 33 cents in just two weeks, the second largest jump in the nation's history. White House chief of staff Bill Daley said the administration is contemplating tapping into our oil reserves, but the Factor doesn't see what good that is going to do since we don't have a supply problem. Democratic analyst Erica Payne thought the Obama administration, in an effort to bring down oil prices, is trying to signal to the market that the U.S. is willing to take major action. Fox Business anchor Stuart Varney warned against demonizing speculators. He also said it's time for a crash program to start drilling in the Gulf.
Weekdays with Bernie Segment 
NYTimes' Keller bashes Fox News viewers
Speaking before the New York Press Club, Times editor Bill Keller: "I think if you're a regular viewer of Fox News, you're among the most cynical people on planet earth...I cannot think of a more cynical slogan than 'fair and balanced.'" Keller then backtracked saying his comment wasn't meant to be a criticism of Fox News viewers.

Fox News analyst Bernie Goldberg: The liberal media lives in a bubble, where they don't think they're guilty of any of the things of which they accuse others.

Bernie found the New York Times slogan 'All the news that's fit to print' far more cynical than 'fair & balanced.' The Factor assumed FNC and its huge influence must be getting under Keller's skin. Otherwise, why would he even bother bringing the network up at the press club?
Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Sarah Palin bashes Kathy Griffin
Sarah Palin bashes Kathy Griffin: "You know, Kathy Griffin can do anything to me or say anything about me because she's a 50-year-old adult bully and has-been comedian -- but I would just ask for respect for my children."

Some folks are angry that Rep. Peter King (R-NY) is investigating Muslim terrorism within the United States. AP: 300 people showed up in New York City to demonstrate against Mr. King's hearings, which begin on Thursday. The slogan of the protesters was "Today I am a Muslim too."

Hugo Chavez received another visit from actor Sean Penn, who is very grateful that the Venezuelan leader is helping him out in Haiti. Check isn't quite sure what Mr. Chavez has actually done for Haiti.

New Quinnipiac temperature poll: Which politician do you feel warmest about? Michelle Obama 60.1 degrees, Bill Clinton 59.2 degrees, Chris Christie 57 degrees, Barack Obama 56.5 degrees. And the politicians bringing up the rear in the warmth category: Harry Reid 34.8 degrees and Nancy Pelosi 32.9 degrees.

Comedy Central's Jon Stewart goes to bat for the teachers: "And don't even try, don't even try, and compare teachers to Wall St. There is no contest!" He then plays some FNC clips where anchors appear to justify the fact that teachers don't get paid as much as people with careers in finance.

Time Warner has fired actor Charlie Sheen today. And now he's got even more trouble -- the witches and warlocks of Salem, Massachusetts are mad at him for referring to himself as a warlock.
Factor Mail 
Viewers sound off
Mimi Ito, Boulder, CO: "Geraldo's argument that we should avoid labeling Muslims who commit terror acts 'terrorists' could have easily fit into the dumbest things of the week."

Bill Morini, Baldwinsville, NY: "I'm going with Geraldo on this one. Terrorism is an overused word for an act that is supposed to instill fear in those who oppose the advancement of ideology."

Another viewer mentions that the Factor has a huge fan base in Argentina, which he discovered after a recent vacation to South America.
Pinheads and Patriots 
You decide who's who!
In addition to all his other problems, Charlie Sheen has now become an object of derision, getting mocked relentlessly on Saturday Night Live. Are the SNL writers pinheads or patriots?

In related news, Friday's P&P featured a clip of Sheen's 'Two and a Half Men' co-star Jon Cryer doing a bit as a temporary receptionist for Ellen DeGeneres. 60 percent of viewers found Mr. Cryer's ability to laugh at himself patriotic.
