Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Do you believe in redemption?
"The Factor believes in redemption, thus we had no problem with Don Imus returning to the radio Monday. Most theologies preach forgiveness and since every human being makes mistakes, if someone asks for forgiveness, it should be granted. And here's the irony - the media drove the Imus story, and what he did was wrong, but not as wrong as the media intentionally deceiving the public. There's no question that media deceit has reached critical mass; the defamations and falsehoods are staggering. Last week Cynthia Tucker of the Atlanta Journal-Constitution, Maureen Dowd of the New York Times, and Lauren Rikleen of the Boston Globe all distorted my position on racism, using almost identical language. Talking Points wishes the dangerous media dishonesty would get half the attention Mr. Imus is getting. In the big picture, Imus is just an entertainer, but the corrupt press is a far different story. It wants to drastically alter this country, and will try to destroy anyone who stands in its way. The upcoming presidential race will clearly demonstrate what I am telling you right now. Pay attention."

For more on the Imus story, The Factor welcomed Rev. Al Sharpton, who was among the radio host's most vocal critics last spring. "He was fired and he paid a price," Sharpton said, "and Imus himself this morning said he should have been fired. But our objective was to make sure this kind of language and talk would not be tolerated, and we won. It didn't have to do with Imus, it had to do with standards." The Factor reiterated that Don Imus was crucified by media outlets that are hardly above reproach. "Isn't it hypocritical for this to be driven by a media that is corrupt? The fabrications that people try to make up are outrageous."

Campaign 2008 Segment 
Huckabee pulls into lead in Iowa polls
Republican Mike Huckabee has been surging in Iowa, and The Factor opined on the media's infatuation with the Arkansas governor. "It's the first time I saw the media in love with a Baptist minister. I think it's because they are actively rooting for a Democrat to win and they know Huckabee can't compete against Obama or Clinton." FNC analyst Kirsten Powers disputed that theory. "The field is a little lackluster," Powers said, "and people have been looking for someone more conservative. I don't think you can say the media is building up Huckabee because he'll be a weak candidate." On the other side of the political divide, GOP strategist Margaret Hoover predicted that Huckabee will soon be jilted by the media. "Is the media artificially building up Huckabee? Yes. Are they doing it so they can take him down later? Absolutely. But I don't think they're doing it so he will win the nomination."

Factor Follow Up Segment 
YouTube question connection to Clinton under fire
Retired General Keith Kerr, the Hillary Clinton supporter who posed the question about gays in the military at last week's Republican debate, entered the No Spin Zone. Kerr admitted he should have informed CNN that he is on a Clinton steering committee. "I haven't really decided whether I'm going to vote for Hillary Clinton, but I did add my name to her steering committee. I should have made CNN aware of all my political affiliations." Kerr also explained why he wants gays to serve openly in the military. "About 70% of younger servicemen feel comfortable around gays. 'Don't ask, don't tell' degrades military readiness - we've thrown out 12,000 people since it was enacted - and many generals and admirals want it repealed." The Factor lauded Kerr for his service and commitment, but argued that the current policy is working. "Most of the Army is made up of traditional people, and they tell me that it's better for morale if we just leave this as a personal matter, that we don't broadcast what people's proclivities are."

Unresolved Problems Segment 
No resolution in Drew Peterson case
Illinois police officer Drew Peterson, whose third wife Kathleen Savio was found dead and whose fourth wife Stacy is now missing, has adamantly proclaimed his total innocence. But forensic pathologist Michael Baden, who was hired by the Savio family to examine her body, told The Factor that evidence suggests otherwise. "Kathleen Savio had been beaten up and drowned, and that should have been classified three-and-a-half years ago as a homicide." The Factor suggested that Peterson benefited from a corrupt system. "Savio's death was ruled an accident and I think the fix was in. There is a cover up, and we're not going to let it stand because we don't want cover-ups in America. The Savio family should sue for millions."
Personal Story Segment 
Pat Buchanan & Bill Bennett on America
Conservative stalwarts Pat Buchanan and William Bennett have both written books about the challenges facing the U.S. Bennett put forth an optimistic vision of America and its resilience. "If you look at the history of the United States," Bennett said, "you have to believe it is still the last great hope of mankind. There are big things that are wrong - immigration and spending and education - but we have been in much worse straits before. We'll come out of it." But Buchanan sounded a near-apocalyptic alarm. "We borrow two-billion dollars a day, we save nothing, we have commitments to defend the whole world. The melting pot is broken and is rejected by the cultural elites. My fear is that the border with Mexico will disappear and the United States will deconstruct along lines of race and class. I believe we are on the path to national suicide, and I don't think the elites of both political parties are attuned to what's going on."
Back of Book Segment 
Body language: Don Imus / Miss Puerto Rico
Tonya Reiman began her body language with Miss Puerto Rico Ingrid Marie Rivera, who told The Factor last week that she was sabotaged during the pageant. "I don't think she was being deceptive," Reiman said. "She consistently looked in the same direction and had all the signs of someone who was genuine." Reiman then watched a clip of Don Imus as he returned to the radio and recounted his ordeal last spring. "It was as if this conversation was distasteful to him. He's done with this, and I got the impression this was the regular old Don Imus." Finally, Reiman scrutinized USO president Edward Powell, who protested that his organization is doing all it can to help the troops. "The words that come to mind are awkward and nervous. He seemed like he wasn't comfortable in his own skin, and he didn't come across as natural."
Pinheads and Patriots 
Who's helping, and who's hurting?
Monday's Patriots: All of you who have shopped at the BillOReilly.com store, where a portion of each sale goes to charity. The Factor reported that "we have now donated more than a half-million dollars this year alone." And the Pinhead: White House correspondent Helen Thomas, who implied that U.S. troops are wantonly killing Iraqi civilians.
