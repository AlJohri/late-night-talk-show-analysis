Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Obama, power and the falling stock market
"As we predicted weeks ago, the debt debacle has weakened President Obama. All the polls show that Americans are furious that the government couldn't settle an obvious problem without a chaotic exposition. Also, people are getting hurt as the stock market plummets - the Dow was down more than 500 points today, 1,300 points in the past two weeks. How can any American be optimistic when the national debt is growing by more than $4 billion every single day? Mr. Obama knows he's in trouble and he knows that Republicans will not cooperate with him any longer because they want to see him defeated in 2012. So for the next sixteen months little will get done in Washington as the House will pretty much block everything the President wants to do. So instead of fighting a losing battle on Capitol Hill, the President has already turned his attention to the upcoming campaign. Mr. Obama knows he can't win reelection without independent voters, so he is talking about 'making investments in our people.' The problem with making investments in the folks is that there isn't any money, America is broke! Simply put, the President's economic policies and liberal tenets of big government have failed. Will the voters realize that? We will not find out until November 6th, 2012."

The Factor was joined by Fox Business anchor Stuart Varney, who reacted to Thursday's 500+ point drop in the Dow. "All the indicators point to another recession," Varney said, "and the President does not have a plan to deal with it. In fact, he has virtually no policy options, so you're looking at a slowdown in America, an administration that doesn't know what to do about it, and an exploding crisis in Europe. Down goes the market!" Varney theorized that President Obama is boxed in by ideology. "If he were to change course and get rid of Obamacare, get rid of these crippling regulations and instill confidence in private enterprise, he would be repudiating his entire presidency. He is ideologically driven and he is taking us down a road that Americans don't want to go on." The Factor lamented that "there isn't any confidence in President Obama's economic leadership."

For another view, The Factor welcomed liberal professor Marc Lamont Hill. "People have a lack of confidence in the American political system," Hill said, "not just in President Obama. Today's crash has much more to do with European markets than it does with Obama's leadership. He has a very clear plan to help the economy but he has never had the opportunity to execute his plan because he's always caving to the right. And now he has fully shifted himself into campaign mode, which means he is playing from the center and maybe even the right." But The Factor complained that President Obama is pursuing an ideological vision that has failed everywhere: "He wants to model the economy and the 'social justice system' on what Western Europe has done, yet now we see country after country collapsing. You would think Barack Obama would notice that and say maybe it's not the right thing to do."

Conservative author and columnist Ann Coulter entered the No Spin Zone with her take on President Obama's economic stewardship. "He's made a hash of things," Coulter said, "and there is no hope in sight. The reason he is losing is power is because he has made a hash of the economy because he has crazy ideas about increasing the size of government while the Republicans want to increase the size of the private sector. As long as you have these two fundamentally different ideas about what you do to run a country and an economy, they are going to disagree. But gridlock is better than letting Democrats do what they want. If the choice is between gridlock and socialism, I'll take gridlock!" The Factor tried to convince Coulter to sing a few bars of "Happy Birthday" to President Obama, but she politely declined.
Personal Story Segment 
Far left reacts negatively to Huckabee 9/11 video
Former Arkansas Governor Mike Huckabee, now a Fox News host, has produced an animated educational video depicting Al Qaeda's attacks on 9/11. The Factor invited Huckabee to respond to those on the left who object to the cartoons. "These are videos that are written by a panel of experts," Huckabee said, "then checked out by historians and teachers. The idea is to take kids through American history in a positive way, and 91% of liberals who have actually watched them say they like them and would get them for their kids. So it's very frustrating that MSNBC, which has fewer viewers than you have people going to the bathroom during a break, goes after us like this." Huckabee refuted accusations he is "cashing in" on 9/11. "The irony is that Michael Moore made $222 million off of 'Fahrenheit 9/11,' and I don't remember anyone on MSNBC screaming about 'blood money' or 'profiteering.' This is like saying Steven Spielberg was cashing in on the Holocaust because he made 'Schindler's List.' A portion of the profits from this will go to 9/11 charities."
Culture Warriors Segment 
Poll reveals God's 'approval rating'
When a polling firm asked Americans about God, only 52% "approved" of God's performance, while 9% disapproved and 40% were not quite sure. The Factor explored those numbers with Culture Warriors Gretchen Carlson and Margaret Hoover. "This is a stupid poll," Carlson declared, "with a stupid question. Approve of God's performance on what, the economy? Half the people in the United States are blaming God for their problems?" Hoover pointed out that, comparatively speaking, God actually fares reasonably well. "For as bad as things are, God has a 52% approval rating, which is higher than Barack Obama, Speaker Boehner or any other elected official. So let's keep in mind that God's doing pretty well. What I want to know is who are the 40% who are not sure?" The Factor defended the poll as original and provocative: "We live in a world that is in absolute turmoil, where bad things are happening all the time, and I think that was the spirit of the poll. If they had called me up, I would have said I'm not smart enough to pass judgment on what God is doing."
Back of Book Segment 
Great American News Quiz: Terrorism edition
Steve Doocy and Dagen McDowell, each of whom is known to toss an occasional verbal bomb, tested their knowledge about folks who set off actual bombs. Among the questions: "The Munich Olympic massacre was carried out by a Palestinian terrorist group calling themselves what?" ... "Due to a TV taping that ran late, which legendary Motown group narrowly missed being on the Pan Am flight that was blown up over Scotland?" McDowell, who had beaten Doocy twice during previous pinch-hit appearances, went 4-for-5 Thursday to continue her remarkable dominance.

Pinheads and Patriots 
White Sox manager Ozzie Guillen
