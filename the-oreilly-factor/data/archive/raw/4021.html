Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
The Biggest Smear So Far in the Presidential Campaign
Watch more video in the Video Center...
"In a vicious and false attack on Senator Marco Rubio, the head of the DNC, Congresswoman Debbie Wasserman Schultz, reached a new low in political attack discourse. Earlier this week, Senator Rubio held a fundraiser at the Dallas home of real-estate guy Harlan Crow, who has one of the biggest private collections of historical memorabilia in the world. The collection includes items from the Third Reich, including stuff from Hitler. Wasserman-Schultz used that historical collection to smear Rubio, calling it 'the height of insensitivity and indifference.' What is truly astounding is the woman's ignorance. I have been to Mr. Crow's home and seen his collection of history. It is amazing, any museum would be proud to display what he has. Both good and evil are represented in an educational way. There are documents and letters from crucial moments in American history, as well as world history, and those include items from villains such as Hitler, Stalin, Tojo and Saddam Hussein. By attacking Rubio using the Holocaust, the head of the Democratic National Committee has shamed herself and her party. She should immediately resign. There comes a point when politics gets so dirty it corrupts the nation, and we are at this point. And it's not just the Democrats who are using despicable tactics, although they seem to embrace them at a high level. Some Republicans are comfortable using falsehood and innuendo to smear liberals. And when Talking Points sees that from the GOP, it will be reported. The presidential race now underway has the potential to get very nasty. We will fight against that, and we begin with Debbie Wasserman Schultz."

The Factor welcomed the target of Debbie Wasserman Schultz's astonishing smear, Republican Senator Marco Rubio. "This didn't surprise me," Rubio lamented. "On top of Debbie Wasserman Schultz's political extremism, she tends to do these sorts of things. But I was outraged because Harlan Crow is a private citizen who participates in the political process and anyone who knows him for two seconds realizes he's not a Nazi sympathizer. This was such a despicable attack that even liberal media outlets have criticized her, and it is indicative of the levels of desperation in the Democratic Party." The Factor worried aloud, "I think this will get worse, this could be the dirtiest political campaign in the history of this country."
Impact Segment 
Muslim President Comments
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Some prominent Muslims have called for Dr. Ben Carson to quit the presidential race after he questioned whether an Islamic person should ever be president. The Factor spoke with Carson about the controversy his remarks have engendered. "I said quite clearly in that interview," Carson protested, "that I have no problem with anyone, whatever their religion, who subscribes to American values and is willing to put the Constitution above their religious beliefs. I would not advocate that we put a Christian in the White House if their goal is a theocracy." But Carson also doubled down on his original criticism, saying, "When I look at Islamic nations, I see places that do not give women equal rights."
Personal Story 
Papal Address to Congress
Pope Francis spoke to a joint session of Congress Thursday, during which he seemed to urge more liberal immigration policies. The Factor dissected the pontiff's message with two members of Congress, Republican Tim Huelskamp and Democrat Henry Cuellar. "I don't think this was an appeal for open borders," Cuellar said, "but he said we need something that is just and humane. We were all foreigners at one time and we need some way to treat people with justice and fairness. We have to secure our borders, but in a humane way." Huelskamp actually interpreted the pope's remarks as an endorsement of U.S. immigration policy. "In the last year we have admitted about one-million new immigrants, we are the most inviting country in the world. I think the pope was giving a compliment to the American people." The Factor added, "I don't think Pope Francis would ever advocate breaking the law."
Personal Story 
New Presidential Polling
A new Fox News poll has Donald Trump leading the Republican field with 26% of likely GOP voters supporting him. He is followed by Ben Carson at 18%, Marco Rubio and Carly Fiorina at 9%, and Ted Cruz at 8%. On the Democratic side, Hillary Clinton leads Bernie Sanders 44% to 30%. The Factor asked FNC polling guru Dana Blanton what was most surprising. "It was the number of people who say they feel betrayed by their party," she replied. "62% of voters say they feel betrayed by the politicians in their party, and among Republicans they say it's because they don't feel their party has done all it could do to stop President Obama's agenda. This is driving them to outsiders. On the Democratic side, Hillary Clinton is at the lowest number we have seen - she was at 61% back in June."
Weekdays with Bernie 
A Vicious Attack
Bernie Goldberg analyzed the anti-Marco Rubio smear by Democratic National Committee chair Debbie Wasserman Schultz. "This is what some people in politics do when they are afraid of their opponent," he theorized, "and they are afraid of Marco Rubio. Harlan Crow also has statues of Lenin and Stalin and Mao, so is he also a communist sympathizer? It's interesting that Debbie Wasserman Schultz and other Democrats didn't say anything when John Kerry described Yasser Arafat, who was dedicated to the destruction of Israel and every Jew in Israel, as a 'statesman.' Where was the liberal outcry over that? Where is the liberal outcry over the constant demonstrations on college campuses portraying Israel as the most evil nation on earth? I would not be surprised if Debbie Wasserman Schultz is a mole planted by Republicans to make Democrats look bad."
Watters' World 
The Pope in NYC
Watch more video in the Video Center...
As Pope Francis touched down in New York City, Jesse Watters asked some Gothamites a few questions about the pope. Some of them weren't quite sure about the pope's name or even his religion. One woman claimed that the pontiff's name is 'Jimmy," while a man asserted that his name is 'Jesus.' As for the pope's religion, the answers included Baptist, Mormon, and Buddhist. Finally, a couple of New Yorkers actually confessed their sins to Watters. "When I was younger," one of them admitted, "I got in trouble for knocking over some tombstones." The Factor, under the powers granted by Fox News, absolved Watters for his arguably sacrilegious segment, saying, "God likes good humor, so you are forgiven."
