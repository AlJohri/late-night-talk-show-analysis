Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Bribes, politics and health care reform
"Christmas comes once a year, but if this health care bill becomes law the government will be playing Santa with your tax dollars 365 days a year. Harry Reid, Nevada's own Kris Kringle, has been handing out goodies, buying off Senators so he can ram through a health care bill this week. But why the rush? Reid and company fear that if the Senate adjourns, he could lose some of his more wobbly votes. Also, the Democrat leadership believes that if they pass any big government program, it will be in place forever. They've slipped language into the bill that would prohibit any future Congress from modifying a key Medicare provision. How is that constitutional? This is the latest evidence that the so-called health care debate is a fraud. It has never been about 'bending the cost curve,' it has never been about incremental change or patient care. It is about liberals taking advantage of an opportunity to engage in a massive redistribution of wealth. This bill will radically alter the relationship between the American people and our government. So don't let anyone fool you - there is nothing 'moderate' about this bill."

Laura welcomed Republican Senator John Thune, a leading opponent of the Senate bill. "This is what happens when you do backroom deals at the eleventh hour," Thune groused. "We were presented with a 400-page amendment on Saturday with all these sweetheart deals for specific Senators. It's insanity to try and bind a future Congress and say we can't go back, so we will do legal challenges. This whole thing smells so bad and that's why the American people are turning on it." Laura suggested that the abortion issue and Democratic pro-life Representative Bart Stupak may still derail the bill: "Stupak says he has thirty Democrats who will not sign on to any bill that does not include the House anti-abortion language. Not a single pro-life organization finds this Senate bill a real compromise."
Barack and a Hard Place Segment 
Grading Obama in 2009
Obama-watchers Alan Colmes and Monica Crowley gave their year-end report cards to President Obama. "On the economy," Colmes began, "I give him a B-minus. We have a long way to go but certain economic indicators are up." Crowley, to absolutely no one's surprise, flunked the president. "I give him a flat-out F because there's a 10% unemployment rate and we have an excruciatingly slow jobless recovery. This president's policies are only going to kill job creation." On foreign policy, Colmes gave the president a C "because he is too slow getting out of Iraq and I don't support sending 30,000 troops to Afghanistan." Crowley handed out a barely-passing D because "he has projected weakness throughout this first year by throwing our allies under the bus and by embracing adversaries." On the subject of health care, Colmes and Crowley gave President Obama a B and an F, respectively if not necessarily respectfully.
Personal Story Segment 
Is Obama's black support wavering?
The Congressional Black Caucus recently criticized President Obama for not doing enough to help unemployed minorities, and now left-wing actor Danny Glover has turned on the president. Laura welcomed Democratic strategist Michael Brown, who defended the president's performance. "He's going through a tough time relative to all communities," Brown stated, "not just the African American community. We're still in the worst economic times of our generation because of policies laid down by the Bush administration. The bottom line is that the president is still fixing the problems of the past administration." Laura reported that residents of big cities are suffering from disproportionately high unemployment. "It's abysmal, and the only people not hurting are government workers because all we keep doing is hiring government workers."
Factor Flashback Segment 
Planned Parenthood tries to silence employee
Laura introduced Bill's November interview with 29-year-old Abby Johnson, former director of a Texas Planned Parenthood clinic, who explained why Planned Parenthood is trying to prevent her from speaking out. "I think they are nervous," Johnson said, "that I am going to tell people that Planned Parenthood's business model has changed. They're not about prevention; they are really trying to increase their abortion numbers because that is the most lucrative part of their business." Johnson also described her sudden switch to the pro-life camp. "I actually saw an ultrasound-guided abortion procedure, and what I saw on the screen was a 13-week baby fighting for its life. I had flashes in my head of my own daughter and I was thinking 'what am I doing?' Planned Parenthood really tries to instill in their employees and the women coming in for abortions that this is not a baby, that it's just a mass of cells."
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Cop pulls gun in the midst of a snowball fight
Major Nidal Hasan, charged with killing thirteen people at Fort Hood, claims his rights were trampled because he was not allowed to pray with his brother in Arabic. FNC legal wizards Lis Wiehl and Kimberly Guilfoyle scrutinized Hasan's complaint. "His lawyer says his religious rights were violated," Wiehl reported, "because he was not allowed to talk with his brother in whatever language he wants. The crazy thing is that Hasan's first language is English, and of course they have the right to restrict this." Guilfoyle added that Hasan is in the military justice system, where the rules are different. "The military has every reason to believe that he still poses a danger. They should not allow him to communicate in Arabic with his brother because he poses a risk." The legal aces turned to veteran Washington detective Michael Baylor, who brandished his handgun when he was caught in the middle of a snowball fight. "This looks bad," Wiehl said, "because everyone else was unarmed. But there is a big caveat - he says he was called to the scene because there was a report of somebody with a firearm. If that can be substantiated, it would make sense that he should be able to have a firearm out."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
New info on the White House party crashers
A report in the Washington Post criticizes White House social secretary Desiree Rogers for her role in the case of White House party crashers Tareq and Michaele Salahi. Author Ronald Kessler agreed that Rogers was obviously negligent. "Every administration," Kessler said, "has had the social secretary's office at the guard post with a check list. But Desiree Rogers herself wanted to go to the state dinner and have a good time. These crashers could have been serial killers, they could have had biological or chemical weapons." Ingraham accused Rogers wanting "to be hobnobbing with people at the state dinner."
Back of Book Segment 
Rock the Vote encourages withholding sex
The political advocacy group "Rock the Vote" is encouraging young people to withhold sex from anyone who opposes health care reform. Laura spoke about the ad campaign with Jehmu Green, former president of the group. "'Rock the Vote' is the premiere organization working to connect young people to politics," Green said, "but I think it's unfortunate that they have dumbed down the important issue of health care reform to overt sexuality. There are so many other ways to be compelling and provocative." Laura added that Obama-care will cost many young people money: "If 'Rock the Vote' actually spent as much time reading through the bill, which mandates that young people have fewer choices in health care, then maybe they'd stop making their idiotic little videos. This doesn't educate anyone; it just makes 'Rock the Vote' look silly."
