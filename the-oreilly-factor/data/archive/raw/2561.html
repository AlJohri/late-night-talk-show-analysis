Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
The White House's new health care strategy
"The White House and Nancy Pelosi have been fairly successful at changing the focus of the health care debate away from the content of the bill and what it's going to do to the quality of our health care. Democrats want to talk about anything but the number of new government bureaucracies created, the reduction in health insurance choices, and the number of unborn children whose lives will be ended. President Obama heralded the bill as one of the greatest deficit reduction plans in history, but remember that Speaker Pelosi is promising a 'doctor's fix' that will undercut any claim of deficit reduction. But the real question is whether this bill is going to improve or harm our health care system. It is now beyond dispute that more Americans than not firmly believe this legislation is going to make things worse. Simply put, this is the biggest con job that has ever been foisted on the American people. And remember - as Nancy Pelosi has said, this is just the beginning. This bill will become the platform for reaching the real goal President Obama has wanted all along - a single-payer system. Stay tuned."

Laura was joined by political strategist Dick Morris, who forecast that health care may be just the first shot in the Obama revolution. "If Obama passes this," Morris said, "I think he's going to use reconciliation to run the table. He'll pass amnesty for illegal immigrants, he'll pass cap-and-trade, he'll pass a public option, and he'll pass financial regulation. He's got his own little Constitution going here." Morris actually claimed that President Obama is willing to sacrifice other Democrats in order to achieve his grand vision. "He knows they're going to lose Congress, but President Obama is going for history and trying to create a permanent record. I think he's going to adopt the entire socialist program by essentially circumventing the Constitution." Morris concluded that his ominous forecast will be averted if the health care bill is defeated, which he called a distinct possibility.
Top Story II 
Has partisan health care debate doomed Dems?
With polls showing that just 18% of Americans approve of Congress's job performance, Laura asked two liberal Democrats whether their party will be routed in November. "Democrats want health care reform," said Democratic strategist Mary Anne Marsh, "while Republicans and independents don't. But independents care more about the deficit than anything else, so if Democrats can convince some independents that the health care bill will reduce the deficit, they can win in November." Radio talk show host Nancy Skinner pointed out that the GOP is also wildly unpopular. "People think Republicans in Congress are just trying to block Obama and that they're doing a lousy job. As soon as this bill passes, all the fear-mongering will be over, people will be happy and the polls will change." But Laura suggested that Democrats will pay a huge price for health care reform: "Support for Obama-care has slipped since January, and now 55% oppose the plan. Democrats have made a huge miscalculation."
Top Story III 
Questioning Gutierrez' about-face
Democratic Illinois Representative Luis Gutierrez has announced that he will vote "yea" on health care reform. Gutierrez entered the No Spin Zone and admitted that his vote is tied to immigration. "This administration has not put the kind of energy and focus to bring about comprehensive immigration reform," Gutierrez stated, "but now I see a President who is more focused and energetic and committed. He can not unilaterally solve the plight of 10-million undocumented workers in this country, but we are going in the right direction." Laura pressed Gutierrez to reveal exactly what he was promised in exchange for his vote on health care. "I got a commitment," Gutierrez replied, "that immigration reform will be pursued with vigor and energy. The White House is going to be engaged."
Personal Story Segment 
Medical community reacts explosively to health care
One unintended consequence of health care reform could be a mass exodus of physicians. Laura explored that possibility with FNC's Dr. Manny Alvarez. "I know thousands of doctors," Alvarez began, "and I can count on one hand those who agree with this health care bill. Doctors will leave the practice if this socialized medicine bill passes, which will destroy health care as we know it in America." But emergency physician Dr. Mark Morocco came down squarely on the side of increased government involvement. "100% of doctors have considered leaving medicine over the last fifteen years because of what's happening in this completely market-driven system we have now. What we have now is worse than socialized medicine and we have to get back to pure doctor-patient relationships." Laura added one piece of anecdotal evidence, saying that "out of the fifteen doctors that I know well, only one is for this bill."
Impact Segment 
States set to sue feds over health care
Some states and conservative groups are vowing to sue the federal government if health care reform becomes law, claiming the bill piles expenses on individuals and state governments. Fox Business Network analyst Elizabeth MacDonald explained that one major issue is the mandate that every citizen purchase health insurance. "The Congressional Budget Office," MacDonald reported, "said in 1994 that having an insurance mandate was an unprecedented expansion of the government's powers. In this country we never have been mandated to buy any good or service. More than that, there is nothing in the Constitution that says the government can fine you for not buying insurance. What are they going to do next, regulate couch potatoes?" But attorney Kelly Saindon argued that Congress is perfectly within its rights. "The government has the right to tax citizens for what it considers to be our best welfare. While I agree that this isn't the best plan out there, I don't think the states stand a chance."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Plans to close Gitmo inching forward
The White House is reportedly close to a deal that will close the prison at Guantanamo Bay in exchange for trying suspected terrorists in military courts. Former federal prosecutor John Flannery, who favors civilian trials for detainees, complained that President Obama is reneging on his campaign promises. "The President said it would be a 'false choice' if we have to choose between security and idealism," Flannery said, "and now we're making a kind of false choice. You should have criminal prosecutions, which have more reliable outcomes. And closing Guantanamo Bay has to be more than just symbolically moving it somewhere else. If you're going to say people have rights, then you should respect those rights."
Back of Book Segment 
How health care is dominating national discussion
Laura concluded with a montage of the sights and sounds of March Madness. No, not the NCAA college basketball tournament, but the protests, debates, arguments and bloviating over health care reform. There were activists claiming that Democrats want to "kill grandma" and complaining that government health care is being "shoved down our throats." And there was Nancy Pelosi vowing that seniors will get a better deal, House Majority Whip James Clyburn saying "we are absolutely giddy," and President Obama promising that "we are going to do something historic this weekend." Laura's sarcastic summary: "And as long as it's historic, that's the only thing that really matters. Right?"
