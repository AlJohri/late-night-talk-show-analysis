Talking Points Memo 
The prisoner swap fracas continues
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guest:Senator John McCain
"It is becoming clear that the Obama administration severely miscalculated reaction to trading a suspected deserter, Sergeant Bowe Bergdahl, for five Taliban war criminals...
"Sergeant Bergdahl definitely deserted his post - there is no question about that. And he may have even collaborated with the enemy. That has been investigated and there is a classified report on the situation...
"Public opinion has definitely turned against Sergeant Bergdahl and the swap in general. In a Rasmussen poll, 43% of Americans oppose the swap, 40% say it's okay, and 17% don't know. That number will change - more will come out against, wait and see...
"Once again the Obama administration has botched an important decision. Even the President admits the five released Taliban war criminals might kill again. And his administration has embarrassed itself by saying Sergeant Bergdahl served with honor. He did not...
"So this is just one more big mess for which Mr. Obama is responsible. Is his administration at the tipping point? I think so."
McCain, recognizing that these Taliban guys are the hardest of the hard core, found it stunning for the President to enter into a deal where he admits they may re-enter the fight.
The Factor countered: the President, who apparently believes you never leave a guy behind, is portraying this as a delicate deal that could have fallen apart if he didn't move quickly.
The Senator admitted he's grateful Bergdahl is back home, but stressed that a commander-in-chief has to make tough decisions. He said war is a very cruel business, but the first priority should always be national security over individuals
The Factor asked whether Sen. McCain has any empathy for Bergdahl, considering he himself was a POW during Vietnam and saw other prisoners, under duress, go so far as to collaborate with the North Vietnamese.
McCain replied that he has sympathy for human frailties, but if the allegations against Bergdahl are true and his actions caused the death of other service people, there needs to be a complete investigation.
The Factor urged the Senator to get answers, opining that Americans need to know what Bergdahl did and how bad these Taliban guys we traded for him are.
Washington Beat Segment 
Controversy Over the Prisoner Swap
Guest:Carl Cameron & James Rosen
Rosen shed some light on the various government investigations into Bergdahl. He reported the Army investigated him after he walked off his base, and then the intelligence community undertook a separate investigation into his final period of active duty, as well as conduct over the past five years. Finally, a new investigation has opened this past weekend upon his release. Rosen described many inside the intelligence community as harboring concerns Bergdahl became an active collaborator with the enemy. In 2012, the Daily Mail reported Sgt. Bergdahl taught the Taliban how to make mobile phones into IEDs.

Cameron focused on the controversy in Congress that the President may have broken the law by doing this swap without authorization. He told viewers that lawmakers are meeting tonight behind closed doors to review classified information. In his opinion, as this unfolds, we'll begin to hear from more Congresspeople and see whether this becomes partisan mudslinging or whether the ongoing concerns about the prisoner swap will be bipartisan in nature.
Impact Segment 
Can the Left Defend Obama over Prisoner Swap?
Guest:Howard Kurtz
The far-left spin from MSNBC is Sgt. Bergdahl is being "swiftboated," a term that describes what John Kerry's opponents did when he ran for president by implying his military service wasn't worthy.

Kurtz admitted a few MSNBC hosts are trying to deflect blame, but insisted that on the whole, if you look at the media landscape, the media is doing its job and digging up facts. The New York Times and CNN, in his estimation, have been aggressive in interviewing platoon members who are angry about Bergdahl abandoning his post.

The Factor pointed out the real die-hard zealots, who will never admit President Obama made a mistake, are having a hard time with this story.

Kurtz cautioned against making the story about Bergdahl's father, saying the focus should remain on Bowe Bergdahl and whether he caused the deaths of other soldiers who went looking for him.

The Factor concluded this story is going to get even more complicated for the Bergdahl family.
Watters' World Segment 
Marine Imprisoned in Mexico
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guest:Jesse Watters
Jesse, armed with a petition signed by almost 200,000 Americans asking the Mexican government to release U.S. Marine Andrew Tahmooressi, headed to the Mexican consulate in New York City. Tahmooressi has been held in a Mexican jail for two months after entering the country in a vehicle containing firearms, which were illegal in Mexico but legal in the U.S. The Factor's stance is that this was all an accident and the Marine should not be subjected to this ordeal.

A Mexican consulate press spokesperson agreed to receive the petition, but Jesse persisted and was able to hand it directly to the Deputy Consul General, who promised she'd get the petition to the appropriate people.

Jesse described the consulate workers as very professional. In updating the story, he said the Marine just fired his last attorney, and the new attorney is holding a big press conference in Mexico next week.

The Factor promised to stay on the story, but doesn't expect to see Tahmooressi released until mid-June at the earliest.
Miller Time Segment 
More on the Prisoner Swap
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guest:Dennis Miller


On the prisoner swap controversy, Miller expressed regret that those five Taliban guys were even alive to trade. The Factor said the far-right is mistakenly characterizing Obama as sympathetic to the Taliban, but he thinks it's just mistakes.

Miller surmised that perhaps Obama is just a dolt, but the Factor disagreed. He insisted that while the President may have deficits in the economy and foreign affairs, he's a very smart guy.

The Factor asked Miller to comment on the backlash about him saying Bergdahl's father looks like a Muslim. Miller joked that he thought he looked like ZZ Top, but guessed the old man was probably driven crazy about his son behind held by these monsters for five years.

Finally, a question from a viewer: What caused Miller's turn-around from liberal to conservative? Miller affirmed he was middle of the road - socially liberal, but he'd like to keep half his money and have the government protect the homeland.
Did You See That Segment 
Martha MacCallum
Guest:Martha MacCallum
NBC News foreign correspondent Richard Engel reported Bergdahl's father had been tweeting, calling for his son's release and for other hostages to be released. But last week there was a bizarre tweet, which was later deleted, saying he was working to free all Guantanamo prisoners.

Martha detailed the controversial statements made by the father: Several years ago, Rollin Stone published emails from Bowe Bergdahl to his father, where the sergeant says he was disenchanted with the military and ashamed to be an American, to which his father replied "obey your conscience."

The Factor confirmed that Rolling Stone, a liberal icon, is the source of all this information. He questioned whether the father, who took on an activist role after his son went missing, ever seemed sympathetic to the captors.

Martha stated that the father figured out early on that his son's captors wanted Gitmo guys released, so he began tweeting about this. She marveled that the White House doesn't seem to know what's going on in the social media world.
