Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Will an unresolved debt crisis hurt you?
"Earlier this week we interviewed Congresswoman Michele Bachmann, who says she will not vote to raise the debt ceiling, no matter what. Talking Points respectfully disagrees with Ms. Bachmann because if a debt deal is not reached Americans will suffer greatly. I know some of you don't believe that, but Federal Reserve Chairman Ben Bernanke has warned that the nation faces a huge financial calamity if the debt ceiling is not increased. Americans should put ideology aside and urge the politicians to get a compromise done, and here's what it should be: There should be at least $2 trillion in immediate spending cuts; there should be no increase in income taxes, but the government should close many tax loopholes; and both parties should agree to revise the entire tax code. President Obama and the Democrats must stop the spending madness immediately. On the other side, Tea Party people can't continue to say hey, we don't care what happens to the economy, we're not going to raise the debt limit. Yesterday White House spokesman Jay Carney said I am a voice of moderation in the debate. So now I am offering to broker the debt compromise - I'll go down there, I'm ready to answer the call because I am looking out for you, not some crazed ideology or political party."

The Factor was joined by Fox News sage Charles Krauthammer, who promptly tossed cold water on the Talking Points Memo. "If you think you are going to get $2 trillion in real cuts from this administration," Krauthammer said, "you really are somewhere out there. We had a resolution of $38 billion in cuts during the issue of the government shutdown, and what did it turn out to be? One-third-of-a-billion dollars! If you want to see real numbers, you're going to have to ask them to spell it out openly and not to leak all this stuff which has no reality. But you're right that we can not allow a debt default. The Republicans should say they'll go along with the President in closing all the loopholes in return for a lowering of tax rates." The Factor argued that the time is right for a serious deal to reduce the deficit: "Critical mass has been reached. I submit to you that it can happen and it should happen."

For more on the debt showdown, The Factor welcomed Democratic Congressman Rob Andrews. "Your point is a refreshing voice of reason," Andrews began, "and I would get involved in doing a deal like you laid out. I'd try to do $4 trillion, but certainly $2.5 trillion is good, I think that's very doable. If the country defaults on its debt, there will be economic disaster. And if we don't get the deficit down, there will be economic disaster." The Factor reiterated that substantial cuts are necessary and feasible: "It shouldn't be difficult for this great country of ours to cut 10% out of the spending. It's a philosophical thing, and I want the Democrats to buy into the fact that we have gone too far in the spending realm."
Personal Story Segment 
Ugly showdown between White House & Fox News
Newly released emails show that White House functionaries tried to marginalize Fox News early in the Obama administration. Bernie Goldberg opined on the relationship between FNC and the administration. "I'm willing to accept that this is history," Goldberg said, "but still, the emails that were released tell us something important. They tell us that the politicians in the White House are able to look you right in the eye and lie to you. This was supposed to be different - Barack Obama told us that this would not be politics as usual if he was elected. Well, it is politics as usual. Somebody needs to tell Barack Obama that he's not the Mayor of Chicago and 1600 Pennsylvania isn't City Hall. You don't go to war with a news operation." The Factor contended that the administration's battle with FNC backfired: "That war with Fox News was the worst thing they could possibly do because we're on the air 24/7. If you're going to give us a hard time, we're going to spotlight that and you're going to look bad."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Who's paying Casey Anthony's legal bills?
Casey Anthony's attorneys say they'll put her on a plane for parts unknown when she is released from prison. The Factor posed the question, "Who's paying for all this?," and crime reporter Aphrodite Jones tried to answer. "I would imagine," Jones surmised, "that her attorney Jose Baez has made a deal with someone to provide a plane. He is the only person she's had a relationship with in the past three years and she won't even see her parents now. Baez and his team have been working pro bono." Jones added that Anthony's trial cost Floridians millions of dollars. "Florida taxpayers are paying her bills in terms of court costs and protection while she's in jail. And if there are any viable threats, we'll also have to pay for those to be followed up."
Culture Warriors Segment 
Is morbid obesity a type of child abuse?
Two Harvard researchers are urging that extremely obese children be taken away from their parents and put in foster care. Culture Warriors Alicia Menendez and Gretchen Carlson weighed in, so to speak. "I don't think the government should get involved in this," Carlson said. "The government might take away your kids if they are malnourished, but this is so much more complex than just stopping a kid from starving. This is a complete lifestyle change and the government is not good at doing this - the foster care system is already overloaded." Menendez added another dilemma to the conundrum. "How do you know the difference between what is genetics and what is legitimately neglect and abuse? If you can answer that, then this is not entirely unreasonable." The Factor seemed to agree that morbid obesity can be a form of abuse, at least in extreme cases: "If a kid is 200 pounds overweight and a doctor says the parents won't feed the kid properly, you're putting the kid in jeopardy."
Back of Book Segment 
Great American News Quiz: Villain edition
Perennial good guys Martha MacCallum and Steve Doocy fielded questions about some genuine bad guys in the Great American News Quiz. Among the questions: "The 1932 film 'Scarface' was based on which real-life gangster?" ... "What actor portrayed the evil doctor Josef Mengele in 'The Boys from Brazil?'" ... "General Idi Amin was the dictator of which African country?" Doocy's impressive knowledge of villains earned him a one-point victory in a match that somehow managed to include footage of Herman's Hermits.

Pinheads and Patriots 
O'Hare TSA agents
Thursday's Pinheads: The TSA officers who patted down former Secretary of Defense Donald Rumsfeld at O'Hare Airport in Chicago. The Factor suggested that "there should be an 'easy pass' system for Americans who can prove they've been solid citizens for a number of years. Patting down 2-year-olds and Mr. Rumsfeld is dumb and pinheaded."
