Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Giving up Khalid Sheikh Mohammed
"Both Fox News and the Washington Post are reporting that the Obama administration is seriously considering returning 9/11 mastermind Khalid Sheikh Mohammed to the military. Attorney General Eric Holder wants to try Mohammed in civilian court in New York City, but it looks like President Obama may overrule Holder. The Factor believes there is some kind of deal-making going on behind the scenes. The Obama people want Republicans to cooperate with closing the prison at Guantanamo Bay; the deal could be that the President will let the military try the terrorists being held there if the GOP helps on Gitmo. Talking Points believes Gitmo is a useful tool in fighting terrorism, but prisons are prisons so these thugs could be held in military facilities anywhere. Also, we do not believe Guantanamo Bay causes people to sign up for the jihad. Are you telling me once Gitmo is closed Al Qaeda's membership will dry up? Give me a break! Politically speaking, we can look at this two ways. On the positive side, it shows President Obama could reverse a bad policy; on the negative side, Holder's civilian trial vision was so misguided it's almost frightening."

The Factor was joined by Fox News correspondent Major Garrett, who laid bare the secret deal-making surrounding the KSM case. "The administration can't get the money from Congress," Garrett reported, "to move detainees from Guantanamo Bay to the maximum security facility in Illinois. How do you get the money? You erase a huge political problem, which is the decision by Holder to have the KSM trial in New York City. This decision on Khalid Sheikh Mohammed will not be dealt with in isolation." FNC analyst Alan Colmes expressed great frustration with the administration's shifting position. "I'm very disappointed because of the Constitution. This is not going to be the death knell of his presidency, but if these kinds of things continue it will hurt him on the left flank. I also have a problem with the additional 30,000 troops to Afghanistan, which was another flip-flop. He keeps moving to the right."

FNC anchor Geraldo Rivera entered the No Spin Zone and stated his objection to the change of venue. "Moving the trial out of New York," Rivera said, "is a product of hysterical overstatement and hype over the danger involved. We are making Al Qaeda into supermen, but they can't even light their underwear on fire. I would love to see the trial within the shadows of the towers that these SOBs brought down." Rivera also reported on John Gardner, charged with killing 17-year-old Chelsea King near San Diego after serving five years for a prior sexual assault. "The court-appointed psychiatrist said he showed absolutely no remorse, he was a very poor potential participant in rehabilitation. And as soon as his electronic bracelet came off, he began a series of rapes and murders, of which Chelsea King was the last one." The Factor urged authorities everywhere to err on the side of public safety when dealing with sex offenders: "The lesson here is that everybody has to get tough with these guys."
Impact Segment 
Hollywood, politics and ideology
An Internet comedy skit promoting financial reform features Saturday Night Live alums Chevy Chase, Dan Aykroyd, Dana Carvey, Darrell Hammond and others. The Factor praised the sketch as "a good example of Hollywood creativity," but then showed footage of actor Tom Hanks mocking Fox News during an appearance on MSNBC. The Factor issued this challenge: "If Mr. Hanks has a political beef with Fox News or conservatives or whomever, he should state it. Be a man, tell us what you think! But over the years Mr. Hanks has become an ideological sniper, and I still don't understand why he's like that. What's his beef, what's he talking about? Nobody knows. That doesn't diminish his talent, but it does make him kind of a mindless partisan. State your case, Tom. Don't be a pinhead."
Personal Story Segment 
Previewing Barbara Walters' final Oscars special
After 29 years of Oscar night specials on ABC, Barbara Walters has announced that this year's edition will be her last. The Factor asked Walters why she is giving up the very popular and lucrative program. "We have very few stars now," Walters explained, "and we are not in awe of them any more. We have animated features, we have 'Avatar,' and we have independent films. There are not as many stars and they're all over the place on different programs. I felt my 'special' isn't so special." The Factor agreed that Hollywood is suffering from a severe glamour deficit: "There used to be a mystery and an aura surrounding Audrey Hepburn and other stars. There's no mystique any more - gossip columns tell you everything about them. And these people are vapid, they have nothing to say."
At Your Beck and Call Segment 
Glenn Beck on Jeremiah Wright
The Factor invited Glenn Beck to opine on Jeremiah Wright, who is presenting "living legend' awards to himself, Louis Farrakhan, and radical priest Michael Pfleger. "I would like to present this silver pen," Beck jested, "to the greatest broadcaster in human history ... me! Bill, what are you giving yourself today?" The Factor denounced the Chicago media for ignoring the Wright event: "The media are afraid because if they say something negative about Farrakhan or Wright, they'll have the wing nuts marching on their buildings. The far left and Farrakhan's Nation of Islam have managed to intimidate the media." Beck then compared Farrakhan and Wright with former White House "green jobs czar" Van Jones. "I don't think there's a lot of difference between them," he suggested. "The difference is that Van Jones has been homogenized and watches his language. Jones used to say all of the same things as these guys and I think he believes all of the same things."
Back of Book Segment 
Dumbest things of the week
As is their Friday evening custom, FNC's Greg Gutfeld and Juliet Huddy looked back at the week's stupidest people and events. Huddy selected Australian authorities who banned a sexually suggestive ad with Pam Anderson. "It's for an Internet domain service," Huddy reported, "and we're here in the United States talking about it, so it was brilliant. It was a little sexual but it was a celebration of women." Gutfeld chose the "Jeopardy" contestant who thought George Carlin was a centenarian and that Al Sharpton was a 100-year-old Senator. "It's everybody's fantasy to be on Jeopardy," Gutfeld said, "and now this guy has to live with this for the rest of his life. Maybe he is so smart he's stupid." The Factor singled out portly portside filmmaker Michael Moore, who called his fellow Democrats a "bunch of wusses."
Pinheads and Patriots 
Factoring the Oscars
The Factor preempted P&P to present his annual Oscar predictions: Supporting Actor, Christoph Waltz; Supporting Actress, Mo'nique; Actress, Sandra Bullock; Actor, Jeff Bridges; Picture, Avatar; Director, Kathryn Bigelow. It will be apparent by late Sunday night whether those selections merit designation as a Patriot or a Pinhead.
