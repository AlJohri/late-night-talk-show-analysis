Talking Points Memo 
A wake-up call for Republicans
"As Barack Obama begins to put his team in place, we see the outlines of the battle ahead for the Republican minority. With Rahm Emanuel as his chief-of-staff, Obama has given us a glance of what a 'post-partisan' government is going to look like. Emanuel is shrewd, fierce, and a savvy partisan enforcer. Republicans better be ready for what's about to come. Bipartisanship sounds nice as a campaign slogan, but Republicans would be fools to believe they could make a steady climb out of the hole they're in by working with those who seek to 're-make' America. The only way Republicans will be able to defeat the coming liberal storm will be to learn from the last two election disasters. Democrats beat Republicans in cultivating charismatic candidates, in the ad wars, in fundraising, in grass-roots organizing, in use of the Internet, and in staff unity. Republicans need a total housecleaning, and that means the leadership on Capitol Hill. This is supposed to be the party of merit and accountability, yet the same people who failed to deliver in the last two elections keep sticking around. Wanted in the GOP: New faces, new energy, new ideas, and new communication skills."

Top Story 
Pres. Elect Obama holds press conference today
Barack Obama met the media Thursday, answering questions that focused on the economy. Laura spoke about Obama's message with FNC analyst Juan Williams. "It sounds to me," Williams said, "as if he's willing to put a lot of money into a number of places. It sounds like he's supportive of giving big money to the auto industry and to the states, but where is all this money going to come from? This is totally out of control." Republican strategist Barbara Comstock worried that Obama may be beholden to his left flank. "Unions have spent money to elect Obama and now they expect payback, like taking the secret ballot away from the workplace so they can increase unionization, which is exactly what we don't need."

News Link: Obama holds first press conference
Impact Segment 
Obama's pledge to fix unemployment
With unemployment at a 14-year high and other economic indicators looking bleak, Laura asked Fox Business Network anchor Stuart Varney about Barack Obama's economic plan. "There is not a feeling," Varney lamented, "that he has really come to grips with the severity of this situation that has gotten worse in the past few days. The plan he offered was vague and hardly dynamic. 600,000 people have lost their jobs in the last three months, Detroit is on the brink of bankruptcy, and we need a sense of urgency from the president." Varney added that Obama can reassure markets if he names Larry Summers or Paul Volcker to be Secretary of the Treasury.
Personal Story Segment 
Sarah Palin fights the smears
Anonymous McCain aides trashed Sarah Palin this week, claiming she lacked basic knowledge of civics and geography. Palin supporter Marjorie Dannenfelser denounced the accusers as cowards. "These guys were supposed to be on her team," Dannenfelser said, "but they were scribbling notes in the corner so they could later destroy her. She's a threat because she's a populist conservative, attractive, and an authentic human being who lives her principles. She has a 90% approval rating among Republicans and she is the future." Laura urged Senator McCain to step in and stop the back-stabbing. "This has gotten beyond ugly and I don't believe any of this stuff. John McCain should demand that people who are saying this come forward, and he should stand with his former running mate."

News Link: Palin strikes back at critics
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Rev. Wright speaks about Obama win
Just two days after the election, Reverend Jeremiah Wright resurfaced and blamed the media for taking his words out of context. Laura welcomed FNC media analyst Jane Hall, who ridiculed Wright's suggestion. "He obviously feels he was used," Hall said, "but I don't see how he can say he was used against Barack Obama. He is responsible for what he said. It would be good to ask him what it says about America that Obama won." Communications professor Tobe Berkovitz added Wright has altered his entire demeanor. "Notice the difference in his tone - he's very soft spoken, and compare that to the clips the church sold. He likes to be in the media, so now he feels it's time for him to rehabilitate his image." Laura agreed that Wright craves the limelight. "We kept wondering where Reverend Wright was, he was obviously just waiting to speak. I wish someone would ask him whether he still believes his Afro-centric approach to preaching is what we need in a 'post-racial' United States."

Unresolved Problems Segment 
Rebuilding the GOP
Laura was joined by Wisconsin Congressman Paul Ryan, a rising GOP star, who gave a candid assessment of his party. "We've done some good things on social conservatism," Ryan said, "but we fell down on fiscal conservatism and economic liberty. We have to go back to our basics and become that reform party again. We should go forward with big ideas and bold principles that we apply to the problems of the day." Laura denounced GOP leadership as out of touch and inept. "The Republican Party has to be synonymous with competence and excellence, but if these people were in corporate America with their track record, they would have been shown the door long ago."
Factor Investigation Segment 
Teacher under investigation for political comments
North Carolina elementary school teacher Diatha Harris was caught on tape disparaging John McCain and praising Barack Obama. Laura asked Professor Marc Lamont Hill about the teacher and her attempts at indoctrination. "This was troublesome," Hill said. "A classroom should be a space for learning and a place to challenge a student's perspective. But that can not be done in a way that students feel indoctrinated and intimidated." Laura complained that too many teachers and textbooks put forth a bleak vision of America and its history. "On occasion we should talk about American exceptionalism and we've done for the rest of the world. Kids don't get enough about the greatness of America."

Back of Book Segment 
Poking fun at the new president
Bill Clinton and George W. Bush are regularly skewered by comedians, but will Barack Obama get the same treatment? TV host Toure told Laura why Obama is a tough comedic target. "It's hard to make fun of really cool people," Toure said, "and he is really cool. So how do we pierce the armor and make fun of him? We just have to figure out how to caricature him, and even Saturday Night Live is struggling. The guy actually is cool." Laura countered that "when Barack Obama is off teleprompter he can say things like '57 states' and the Nancy Reagan 'sance' comment."
