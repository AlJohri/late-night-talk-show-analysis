Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
The real problem in America
"Many Factor viewers have emotion invested in the repeal of the federal health care takeover. It symbolizes to them all that is wrong in America -- an intrusive federal government, a curtailing of individual freedom, and an assault on the free marketplace. But it is not the most important issue facing Americans right now. Massive government spending is. And, of course, Obamacare is part of that. According to data assembled by CBS News, over the past four years since Democrats have control of Congress, the amount of money that the USA owes has increased by an astounding $5.2 trillion. That is simply unsustainable. Right now, every American citizen owes about $45,000 each. President Bush could have vetoed the big spending bills. He did not. And his successor, President Obama, has embraced spending at record levels. This must stop. And liberal Americans must also stop distorting the situation. Nancy Pelosi said today that 'Deficit reduction has been a high priority for us. It is our mantra -- pay as you go.' That is simply not true. It is an outrageous spin. I'm not going to call the woman a liar. But what she said is flat out not true. If the United States continues to run up debt, our economy will collapse and there's not going to be any bailout for us. It is long past time to stop the social justice nonsense and speak clearly to the folks. The federal government cannot afford to provide cradle to grave entitlements. It cannot afford to run around the world spending billions of dollars in places like Iraq and Afghanistan. It cannot afford the far-left vision of income redistribution using punitive taxation on business and affluent. It can no longer afford to allow foreign citizens live here without proper documentation. All of that must stop right now. Nancy Pelosi, Harry Reid, President Obama, and other powerful liberal politicians should get this simple message. We tried it your way. It didn't work. We are now heading for disaster."

For reaction, The Factor brought in Fox News analysts Monica Crowley and Alan Colmes. Crowley was fired up over what Nancy Pelosi said, but thought it was par for the course for the outgoing Speaker: "She has said many delusional and outright false things." Crowley also tossed out a shocking statistic: "This Democratic Congress has added to the national debt $4.2 billion a day -- that's on average under Pelosi's leadership." The Factor was amazed: "These numbers are so staggering that we can't even explain them to you because there isn't enough money in the world." Colmes, meanwhile, was unimpressed with the GOP attempt to repeal ObamaCare: "They're not going to overturn it. They're doing it, as I see it, as a stunt because they know they don't have the votes to do it." The Factor disagreed, saying that the repeal vote was symbolically important for the Republicans: "I don't think it's a waste of time. But I do think that the emphasis should be on the wild spending."
Impact Segment 
Atheists continue to insult religion with billboards
An Atheist group has put up a billboard in Alabama that says all religions are "scams." The president of American Atheists, David Silverman, entered the No Spin Zone to defend his group's actions. "They're not insults. They are truths," Silverman said. "Everybody knows religion is a scam." The Factor cut him off: "Everybody knows? I don't know, Mr. Silverman." Silverman thought that The Factor should be more incredulous about religion: "You sit here and you're skeptical every day on your show -- and then you go to church and you get on your knees and pray to an invisible man in the sky? And you don't think that's a scam?" The Factor thought that Silverman's confrontational style was not winning any converts to his side: "What good does it do you to insult 80 percent of the American people by saying they are too stupid to know what they're doing?"
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Battle over 'illegal aliens' term brews
The Society of Professional Journalism recently said that the term "illegal immigrant" or "illegal alien" is offensive to Hispanics. To explain the rationalie, The Factor invited Leo E. Laurence, who suggested the policy, and Hagit Limor, president of the SPJ. Laurence said the rationale for the policy had a legal basis: "This is not political correctness... Everyone is presumed to be innocent until proven guilty in the court of law. So, it's not a journalist that can say someone is illegal. It's a judge." The Factor found a hole in that argument right away: "Therefore, I couldn't call the al Qaeda people terrorists unless they are convicted of terrorist acts. So, I can't really say Osama bin Laden, he's a terrorist because he hasn't been convicted of a a terrorist act in a court of law, correct?" Meanwhile, Limor denied that the SPJ had settled on an outright ban of the phrase: "What we decided to do was to launch a year-long effort to educate reporters on how to treat and report on all manner of under-represented populations, that includes the disabled or minority groups. We did not in any way or fashion say that the term illegal immigrant or illegal alien was off the books."
Stossel Matters Segment 
John Stossel on New Year's resolutions
Fox Business host John Stossel has a special this week about New Year's resolutions -- from a libertarian standpoint. Stossel explained the significance: "A New Year's resolution is the free choice of an individual... And when the United States government says you can't do something, that takes everyone's freedom away." Stossel had an analogy for what the government was trying to do: "It's like I make this resolution that, Bill, you should shut up and let your guests talk more." "You're imposing it on me?" The Factor asked. "Well, that's what government does," Stossel explained. "But you wouldn't do that," The Factor pointed out. "I wouldn't dare," Stossel said. The Factor saw a backlash against the government brewing: "The tide has turned now. Folks are mad." Stossel agreed: "That's true. I hope we'll see a whole new world with people resolving to hold these guys' feet to the fire."
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Arizona 'honor killing' trial to begin shortly
Legal eagles Lis Wiehl and Kimberly Guilfoyle came in with a sad story. Guilfoyle set the scene: "He came here from Iraq with his daughter. His daughter refused a marriage that he wanted her to engage in." She went on to explain that the father eventually ran over her with a car, killing her. Wiehl brought up the man's novel defense : "He's saying this is honor killing." The Factor was shocked: "He's hiding behind his religion, saying it gives him the right to murder his daughter?" "Right," Wiehl said. "But he doesn't understand the criminal justice system." "He feels we're violating his freedom of religion, his rights, by not allowing him to live his life and conduct his life with his family," Guilfoyle said. The Factor predicted an open and shut case: "So we think this trial ought to take about two days."
Back of Book Segment 
Will new GOP majority repeal Obama-care?
Charles Krauthammer had mixed feelings about the GOP's plan to kill ObamaCare. He explained his thoughts to The Factor. First, Krauthammer said he actually liked the plan for the House to vote for repeal: "That's a good idea... It will carry all the way through till the presidential election. It will be a millstone around the necks of the Democrats." But what Krauthammer didn't like was the plan to cut off funding for certain parts of it. He pointed out that the GOP could never shut off all the money: "The Republicans will end up taking away the money for a commission here, a provision here, adding to the chaos and the incoherence of it. And when Obama care, which is intrinsically chaotic, incoherent, inefficient, and extremely expensive, collapses, the Republicans will take the blame." The Factor saw a trap for the GOP in trying to repeal the law: "There's some good stuff in that bill. And that's what the Democrats are going to grab onto. 'Ooh! They want the greedy insurance companies to be able to throw you off the rolls after you get sick.'"
