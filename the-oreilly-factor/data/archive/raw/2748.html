Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
President Obama and your money
"Republicans are squeezing President Obama because they know he can not get a tax hike on the affluent passed at this time. Mr. Obama will have to compromise or give up entirely and extend the 2003 Bush tax cuts. Mr. Obama is caught because if he gives up, the far left will go crazy, and they are the people the President needs to fund his campaign in 2012. Radical left wing Americans want, above all, to take income from the affluent and give it to those who have less. Talking Points believes President Obama is basically with the far left on this - he does believe in income redistribution. But he can not do that now because the Senate will block a tax hike on the affluent. Taking ideology out of the equation, it's risky to raise taxes on anybody with the economy as weak as it is - 50% of any tax hike on the affluent would be paid by small business and that will slow hiring. But remember that some of the far left want to harm the economy because the worse it gets, the more people will look to big government to save them. In the end, that's what MoveOn and the rest of these loons are really striving for."

Radio talk show host Laura Ingraham entered the No Spin Zone with her analysis of the tax battle. "I just got off the phone with the leadership's office in the House," Ingraham reported, "and the scuttlebutt on Capitol Hill is that the left is in a real state of frenzy. There's a lot of infighting and there's an enormous amount of pressure on the President to take this one to the mat and raise taxes on 'Subchapter S' corporations and Americans who make more than $250,000." Ingraham added that conservatives in Congress feel they have been given a mandate. "Republicans say they have 'the wind at our backs and the people with us.' They feel very confident and there's no reason for them to compromise." The Factor warned that Republicans should not overplay their hand: "There's not a lot of sympathy in this country for rich people like me and you. The perception is that fat cats are making a ton of money and living large while most Americans are struggling."
Impact Segment 
Government spends $3M+ on inaccurate ad
84-year-old Andy Griffith, TV's beloved Sheriff of Mayberry, appeared in commercials extolling the virtues of health care reform for seniors. But according to FactCheck.org, parts of the ad were simply not true. The Factor pursued the issue with FNC's Ellis Henican and Leslie Marshall. "It's fine for the government to do this," Henican said, "because there were fear-based lies about reform and the folks who run Medicare have some responsibility to inform their constituents about the truth. Yes, the language of the ad was a little weasely, but it's technically correct." Marshall argued that government-sponsored advocacy ads are nothing new. "How come nobody was outraged when Bush was spending $22-million on an ad about Medicare? California Democrats stated that President Bush was misinforming and misleading the American people." The Factor concluded that government ads have to be absolutely truthful: "I don't mind a public service announcement that is fact-based, but I want the record to show that both Leslie and Ellis are not offended that their taxpayer money was spent to mislead them."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
FBI terror sting remains controversial
19-year-old Mohamed Osman Mohamud was arrested in Portland for trying to set off a bomb that could have killed hundreds at a Christmas tree lighting ceremony. The Factor asked two Muslim Americans whether the FBI's sting operation was appropriate. "I can't tell you how much I appreciate what the FBI did," said Zuhdi Jasser of the American Islamic Forum for Democracy. "Muslims who claim entrapment are trying to deflect responsibility - they don't want to look at the fact that his kid had a radicalization pattern for years. The FBI did not put those ideas in his head." Muhammad Ali Hasan of Muslims for America insisted that most Muslims are actively opposed to radical jihadists. "This man was turned in by his own father, who is a fellow American Muslim. Muslim groups are providing resources, recruiting Muslims to join Homeland Security, and encouraging people to call the FBI." But The Factor complained that more Muslims should be heard: "There has been a silence from the American Muslim community - most Americans want you to condemn jihad louder."
Culture Warriors Segment 
Couple asks Internet: Should we abort?
A married couple, Pete and Alisha Arnold, invited Americans to vote on whether they should abort their fetus. Culture Warriors Margaret Hoover and Gretchen Carlson were uniformly disgusted by the couple's actions. "The decision to have an abortion," Hoover said, "is a deeply personal decision between a woman, her family, her doctor and her God, but not her government and not the public at large. This should not be open to a vote." Carlson lamented the intrusion of modern technology. "This is what is wrong with the Internet. You're going to go to two-million voters about the most intimate and personal decision about whether you're going to keep a pregnancy? What kind of world do we live in?" The Factor reported that "77% of those who voted on this crazy thing said the woman should have the abortion, but I don't expect them to do anything. The fact that they put this spectacle on is revolting."
Kelly File Segment 
Why isn't Charles Rangel being punished?
Democratic Congressman Charles Rangel, who dodged taxes and engaged in shady deals, has been "censured" by his House colleagues, but faces no criminal charges. The Factor asked FNC anchor Megyn Kelly why Rangel is seemingly getting off easy. "Rangel got 'outed' by the New York Post," Kelly reported, "and as soon as he was outed, he contacted the IRS and cut a check. I'm not excusing it, but the bottom line is that Rangel owned up to what he did and paid the back taxes. What incentive does the IRS have to criminally prosecute those who own up to what they did?" The Factor accused the IRS of sending precisely the wrong message: "What the IRS is telling me, backed up by Megyn Kelly, is that I can try to get away with it and if I'm caught I just pay up. C'mon! That is offensive to those of us who do pay our taxes."
Back of Book Segment 
Great American News Quiz
Steve Doocy and Martha MacCallum resumed their rivalry in the Great American News Quiz. Among the questions: "According to the WikiLeaks documents, which world leader is followed around at all times by a voluptuous blonde nurse?" ... "On which college campus will the Bush Presidential Library be located?" As is their wont, Doocy and MacCallum went toe-to-toe the entire match, were nearly perfect, and finished in a most unsatisfying tie.
