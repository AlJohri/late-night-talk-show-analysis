Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Anti-Obama protesters visit Washington
"In Washington on Saturday, about 75,000 protesters gathered, loudly criticizing the policies of President Obama. Organizers say crowd was mostly 'fiscal conservatives' who object to Mr. Obama's expansion of the federal government. As always in a crowd of that size, there were different degrees of protest. More militant folks compared the president to Hitler, the Mafia, to Muslim Marxists. The very liberal NBC News locked in on the far right, and with the media branding the protesters as kooks and racists, what are we to think? The latest Rasmussen poll says 51% of Americans now have a favorable view of Obama-care, while 46% oppose. About half the country isn't crazy, as NBC News would have you believe. The fact of the matter is that President Obama believes a huge federal government can bring relief and prosperity to the American people. But many Americans do not believe that; they think the government will harm them and screw things up so badly that everyone will suffer. And so the battle continues and The Factor is right in the middle of it, bringing you the facts."

Fox News analyst Brit Hume entered the No Spin Zone with his take on the anti-Obama protesters. "There are going to be some nuts and extremists in every crowd," Hume began, "but for the most part these were everyday Americans who are very angry and upset with the Obama agenda. A lot of them weren't for him in the first place, but some of them were. They're frightened about the economy, they're frightened about the remedies that have been applied to the economy, particularly the $787 billion stimulus, and they're alarmed about big government." The Factor suggested that hostile media coverage of the protests is backfiring. "The stridency of the liberal media, which is about 80% of it now, has galvanized the protesters by attacking them when the tea parties took place. The media is driving the opposition against Barack Obama."
Weekdays with Bernie Segment 
Evaluating media coverage of Obama protests
The Factor asked FNC media analyst Bernie Goldberg to evaluate media coverage of Saturday's taxpayer protest. "When reporters cover liberal rallies," Goldberg said, "they downplay or ignore the most extreme, crazy views. But if it's a conservative rally, NBC News apparently figures it has to play the crazies up. The New York Times ran a basically fair story, but they played it on page 37. If this was an abortion rights rally or an anti-war rally, the story would have been closer to page 1. But a lot of people at that rally don't care any more if the media is fair - they know that the mainstream media doesn't respect them, so they don't respect the media." The Factor denounced pundits who ascribe "racism" to anti-Obama protesters, but also pointed to the march's one-sided racial composition: "There were very few African Americans at the rally and it's hard to say how many Hispanics. If that continues, the Obama presidency is going to come down to race, even though the president doesn't want it to."

Returning for a second segment, Bernie Goldberg focused on a new poll showing that trust in the media's fairness and accuracy has reached an all time low. "We keep referring to the New York Times and these other organizations as the 'mainstream' media," Goldberg complained. "I want to start something right now - we should never use that term again. There is very little mainstream about these people. People have had it with the so-called 'mainstream media,' and we shouldn't contribute to that. Fox is more 'mainstream' than the New York Times." The Factor theorized why so many liberal news organizations are losing money: "If you have a product that you don't trust, why would you pay $1.75 to buy a newspaper, and why would you turn on a TV news program that you know will ignore stories like ACORN and Van Jones."
Impact Segment 
Fallout continues from Rep. Wilson's speech outburst
Writing in the New York Times, columnist Maureen Dowd implied that Congressman Joe Wilson, who shouted "you lie" at President Obama, is a racist. The Factor asked FNC analysts Mary Katharine Ham and Juan Williams about that incendiary accusation. "Wilson was a member of the Sons of Confederate Veterans," Williams said, "and fought to have the Confederate flag fly over the South Carolina state house. So there's a circumstantial case based on his history, and he did something that was disdainful of the president." But Ham argued that Dowd displayed her own biases. "It didn't matter who Joe Wilson was, he has three characteristics that Maureen Dowd was looking for - he is white, he is Republican, and he is from the South. This is a cudgel that the left wants to use against the right, and it's troublesome to say that people who go out and protest against the president or have to be racist."
Personal Story Segment 
Confusion surrounding the 'public option'
Immediately after President Obama's Wednesday night address to Congress, The Factor declared the public option "gone," but Karl Rove vigorously disagreed. There is now a growing consensus that the public option is indeed off the table, but Rove returned and reiterated his opinion. "I hope you're right," Rove said, "but Nancy Pelosi, Harry Reid, and others are saying it's very much alive. It's a question of math - there is a huge Democratic majority in the House and they could get it done. And in the Senate they're looking for a way to get a 'soft' public option." The Factor insisted that a "big federal insurance apparatus isn't going to happen." Rove also speculated that President Obama wants to dramatically transform this nation. "Barack Obama has a very left-wing view of what public policy ought to be. He is someone who ran as a centrist but has governed from the left. He has associations with people on the far left of American politics that lead him very much out of the mainstream of America."
Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Serena Williams penalized for outburst
Tennis star Serena Williams lost her cool at the U.S. Open, screaming at an official who made a questionable call. The Factor's Reality Check: "Williams was penalized a point, which cost her the match, and fined $10,500. Ms. Williams did issue an apology." Singer Whitney Houston confessed to her prior drug use on Oprah Winfrey's show Monday. The Factor's Check: "America is a country that generally believes in redemption, so we wish Ms. Houston the best." Left-wing comic Bill Maher has denounced President Obama for accepting Van Jones' resignation and for not standing up to "crazy morons." The Factor's Check: "By referring to America as 'cracker nation,' Mr. Maher loses whatever debate he wants to have."

Kanye West & video parodists
Monday's Pinhead: Rapper Kanye West, who shamelessly interrupted Taylor Swift's acceptance speech at the Video Music Awards. And the Patriots: The folks who immediately turned Kanye West's outburst into a YouTube parody.
