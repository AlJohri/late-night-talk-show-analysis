Talking Points Memo 
Another Hollywood bomb on Iraq
"The film 'Stop-Loss' is a flop, earning less than $5 million in its opening weekend. So once again a negative movie about Iraq bombs, pardon the pun. The worst failure was Mark Cuban's 'Redacted,' which has grossed an amazing $65,000. That vile movie cost millions to make, so the billionaire Cuban got what he deserved. Why are Americans turning away from this stuff? Talking Points believes most Americans don't feel the USA is evil for removing Saddam and trying to bring democracy to the Middle East. So when Hollywood opens up its artillery on America and puts forth that we are not a noble nation, the folks walk away. We do make mistakes and some of us are not noble people, but generally the USA still stands for liberty and justice. We are willing to sacrifice for those goals, and all the Hollywood propaganda in the world will not change that."

Top Story 
Top Dems think Hillary's campaign is kaput
The Factor welcomed political analyst Dick Morris, who in effect pronounced Barack Obama the Democratic nominee. "It's over," Morris asserted. "There are nine more primaries and Hillary and Obama will more or less break even, but it doesn't matter. He enters these primaries with a lead of 161 elected delegates. The big leaders in the party will probably get together and say it's time for the superdelegates to make commitments, and will urge them to vote the way their states voted. And they'll say to Hillary it's time to withdraw. By July 1st this will be completely over." Morris also predicted that Obama's former pastor Jeremiah Wright will be a millstone around his neck. "In the general election that's going to be a very tough thing for him to carry around. It'll come across as a weakness and a lack of decisive leadership."
Impact Segment 
Government bailouts for homeowners
Hillary Clinton and Barack Obama favor bailing out homeowners who are facing foreclosure because of the mortgage crisis. But former Bush advisor Karl Rove questioned the political wisdom of a federal bailout. "I'm not certain the 75 million Americans who have a mortgage or have paid one off want to be called upon to subsidize the choices of those who are in foreclosure. We ought to be encouraging ways to have the lenders and borrowers sit down and work out a sustainable long-term solution." The Factor argued that a multi-billion dollar rescue effort is unwarranted and unwise. "The federal government looks like it's developing into a nanny state. They're giving people free money, my money and your money are paying for counseling! You're crazy if you don't think this is going to be a boondoggle."

Factor Follow Up Segment 
Texas won't close lawbreaking strip joint
As reported previously on The Factor, the Dallas strip club that hired a 12-year old to dance nude remains open for business. Texas Attorney General Greg Abbott entered the No Spin Zone with an update on the outrage. "We're working around the clock on this issue," Abbott declared. "We will find out if anyone connected with the ownership or anyone else can be prosecuted criminally. If they knew this was a 12-year old girl, we believe they can be prosecuted. We are also looking at ways we can shut down this club." The Factor praised Abbott for his diligence, and urged Dallas to pass new laws. "According to Dallas city ordinance, this club can just keep on rolling, and that's got to be changed. We can't have 12-year old girls being exploited like this."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Jay Leno and political incorrectness
Late night host Jay Leno is under fire for jokingly asking actor Ryan Phillippe to give his "gayest look." Leno apologized, but some homosexuals remain offended. "There's a line that was crossed here," gay rights activist Wayne Besen said. "If he told an actor to 'look Jewish,' I don't think anyone would be laughing. If you look at television now, you have gay joke after gay joke. People are not punch lines, and it's going over the line when just being gay is ridiculed and scorned." The Factor pointed out the double standard that applies in comedy and modern American life. "You can make fun of white Christian men, you can make fun of famous people, but if you make fun of any minority you are going to catch Hell."

Miller Time Segment 
Dennis Miller on Rev. Wright
Barack Obama's former pastor Jeremiah Wright has been greeted with raucous applause at some recent appearances, but Dennis Miller certainly isn't among the worshipful. "To me he's like Jeremiah Dice Clay," Miller joked, "and I think it's a bit of an act. But one thing he knows about America - it's a place where you can stand at a pulpit and say the government has injected members of your race with the AIDS virus, and you don't have to worry about the government coming and injecting you with the AIDS virus. The stuff's idiotic and I don't think he even believes it. I can only assume Barack Obama stayed in that church for twenty years for the same reason Hillary Clinton has stayed in her marriage, and that's political expediency." Miller also took a moment to skewer Ted Turner, who claimed global warming will eventually result in cannibalism. "He's a rich guy who won a boat race, started a TV station, but he's not a genius. And I'd rather be a cannibal than eat that crap bison meat that Ted Turner's pushing."
Back of Book Segment 
Policing the 'Net: Incriminating photos
Internet expert Mary Katharine Ham began with 67-year old Max Mosely, president of an international auto racing association, who was caught having sex with five German prostitutes. Mosely is threatening to sue the newspaper that put video of the encounter on its web site. "He may go after the newspaper," Ham reported, "for 'breach of confidence.' But he put himself in a horrible situation and has to take responsibility for the fact that this got out on the web. There's no bringing it back." Ham also reported on the mother who wants police to arrest a young man who posted nude photos of her daughter on the Internet. "The pictures were on the young woman's computer, and their story is that her ex-boyfriend stole them. But the problem is that your private photos are no longer private. If they're digital, chances are they'll be sent to somebody. Do not let your boyfriend near you with a camera if you're naked."
