Top Story 
New developments in Limbaugh-NFL controversy
Juan began by replaying an excerpt from Thursday's Factor, during which he debated the Rush Limbaugh-NFL controversy with black radio host Warren Ballentine. At the end of the discussion, Ballentine told Juan to 'go back to the porch,' and on Friday Juan Williams delivered this response: "This guy told me to 'go back to the porch.' That's code for being an Uncle Tom, a house Negro, a tool of slave owners. A real man would probably apologize, but Ballentine is bragging about it. Apparently it's okay to use these kinds of racial slurs as long as you're defending what some consider the politically correct point of view."

Juan pursued the story with Rev. Ken Hutcherson, a black former NFL star and a friend of Rush Limbaugh. "What Warren Ballentine said to you," Hutcherson told Juan, "was one of the most racist statements anyone can say - the man flat-out called you a house Negro. We should not have to put up with that kind of rhetoric from the other side that calls themselves 'tolerant.' This is about attacking conservative values and I'm not going to put up with it." Radio talk show host Tammy Bruce contended that the liberal media, unions and other groups teamed up against Limbaugh. "Never have I seen, including my time on the left, such an effort to destroy an individual. This is an effort to isolate and demonize those who do not conform, and the American people have to ask themselves whether this is the kind of dynamic they want." Juan summarized the developments of the prior few days: "We've seen the MSNBC's and the CNN's of the world put out statements attributed to Rush Limbaugh that turned out to be absolutely false. And even for me, as someone who sees himself on the liberal side of things, the minute you disagree you get put down."
Impact Segment 
Analyzing Obama's road show
President Obama is seemingly back in campaign mode, promoting his health care initiatives to various audiences. Juan analyzed the president's strategy with speechwriters from both political parties. "You have to get out there and sell your agenda," said Democrat Josh Gottheimer. "Getting elected is just the beginning, and when you have folks on the right throwing mud at you all day long, a president has to use the bully pulpit and make his point. Barack Obama has gone out of his way to reach across the aisle, but the Republicans want to stonewall." Naturally, Republican Marc Thiessen reached a different conclusion. "The president is in a permanent campaign. If you go back and look at his two predecessors at this stage, President Clinton and President Bush had both passed bipartisan bills. Barack Obama doesn't have a single bipartisan initiative and on every issue he's pursued a maximalist liberal position."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Promoting service & volunteerism on network TV
The federal government has teamed up with the entertainment industry to promote "service and volunteerism," leading some conservatives to complain about government-inspired propaganda. TV critic Linda Stasi entered the No Spin Zone and rejected the notion of a conspiracy. "A memo was sent out about volunteerism - it encourages the promotion of wellness, community support and supporting military families. It doesn't say anything about health care." But Patrick Courrielche of BigGovernment.com contended that some TV programs are promoting Obama administration policies. "This is a pattern we've been seeing. There has been policy advocacy that has resulted from White House initiatives - we've seen policy advocacy in TV shows and videos and on posters."
Personal Story Segment 
Cheerleader permanently disabled by flu shot?
26-year-old Washington Redskins cheerleader Desiree Jennings is suffering from grave physical ailments that she claims resulted from a seasonal flu shot. Juan welcomed Dr. Leigh Vinocur, who contended that both seasonal and H1N1 vaccines are safe. "I researched the literature extensively," Vinocur reported, "and I could not find where anyone reported this kind of reaction. Some movement disorder experts have looked at the video of this woman and they feel it could be psychogenic, meaning it isn't caused by the flu or the vaccine. There will be people who have bad reactions, but with vaccinations you always weigh the public health benefits. You need to get the flu shot and protect yourself from H1N1, and these are very safe vaccines."
Unresolved Problems Segment II 
Was the runaway balloon story a hoax?
Juan explored the strange case of the runaway helium balloon, which captured national attention Thursday when people thought it was carrying 6-year-old Falcon Heene. The boy was actually hiding in his family garage, and there are accusations of a hoax engineered by the parents. Fox News correspondent Alicia Acuna reported the latest from Colorado. "At this point," Acuna said, "the sheriff does not think this was a hoax or publicity stunt. But the family's past behavior has raised a lot of questions - they've been on reality TV shows and the kids have their own rap video on YouTube." Juan questioned why the Heenes were so eager to appear on TV. "This family was all over television today and it sure looks like they're interested in publicity. It's not hard for people to reach the conclusion that this was more than a mere accident." Psychologist Keith Ablow also offered his expert analysis, and left no doubt about his conclusion. "This is a family addicted to the media. Who would tote their kids to media outlets after the supposed near-death experience of their son? Publicity may be the only thing these people care about, the same way a heroin addict only cares about heroin."
Back of Book Segment 
Harvard rescinds invite to Minuteman Project founder
A student group at Harvard University extended - then rescinded - a speaking invitation to Jim Gilchrist, founder of the anti-illegal immigration Minuteman Project. Juan welcomed Harvard junior Kyle de Beausset, who fought to prevent Gilchrist from speaking. "The First Amendment protects us from the government infringing upon our right to free speech," de Beausset said, "but it doesn't obligate private institutions to give a platform to extremists like Jim Gilchrist. Gilchrist has shown his ability to distort the truth completely - is that someone we should give a platform?" Juan suggested that the Harvard students displayed a lack of backbone. "People must have done research and they knew who they were inviting. Once you issue the invitation, I don't see the basis for then saying don't show up."
Factor Flashback Segment 
Are we facing a shark attack pandemic?
Finally, Juan introduced Bill's recent interview with Discovery Channel shark expert Andy Dehart, who warned that sharks are in grave danger. "We're killing sharks at the rate of 250,000 a day," Dehart claimed, "and most of that is being done for their fins, which are a delicacy in China. A lot of this is going on in international waters, and about a third of the ocean-going species are endangered." Dehart added that, because of that population decline, attacks on humans are decreasing. "We've seen a gradual decline in shark attacks - the reality is that sharks are not hunting humans for food. The odds of getting attacked by a shark are far less than being struck by lightning."
