Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Obama using contraception controversy to win over female voters?
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guests: Mona Charen & Sally Quinn

"Last Friday we invited 30-year-old Sandra Fluke to appear on The Factor. The Georgetown Law student has become the poster woman in the debate over birth control coverage mandated by the federal government. Ms. Fluke would not appear; she has chosen friendly venues where she will not be challenged. I did not want to talk about contraception - that's a personal issue and she's free to do whatever she wants. I did want to ask the young woman about her sense of entitlement. Why should anyone have to pay increased insurance premiums so she and others can get the pill for free? When the Obama administration ordered the Catholic Church to cover birth control and the morning after pill, they quickly got into trouble. Sensing danger, the administration pivoted and attempted to make the situation about 'women's health.' Enter Sandra Fluke, a contraception activist. Democrats tried to force a House committee to hear her testimony, but the committee said she was not qualified to speak on church-state issues. That's when Ms. Fluke went public. Presto! What was a big loser - President Obama intruding on the Catholic Church - became a big winner - President Obama protecting the rights of women. In reality, the whole contraception situation is bogus - 75% of counties in the USA provide free birth control. President Obama needs American women to support him in November and Sandra Fluke is now a major part of the Obama reelection campaign. That's what this is all about."

The Factor pursued the issue with columnists Mona Charen and Sally Quinn. "President Obama has been given a huge gift by Republicans and the religious right and Rush Limbaugh," Quinn declared. "They have managed to make the Republicans and the religious right look as though they are anti-women, and it may be true that they're not supportive of women." Charen accused Democrats of exploiting Sandra Fluke for political gain. "Once again Democrats and liberals have demonstrated a real talent for changing the subject to something they find more congenial. They didn't like the subject of the Obama attack on the faith community, so now they've created this 'war on women' idea that if you are not given something for free, this is somehow denying you your right." The Factor reiterated that "no one is denying American women access to contraceptives, you can get them free almost anywhere you want."
Impact Segment 
Super Tuesday preview
Guests: Juan Williams & Mary Katharine Ham

On the eve of ten Republican primaries, The Factor solicited predictions from Juan Williams and Mary Katharine Ham. "Romney looks like a sure bet," Williams stated. "I think he turned a corner in Michigan - there will be pockets of resistance, but the pattern looks like he has momentum and he will race past Santorum in Ohio, which is the critical state Tuesday." Ham agreed that Tuesday will indeed be "super" for Romney. "He is pulling ahead in Ohio and he's going to get a lot of the delegates Tuesday even if he doesn't win every state. The issue will be whether Gingrich stays in, and if he wins Georgia that gives him a rationale to stay in."
The Hume Zone Segment 
Is Romney about to wrap this race up?
Guest: Brit Hume

The Factor also invited FNC analyst Brit Hume to preview Super Tuesday. "We're now at the stage of this contest," Hume said, "where the margin of victory matters in terms of delegates. This is a long effort to gain enough delegates to win, and even in states where he loses Tuesday, Mitt Romney will get some delegates. He'll get nearly all of them in Virginia and Massachusetts and he might get the lion's share in Ohio." Hume suggested that either Newt Gingrich or Rick Santorum could soon be packing it in. "There comes a point in these campaigns where even though you're getting some delegates, you don't have any money. What's been keeping Santorum and Gingrich alive are a couple of very rich guys who have been willing to finance their super-PACs. You have to wonder how much longer they're going to do this."
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Federal judge under fire for sending a racist email about Pres. Obama
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guests: Kimberly Guilfoyle & Lis Wiehl

District Judge Richard Cebull has been threatened with impeachment after he emailed a racially offensive "joke" about President Obama. Fox News legal analysts Lis Wiehl and Kimberly Guilfoyle assessed the controversy. "He will not be impeached," Wiehl declared. "Impeachment requires 'high crimes and misdemeanors' and since 1804 there have only been 15 impeachment hearings for federal judges." Nevertheless, Guilfoyle urged Judge Cebull to resign. "That's the right thing to do because it's setting a standard. For him to make this kind of statement is unconscionable." The Factor questioned Cebull's mental competence, saying, "No one can justify this 'joke,' so you would have to say there is something wrong with this guy."
Weekdays with Bernie Segment 
Analyzing the media's coverage of the birth control debate
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guest: Bernie Goldberg

Bernie Goldberg entered the No Spin Zone to evaluate the media's handling of the story involving law student Sandra Fluke. "All the attention is now focused on the comments Rush Limbaugh made," Goldberg lamented. "That's legitimate, but it has distracted us from another set of questions. Here's a woman who is smart enough to get into Georgetown Law School, yet she thinks a Catholic university should change its values to suit her. She believes, and a lot of other people on the left believe, that the federal government has the right to require an entire industry to give away a product for free. She is a poster person for the entitlement society - let's have a discussion about that. But because of what happened on a radio program, the media's attention is focused on that." The Factor contended that "the media that wants to reelect Barack Obama doesn't want to talk about what you're talking about and what I'm talking about."
Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Deadly tornadoes ravage the Midwest
There was another lethal reminder of nature's unpredictability last week. The Factor's Check: "The death toll is now 39 and rising after tornadoes tore through ten states. The nation was transfixed when 15-month-old Angel Babcock was found alive in an Indiana field. People thought she might survive, but she subsequently died from her injuries." General Motors announced that it will temporarily pull the plug on the Chevy Volt. The Factor's Check: "The electric car is not selling well so production will halt for five weeks. If you would like to buy a Volt, they cost about $40,000." Meanwhile, after conservative columnist George Will suggested that Republicans should forget about winning the presidency and instead focus on Congress, Donald Trump called him an "overrated fool" and a "hack." The Factor's Check: "For the record, we don't think Mr. Will is a 'hack,' I just think he's wrong."
