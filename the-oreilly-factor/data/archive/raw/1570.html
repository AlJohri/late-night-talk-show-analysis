Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Honoring a fallen hero
"At a White House ceremony Monday, Navy SEAL Michael Murphy's family received the Medal of Honor. The sacrifice Lt. Murphy made epitomizes the best of America, and that's why we are leading with the story. Talking Points believes the USA is a noble nation and that most of its citizens, especially the military, are good people. That puts us in stark contrast to other media outlets who consistently use their power to denigrate this nation. You often hear that Fox News Channel is some kind of Republican outfit shilling for conservatives, but that is false. What is true is that Fox News often takes a pro-USA viewpoint; that is, we give our country the benefit of the doubt. A true American hero was honored Monday, but the story received minimal attention from the liberal press. It ran in the 'Metro' section of the New York Times. If Lt. Murphy had gunned down civilians in Afghanistan, it would have been page 1 in the 'National' section so everyone in the world could see it. That's why Fox News is so desperately needed, to counter the world view of the Times, CNN and NBC News. No shilling here, just putting the truth in perspective."

The Factor welcomed Navy Seal Marcus Luttrell, who was with Lt. Murphy on the deadly mission against Taliban fighters in Afghanistan. "We were taking some heavy fire," Luttrell recounted, "and they had us completely surrounded. We were running low on ammo and had already lost one guy. Mike grabbed a phone, but he had to expose himself to get a signal. He took two rounds in the back. He was a great leader, a great friend, and the type of guy everyone else tried to emulate." The Factor reiterated that Lt. Michael Murphy exemplifies America at its best. "He made that call, which is why you are alive today. He sacrificed his life for that unit, and he died a supreme hero for his country. We send our best to Lt. Murphy's family on Long Island."

Impact Segment 
Bill Maher heckled by far-left conspiracy theorists
During a live broadcast of HBO's "Real Time," far-left host Bill Maher was heckled by even-farther-left "truthers," who believe 9/11 was engineered by the Bush administration. This comes on the heels of the invasion of a Catholic Church in San Francisco by loony protestors. The Factor played an extended clip of the Maher incident, then issued this warning: "Driven by crazy web sites, the far left is so out of control that someone is going to get hurt. No American has a right to intrude on church services, television programs, or any other private gathering. If authorities don't wise up fast, something bad is going to happen."

Unresolved Problems Segment 
Ben Stein on intelligent design
Author, lawyer and TV host Ben Stein has created a documentary about "intelligent design," the theory that a deity created the universe. "Darwinism was a brilliant theory," Stein explained, "that basically said mankind evolved from apes. But it didn't say how life began, how a cell got to have hundreds of thousands of moving parts. I thought there were a lot of gaps in Darwinism, and intelligent design is an effort to fill in some of those gaps. There are many scientists who have been expelled from their jobs because they wanted to question the limits of Darwinism." The Factor warned Stein to expect a hostile reaction. "Christopher Hitchens and Bill Maher are going to say you're a primitive and intellectually deficient. I've never understood the secular authorities in education who persecute people for raising a very legitimate question."

Personal Story Segment 
Rosie O'Donnell turns down Oprah
Former talk show host Rosie O'Donnell is refusing to appear on TV shows to promote her new book, for which she was reportedly paid $2 million. Marji Ross of Regnery Publishing stressed the importance of a promotional tour. "Her publishers must be pulling their hair out. When you ship hundreds of thousands of books in advance, you need to have media like Oprah and Good Morning America." FNC's Terry Keenan reported that she took a camera crew to one of O'Donnell's book signings "There were about ten crews there, but O'Donnell snuck in the building. I tried to get an interview, but she evaded all the cameras." The Factor theorized that O'Donnell is angry with the publisher and is sabotaging the book. "She punished Barbara Walters, she punished her magazine people. If you cross Rosie O'Donnell, she is going to try to get you. I think there are problems in Ms. O'Donnell's psyche."

Defining America Segment 
Children and political debates
The Factor welcomed Republican consultant Margaret Hoover and FNC's Kirsten Powers, who kicked off a new segment called "Defining America." They began their analysis with Congressman Pete Stark, who said U.S. troops "get their heads blown off for the president's amusement." Powers pointed out that Stark has long been a loose cannon. "He is known for saying things he shouldn't say. He called another Congressman a 'little fruitcake,' and he accused George Bush of being a terrorist." Hoover was stunned by the CNN poll in which 88% of respondents said Stark should not apologize. "If you were CNN, wouldn't you be concerned that 88% of your viewership is fringe radicals? I'd be very concerned that I'm not appealing to a widespread and centrist constituency." Hoover also criticized Democrats for using 12-year old Graeme Frost to promote their proposed expansion of a health care program. "Democrats are insensitive enough to make a political tool out of a child who has brain damage. I'm offended by the hypocrisy of liberalism." Not surprisingly, Powers blamed the other side. "He became a political football when Republicans started making up a bunch of stuff about his family."

Back of Book Segment 
Body language: Ellen / Colbert / Cosby
Tonya Reiman began her analysis with Ellen DeGeneres, who broke down while talking about a dog last week. "Everything she did indicated she really was crying," Reiman said. "She's rocking back and forth, making a pleading gesture, and all these signs indicate she's truly upset." Reiman also watched Bill Cosby's appearance on "Oprah," during which he wore sunglasses. "I think he really had something going on with his eye. But his body language showed just how close he feels to Oprah - he was leaning towards her and you can see there's good rapport."
Pinheads and Patriots 
Who's helping, and who's hurting?
Monday's Patriots: American moviegoers, who stayed away in droves from the anti-American film "Rendition", turning it into a box office bomb. And the Pinhead: John Edwards who criticized The Factor with this bit of logic: "If you actually say something, they think you're in the left wing."
