Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Economic disaster on the horizon?
"A few months ago, President Obama appointed a commission to examine America's debt now standing at more than $13 trillion. The president appointed Erskine Bowles, a former top adviser to Bill Clinton, to co-chair the commission. And now Mr. Bowles is delivering his verdict, saying 'What's clear to me from just glancing at it is that if we don't restore some fiscal sanity around here as a nation, we are going to go broke.' Now, how will Mr. Obama react? Will he stop the enormous spending? Will he cut back on entitlements? Will he reevaluate Obamacare? If I had to bet, I'd say no. The president will not do any of those things. Of course, I could be wrong. And so, the most liberal president in the nation's history, Barack Obama, is faced with a very painful reality. Income redistribution and entitlements may very well lead to the country to economic collapse. Talking Points suspects liberal Democrats will try to tax their way out of the mess. And it's not just Mr. Obama's fault. President Bush spent an incredible amount of money in his eight years in office. But the buck, so to speak, stops now with Mr. Obama. Will he change course or will we all go down?"

For more on the top story, The Factor brought in Fox News analysts Mary Katharine Ham and Juan Williams. The Factor asked Juan if he was going to renounce his liberal beliefs. Juan replied that he knew that some of the spending liberals wanted was just not sustainable: "You can't have something for nothing. And you can't go on with these liberal ways." Mary Katharine thought that Americans have been in denial over the debt situation for too long, and that the people were finally ready for politicians to do something about it: "The American public is actually clamoring for solutions and saying 'Hey, make the tough choices you said you were going to make.'" The Factor pointed out that Democrats weren't totally to blame: "When the Republican party for eight years had power, it spent like the proverbial drunken sailor -- with no ill respect to sailors." The Factor also had his own unique idea for a contribution to America's debt problem: "I'm leading the way. And I can do this, because I'm a rich man -- I'm not taking a penny of Social Security ever. I'm sending it right back in to the Treasury. You send me the check, I'm sending it back."
The Hume Zone Segment 
Questions raised about Schieffer interview
CBS News' Bob Scheiffer interviewed Attorney General Eric Holder over the weekend, and appeared to have the facts wrong on the Arizona immigration law. Scheiffer, describing the law, said this: "It allows the police, if they think someone might be in this country illegally, gives them the right to stop them. And they have to produce papers to show that, in fact, they are citizens." Fox News analyst Brit Hume said he respected Scheiffer, but thought that he had the facts wrong: "It specifically does not permit that. In fact, race may not be used at all as a criterion for questioning someone's illegal immigration status." The Factor thought that Scheiffer's statement could have been a little insidious: "Schieffer either doesn't know what the law says -- which if that's true, is troubling on many levels -- or is purposely misstating the law to play into the liberal philosophy that it's a racist law." Hume said he didn't think that Scheiffer would do something like that on purpose, and admitted that he himself had made the same mistake on the air when the story first broke. The Factor thought that Scheiffer's error was indicative of a larger problem: "Look, I'm not here to bash Bob Schieffer, but I am teed off, I'm really teed off, because I believe that the three network newscasts ignored the Black Panther polling story. Didn't cover it at all. I believe that they are now basically not being honest about the news anymore, because they have an agenda. And the agenda is to protect President Obama. That's what I believe is happening."
Impact Segment 
Why won't Holder uphold the law?
The Factor started the segment playing clips of members of the New Black Panther Party saying kooky things -- expressing admiration for Osama bin Laden, admitting engaging in voter intimidation, and arguing with Geraldo. Despite all the craziness, The Factor opined that Malik Zulu Shabazz, the Black Panther leader, wasn't the real problem: "Mr. Shabazz means nothing to this country. He's a loon looking for attention. Also, his party is as fringe as it gets. Nothing to be concerned about. But that is no reason for Attorney General Holder not to uphold the law. That's his sworn duty. Holder's getting away with this because the liberal print press and the network news pretty much ignoring the story. Somebody email Brian Williams. Once on this broadcast, I called Janet Reno the worst attorney general ever to serve. Now I must apologize to Ms. Reno. Mr. Holder has taken the mantel."
Personal Story Segment 
NAACP leader insults victim of attack
Zaki Baruti, speaking at an NAACP rally, criticized a black Tea Partier who was attacked by union members last August. Barruti called the victim, Kenneth Gladney, an "Uncle Tom" and a "negro." Radio talk show host Dana Loesch told The Factor that she was surprised that someone associated with the NAACP was attacking Gladney: "I thought the whole purpose of the NAACP was to represent the interests of the black community, and what I see happening is people putting political ideology above civil rights." The Factor was amazed that the story wasn't getting more attention: "I bet you people don't know this happened. I think this is one of the first times it's been done on the national level. I'm starting to get really, really troubled by the news blackout front in this country." Loesch said that the violence against Gladney wasn't necessarily isolated -- she revealed that she's had to ramp up her security lately because of threats. "You believe that the fringe far left is targeting you guys and wants you to get hurt?" The Factor asked. "Yes, I absolutely do. I absolutely do," Loesch replied.
Weekdays with Bernie Segment 
Bernie Goldberg on the Schieffer interview
The Factor brought in Bernie Goldberg, who has a provocative column this week speculating that Hillary Clinton will challenge Barack Obama for the presidency in 2012. "African-American voters will not forsake Barack Obama. We know that," Bernie began. "White liberals normally would never abandon the first black president, not if it means voting for some vanilla liberal Democrat white guy. But, if they get a chance to vote for Hillary Clinton, they can make history twice. The first time they elected the first black president. The second time, it will be the first woman president. And this will go a long way in easing their white liberal guilt, which normally knows no bounds." The Factor agreed that the scenario was more in play now than it was a few months ago, but still saw a lot of problems for Hillary: "It would be a ferocious, ferocious war within the Democratic Party. And Hillary Clinton would be a pariah forever if she didn't win. And the odds of her winning the nomination would be slim, because the Democratic machine is not going to throw Barack Obama overboard."
Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: 'Barefoot Bandit' in custody
The Factor introduced the Reality Check segment, "where we set the record straight." He kicked things off with a controversial commercial that CBS refused to run, from a group that opposed building a mosque at Ground Zero. Next he played a clip of the mother of the so-called "Barefoot Bandit." She told Shepard Smith that her son is very smart: "He is two or three points below Einstein." Pointing out that the Bandit had been caught by police in the Bahamas, The Factor quipped: "Well, now Einstein is in prison, where he's likely to stay for many years. Proud of that, Mom?" The Factor finished off the segment with a clip of a wedding photographer, walking backwards to photograph a newly-married couple. He doesn't notice the baptismal font behind him, and trips right into it. The Factor was sympathetic: "Looking for a dangerous job? Try photographing weddings... Poor guy."
