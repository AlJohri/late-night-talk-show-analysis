Campaign 2016 
Donald Trump, Chairman and CEO of the Trump Organization
Polling Average 23.4%

69 years old, heads a real estate empire, one of America's wealthiest individuals. Considered a presidential run in 2012. Executive producer and host, The Apprentice 2003 - present

Strengths:
Trump's biggest strength is his message and his ability to deliver it. While many supporters were initially attracted by his entertainment value, many have stayed because of the substance. Trump has highlighted illegal immigration, he has fought the political establishment and the media, to the delight of many conservative Republican voters. In effect, Trump is a giant middle finger to the political elite. Many people like that, as well as the fact that he cannot be bought off. Another strength is that this debate is set up for Trump to succeed. If he plays it straight and substantive, he will confound his opponents and the media. What is holding a lot of people back on Trump is that they think he is non-serious sideshow, so his demeanor will be crucial.

Weaknesses:
His biggest weaknesses are his bombast and aggression, which do not hold universal appeal. Those who dismiss Trump see him as a clown, someone not to be taken seriously. Also, he often cannot resist attacking people he should ignore. Another weakness if a lack of specifics on policy, which will very likely be exposed by the Fox moderators. He has yet to announce specific plans on anything, so it is absolutely unclear whether he is well versed on important issues. As just one example, Trump has said he will repeal Obamacare and replace it with "something terrific." Like what? Perhaps that is what we are about to find out.
Campaign 2016 
Jeb Bush, Former Governor of Florida
Polling Average 12.0%

62 years old, former Florida Governor, 1999-2007, and scion of a political dynasty. FL Sec. of Commerce (appointed) 1987-88; Dade County Republican Party, chairman, 1984-86. Previous occupations include: Private equity investor; Commercial real estate; Banking

Strengths:
As the son of one president and the brother of another, Bush is uniquely familiar with the process and perils of running for president. He stresses that this is not a sprint, but a marathon. Bush was very successful in running a large state under conservative principles, and he has proven beyond any doubt that he can raise vast sums from campaign contributors. Jeb Bush also speaks Spanish fluently, which certainly can't hurt him among Spanish-speaking voters.

Weaknesses:
His biggest weakness remains his ability to explain himself with clarity and pithiness. Jeb Bush hasn't been able to convince primary voters that he's not his brother or father and that he has a very conservative record (despite his work in recent years on immigration and education reform). He recently gave a very awkward and convoluted answer when asked a simple question about his own father. This is Jeb Bush's first real opportunity to speak to a national TV audience and tell them exactly who he is ... and, given his last name, who he is not.
Campaign 2016 
Scott Walker, Governor of Wisconsin
Polling Average 10.2%

47 years old, Governor of Wisconsin, 2011 - present; Milwaukee County Executive, 2002 - 2010; Unsuccessful candidate for Governor, 2006; Member, Wisconsin State Assembly, 1993 - 2002

Strengths:
Walker's biggest strength is his apparent fearlessness and willingness to stand up for his conservative principles. Despite massive protests by public employee unions and their supporters, he enacted reforms that saved Wisconsin taxpayers billions of dollars. Demonstrators actually threatened Walker's life and family, but he stayed the course with reforms that put the state on sound financial footing. This is a dramatic and compelling narrative that excites the GOP base, including social conservatives who see him as someone who is willing to fight for their causes. Governor Walker also has a track record of winning elections (even a recall) in a state that generally votes Democratic.

Weaknesses:
His experience is limited to the local and state offices, making him vulnerable when it comes to national and international affairs. Walker was a county executive before becoming governor, and thus has no experience on a national platform. He pointedly took an overseas trip this year and is trying to bone up on national policy issues. Tonight, Governor Walker needs to convince voters that he is fully up to speed on all the issues facing the next president.
Campaign 2016 
Mike Huckabee, Former Governor of Arkansas
Polling Average 6.6%

59 years old, Republican presidential candidate, 2008; Governor of Arkansas, 1996 - 2007; Chairman of the National Governors Association, 2005 - 2006; Lieutenant Governor of Arkansas, 1993-1996, former Fox News host.

Strengths:
Governor Huckabee has experience at all this, having run for the GOP nomination in 2008, and he is a polished speaker and TV performer. He is also a deeply religious man whose message resonates with evangelicals and other religious Americans. Beyond that, Huckabee can talk honestly about his hardscrabble upbringing and his mostly successful governorship.

Weaknesses:
At the moment, Governor Huckabee is badly underfunded. He's never been particularly adept at fundraising, which matters enormously in the 2016 race. Also, his record of pardons in Arkansas could be considered too liberal, and might be used against him if he begins to gain traction. Mike Huckabee's social conservatism, while appealing to some on the far right, is considered too extreme by more moderate Republican voters. His goal tonight is to emphasize his 'everyman' qualities and his effusive personality.
Campaign 2016 
Ben Carson, Professor Emeritus of Neurosurgery
Polling Average 5.8%

63 years old, acclaimed neurosurgeon and professor. Speaker, President's National Prayer Breakfast, 1997 and 2013; Recipient, Presidential Medal of Freedom by the President, 2008; Appointee, President's Council on Bioethics , 2004

Strengths:
Like Donald Trump, Carson is not a career politician in a year when voters are pining for a real outsider. He is unquestionably brilliant and has won a solid base of support that has put him ahead of some better-known senators and governors. Dr. Carson displayed some real courage when he criticized ObamaCare at a National Prayer Breakfast ... while President Obama sat on the state in stony silence. Ben Carson is a man who speaks his mind

Weaknesses:
Carson is extremely soft-spoken, which could hurt him in a raucous debate. But at the same time, he has blurted out some thoughts (on homosexuality, for example) that required a bit of backpeddling on his part. Dr. Ben Carson's greatest strength - his lack of political experience - is also his greatest weakness. He'll have to convince voters, in his modest and anti-Trumpian way, that he is ready to be the most powerful man in the world.
Campaign 2016 
Ted Cruz, U.S. Senator from Texas
Polling Average 5.4%

44 years old, Senator from Texas, 2013 - Present. Previous occupations include lawyer; state solicitor general; U.S. Justice Department official; Federal Trade Commission policy director; presidential campaign aide

Strengths:
Ted Cruz knows what he believes and how to express it. He's been steeped in politics since he was a teenager studying at a conservative think tank. Cruz is a brilliant speaker who debated competitively while at Princeton, and an unyielding disciple of conservative principles. Beyond that, he is an exciting, charismatic communicator who excites the conservative base. Cruz has raised a great deal of money and is running a strong campaign.

Weaknesses:
His oratory skills, while exciting his fans, make Cruz come across as almost too polished. More important, he has shown no sign that he can expand his appeal beyond the Republican base, a serious detriment to any Republican candidate who wants to occupy the White House. His ability to project "likability" and compassion for people's struggles are also questionable. His fights with GOP leaders win rave reviews from the base, but Donald Trump has stolen some of his anti-establishment thunder. Ted Cruz bears watching Thursday night and beyond.
Campaign 2016 
Marco Rubio, U.S. Senator from Florida
Polling Average 5.4%

44 years old, U.S. Senator from Florida, 2011 - present; Speaker, Florida House of Representatives, 2006 - 2008; Member, Florida House of Representatives, 2000 - 2008; Lawyer.

Strengths:
Senator Rubio has an incredibly moving family story, which he frequently relates with great emotion. The son of a bartender and a maid, he achieved the American Dream and firmly believes others can do the same. On a more practical level, Marco Rubio sits on the Senate Foreign Relations Committee and is well versed in foreign affairs.

Weaknesses:
Rubio's one-time support of 'comprehensive immigration reform' continues to haunt him among some in the conservative Republican base. He has waffled on that issue, which is so important to so many GOP primary voters. Senator Rubio's relative youth and lack of executive experience are also considered negatives. He has to prove Thursday that he belongs in that top tier of candidates and that his conservatism is genuine.
Campaign 2016 
Rand Paul, U.S. Senator from Kentucky
Polling Average 4.8%

52 years old, Senator from Kentucky.

Strengths: Senator Paul's strength, to his supporters, is foreign policy. The libertarian-leaning lawmaker, who was once branded 'the most interesting man in politics,' insists that he does not want to meddle in the affairs of other nations, and certainly doesn't want a war with Iran. His libertarianism, his stress on human freedom, his belief in limited government, and his opposition to official eavesdropping hold great appeal for a significant segment of the Republican electorate.

Weaknesses: Senator Paul's weakness, to his detractors, is also foreign policy. Many voters are skeptical that he has the will or the ability to be a commander-in-chief that would take on ISIS and growing unrest in the Middle East. The excitement that surrounded Paul's initial campaign launch has largely dissipated. Rand Paul's goal Thursday will be to regain some momentum and show that his libertarian ideology is not a cult, but a genuine and valuable conservative philosophy.
Campaign 2016 
Chris Christie, Governor of New Jersey
Polling Average 3.4%

52 years old, Morris County freeholder, 1995-97; Dir. Freeholder Board, 1997; Gov. of NJ, 2010-Present

Strengths: Governor Christie is whip-smart and nimble on his feet, which he has displayed repeatedly at town hall meetings across New Jersey. As for policy, because he was U.S. attorney he has always campaigned as a law and order candidate. Christie was 'on the ground,' so to speak, at the very beginning of the fight against terror. The governor has also shown, like Governor Scott Walker, that he is not afraid to take on powerful and well-funded public sector unions. He is public enemy number one among the teachers' union, making him a top choice for many Republican voters. He has also shown that he can get elected in a deep blue state.

Weaknesses: Chris Christie has lost some of the momentum he had four years ago when he declined to run for the White House. The largely-discredited 'bridgegate' affair took its toll, pardon the pun, and many voters have grown a bit weary of his confrontationaly style. Governor Christie is not especially popular in his own state, but he'll remind everyone that polls fluctuate. Chris Christie wants to regain his spot as an exciting and refreshing candidate.
Campaign 2016 
John Kasich, Governor of Ohio
Polling Average 3.4%

Governor of Ohio, 2011 - present; U.S. Representative from Ohio, 1933 - 2001; Member, Ohio Senate, 1979 - 1982, former Fox News host.

Strengths: Governor Kasich's greatest strength is his resume. He turned Ohio from a state deeply in debt to one with a billion dollar surplus and low unemployment. He defeated a Democratic incumbent and was re-elected in a must-win state for Republicans. Prior to becoming governor, Kasich was a Congressman and chair of the House Budget Committee. He helped lead the U.S. to a balanced budget, something that has not been done since. John Kasich is competing in the moderate Republican lane and can be thought of as a Bush or Christie without the baggage. The son of a mail carrier, he also has a compelling life story.

Weaknesses: Kasich's main weakness involves Obamacare. He is one of the few Republican governors who accepted Medicaid expansion, which he still defends as a moral issue. This doesn't sit well with many GOP primary voters. Also, he is famously brash and short-tempered, traits he will have to keep in check on the campaign trail. John Kasich is known for being unscripted, which his supporters say underscores his authenticity, although it can also make him appear disorganized. John Kasich will try to show voters tonight that his authenticity is, well, authentic.
