Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Is America still the land of opportunity?
"What the debt situation is really all about is your right to pursue happiness. America is the greatest country on Earth because it gives the most people the most opportunity to prosper. It does that by using the free marketplace, whereby people can work hard and provide for themselves and their families. But since the mid-1960's, America has practiced 'social engineering,' spending tax money trying to improve the lives of those who don't have very much. Liberal Americans tend to support the entitlement society, while conservatives are more inclined to promote individualism and smaller entitlement spending. President Obama is a liberal, and the Democratic Party is now dominated by the left. That's why federal spending has broken the bank in the last two-and-a-half years. But what are we getting for all the spending? The unemployment rate proves that no matter how much money the feds pump into the hands of the less affluent, it doesn't make much of a difference. Barack Obama believes Lyndon Johnson's 'Great Society' entitlements can elevate the poor to prosperity. They can't! In 1965 the poverty rate stood at 14%; now, after untold trillions have been spent fighting poverty, the rate is 14.3%! The conclusion: America is bankrupting itself with an entitlement philosophy that does little."

The Factor invited Alan Colmes and Monica Crowley to analyze the Talking Points Memo. "I disagree with your statement that this President is like Lyndon Johnson," Colmes said. "He has offered to cut Medicaid, Medicare and Social Security. He has not promoted a 'Great Society,' he has not promoted entitlement spending, he's been willing to cut it to the detriment of his own base." Crowley suggested that many entitlement programs actually harm their intended beneficiaries. "Every time we have run this experiment of big government, big spending, and big entitlements, it has led to economic catastrophe and a huge debt burden on the American people. The 'Great Society' began the big uptick in deficit spending. Not only have the poor not been elevated out of poverty, but we've also perpetuated dependency and victimhood."
Stossel Matters Segment 
What do the folks know about the debt crisis?
Fox Business anchor John Stossel took to the streets and asked folks what they know about the debt debate. He entered the No Spin Zone with his very unscientific findings. "We have jobs that allow us to pay full attention to this stuff," Stossel said, "and it's still hard to keep track. I was actually surprised that most people knew what the debt ceiling is, but they don't understand the complexities. But I don't think most politicians or most broadcasters understand the complexities either. To me the bottom line is that we've doubled spending since Bush. Barack Obama says we need 'balance, balance.' Well, balance would be to say that Bush and I doubled spending and we're going to cut it back to the Clinton years."
Impact Segment 
Arguments abound over debt talks
Fox News analyst Charles Krauthammer gave his take on the budget talks. "President Obama asked for time on national television," Krauthammer said, "which is what you do when you're launching an invasion, then he gave a campaign speech and didn't offer a plan. The President has portrayed himself as the only one who is acting in the national interest, and yet through months of argument and debate he doesn't have a plan." Krauthammer looked ahead to the coming days and weeks. "Something has to happen or we'll go over a cliff. At some point, when you have a government that's borrowing 40 cents on every dollar it spends, if you withdraw the credit card all hell breaks loose. I've been agitating for weeks for the Republicans to pass a plan in the House and it will be catastrophic if Republicans don't go along with the Boehner plan." The Factor agreed that Republicans need to be more flexible: "The big battle comes in the presidential election, so you have to appear to be reasonable to the independents."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
The media and the Norwegian mass murderer
Some liberal media outlets have branded Norway killer Anders Behring Breivik a "Christian," and Washington Post's Sally Quinn defended that description. "I say the guy's a Christian," Quinn declared. "He talks about 'Jesus Christ our Lord,' he actually said 'I am a Christian,' and he says we have to rid Christian Europe of the Islamic terrorists. If someone says they're a Christian, you have to take them at their word." The Factor questioned Quinn's logic and conclusion: "There's no evidence that this guy is in any church or that he followed the teachings of Jesus Christ, yet they call him a 'Christian' because he says he is? The Ku Klux Klan calls itself a Christian group!"
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Court OKs threatening language towards Obama
Californian Walter Bagdasarian was convicted for writing in 2008 that President-elect Obama should be shot, but a federal appeals court has overturned that conviction. Legal aces Lis Wiehl and Kimberly Guilfoyle scrutinized the decision. "The appeals court ruled that the rantings of this guy," Wiehl said, "were protected by the First Amendment. He was drunk, there was no real threat, and political speech is the most highly regarded speech in this country." Guilfoyle reached a far different conclusion. "I disagree with the ruling because I don't think you should protect violent speech. He wrote about getting a pistol and getting rid of him. Speech that incites violence is not protected." The legal wizards turned to Kim Kardashian, who is suing Old Navy for using a lookalike actress in one of their TV ads. "I really don't think she has the trademark," Wiehl said, "and this is just silliness." Guilfoyle again begged to differ, saying "I think she has a strong claim for commercial misappropriation of her likeness."
Back of Book Segment 
McDonald's to alter Happy Meals
Under pressure to provide healthier meals, McDonald's has announced that its kid-friendly "Happy Meals" will include a fruit or vegetable. The Factor welcomed health advocate Meme Roth, who is calling for a tax on unhealthy food. "Most of America is fat and sickly," Roth said, "and we're becoming a nation of invalids. It's going to be free mobility scooters for all, dialysis for all, and insulin treatments for decades for all. We haven't eaten ourselves into disfigurement until very recently and it's outrageous that we subsidize junk food." The Factor worried about government encroachment: "Life expectancy in this country is 80 years, illness has been with us since the dawn of man, and this is a matter of freedom and choice. You want a giant federal food police state."
