Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Ahmadinejad and Ground Zero
"The Iranian government gives bombs to Iraqi terrorists as American soldiers are dying. It funds Hezbollah, which started a war with Israel during which thousands of civilians were killed. It is providing sanctuary to Al Qaeda big shots. It is a fascist state where dissent is punished and women are denied many freedoms. Iran is the world's most dangerous nation. So when President Ahmadinejad announced he was going to visit Ground Zero, most Americans were appalled. So was the NYPD, which has denied Ahmadinejad's request for security reasons. There are people who believe Ahmadinejad should be allowed to visit Ground Zero because this would show the world that America is a tolerant society. But Talking Points is tired of that. The America-haters are not going to change their minds because some thug is allowed to do a dog-and-pony show at Ground Zero. The cold truth is that Ahmadinejad is a terrorist and the world needs to know how bad this guy really is. On the Radio Factor today, I received just one call disagreeing with my analysis, and that caller said I oppose Ahmadinejad because I am 'a front for the Jews.' Nice to know the mentality of Americans who are sympathetic to Iran.

The Factor welcomed human rights activist Rostam Pourzal, who opposes any sanctions on Iran. "As for the sins you just listed," Pourzal said, "there is only evidence for one, that Iran funds Hezbollah in Lebanon. The rest are speculation. And in the bigger picture, Iran cooperated with in overthrowing the governments of Afghanistan and Iraq." The Factor repeated that Ahmadinejad represents an evil regime. "Iran has stated that it wants to push Israel into the sea, it wants to have power in Iraq, it wants to develop nuclear facilities, and it oppresses its own people. Why would you let a terrorist like Ahmadinejad visit Ground Zero?"

Weekdays with Bernie and Jane Segment 
Dan Rather suing CBS for $70M
FNC media analysts Bernie Goldberg and Jane Hall examined the case of Dan Rather, who is suing CBS because of his firing after the bogus 2004 report about President Bush's military service. "This is not about money," Goldberg asserted. "This is about a very proud man trying to salvage his reputation. But he's being delusional - he's portraying himself as a man who was paid to sit behind the microphone and read the script. Rather did every key interview in that story and went over every single line." Hall agreed that Rather's case is shaky at best. "To quote his old nemesis Richard Nixon, Dan Rather gave CBS the sword. What he did was indefensible, and given his role in the program, it will be hard for him to claim that CBS did something wrong." The Factor argued that Rather is out for revenge against CBS executives. "He wants to get Les Moonves and Sumner Redstone under deposition because he knows where the bodies are buried. Nobody will win in this case - everybody will get hammered."

Factor Follow Up Segment 
O.J. Simpson charged with 10 felonies
The Factor was joined by two defense lawyers who hypothetically explained how they would defend O.J. Simpson. "The defense would be based on entrapment," said Joe Tacopina. "These guys knew what was going to happen and actually tape recorded O.J. coming into the room. And quite honestly, I would also try to get African Americans on the jury." Mike Dutko added that he would be upfront about Simpson's past. "Jurors need to be schooled that this was a set up, and that O.J. Simpson is being aggressively prosecuted now for what people think he was acquitted of in 1995." The Factor predicted that Simpson will not walk free this time around. "You can't get around the fact that his voice is on tape. He was an aggressive guy in that room, so I say he's going to get convicted on a lesser charge, probably not kidnapping."

Unresolved Problems Segment 
Racial controversy erupts in Louisiana
The Louisiana town of Jena is at the center of a racial controversy that has attracted thousands of demonstrators. Nooses were hung on a tree where some black students had been sitting, after which six black students severely beat a white classmate. Professor Marc Lamont Hill contended that black students may be facing inordinate punishment. "We know for certain that nooses were hanging from a tree. At the very least the white students who did that should have been expelled. And there's racial tension because the charges against the black students seemed excessive. The six men involved in the beat down should be punished, but the question is whether the punishment fits the crime." The Factor reminded Hill that the accused students are not victims. "You had six black students beating up one white student unconscious, and he had nothing to do with the nooses. Somebody could ask who is the aggrieved party here? Why not let the system play out and see what happens?"

News Link: Racial rallies for "Jena Six"
Personal Story Segment 
Police officer killed by illegal alien
22-year old Erik Martinez, an illegal alien who had been arrested many times previously, allegedly shot and killed Phoenix police officer Nick Erfle. Arizona State Representative Russell Pearce complained that Erfle's death is not an isolated incident. "This is happening all over. There are sanctuary policies where police are not allowed to check immigration status. We wait until someone commits a serious felony or kills a police officer. All sanctuary policies are illegal and should be eliminated." Former Congressman J.D. Hayworth eulogized Officer Erfle as an exemplary man. "He was an 8-year veteran who had received the Medal of Valor from the Phoenix PD. He had battled cancer and instead of taking a desk job, he had gone back on the streets. He has two kids and a wife, and this is a tragedy." The Factor wondered when Congress will finally take action against criminal illegals. "We need a new federal law that says every arrest of an illegal alien has to be reported to the federal authorities. I do not understand why our representatives in Washington can't pass that. If it did, this fine man would be alive."

Back of Book Segment 
The Dhue Point
In her role as "ombudswoman," Laurie Dhue relayed some viewer complaints to The Factor. One viewer wrote that "we have had enough of O.J. Simpson to last a lifetime." The Factor's response: "This is a business and you have to cover the stories like Simpson. If we didn't cover the tabloid stories, we would lose money and couldn't cover the big stories." A viewer urged The Factor to "get off the 'Iraqi government is a disaster bandwagon,'" and another complained that The Factor's treatment of Steve Doocy is "very, very offensive." The Factor protested thusly: "I'm not mean, we just give him a little jazz. Everybody gets jazzed on this program." Viewers with grievances, ranging from the serious to the frivolous, can contact Laurie Dhue at dhuepoint@foxnews.com.
Pinheads & Patriots 
Who's helping and who's hurting?
Thursday's Patriot: South Caronlia Judge Thomas Cooper, who sentenced Vinson Filyaw to 421 years for kidnapping and sexually abusing a 14-year old girl. Filyaw held the girl hostage in an underground bunker. And the Pinhead: Mayor Lois Frankel of West Palm Beach, Florida, who illegally included an anti-war statement with city water bills.
