Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Factor Viewers and the Republican Race
Watch more video in the Video Center...
"I spent hours this weekend reading your emails about last Thursday's debate and my interviews with the candidates afterward in Detroit. Opinions are all over the place, and we begin with Dennis Dreher of Canada: 'The three debate moderators claim to want to hear about policy but then instigate a hatchet job. Do you agree?' I do not. All four candidates were asked legitimate questions about policy and their political history. Inconsistencies on the part of the candidates about their policy records are fair game. Angie Sims, Chicago: 'We were disappointed because you promised you would tell us who won the debate. We stayed up late, but you never gave your opinion.' At the beginning of the Kasich interview I stated to the governor that he had the best policy arguments, so that was my pick. His problem-solving rhetoric was superior. Mike Porter, Flat Rock, Michigan: 'The post-debate interviews by O'Reilly appeared to be a set up. Standing up next to Trump so he could appear taller than he is, then sitting down with the rest of the candidates.' Simply a matter of logistics. We opened The Factor on stage because our set was in the balcony and it would have taken Mr. Trump too much time to get up there. During the first commercial break, the other guys had time to make the trek as I did. Randy Furnas, Coupeville, Washington: 'Everybody knows why Kasich hasn't had the attention of Fox News and other media. You and others want a circus to entertain the masses. It's all about ratings.' Governor Kasich has chosen to run a retail campaign where he meets with as many folks as possible. We have reported and analyzed that. Trump says something new and provocative nearly every day, so obviously he's going to get more coverage. The cornerstone of Trump's support is that his point of view echoes that of Americans fed up with phony politicians. Also, that he rejects political correctness which has battered the nation and suppressed opposing points of view. Mr. Trump has harnessed that anger into votes and it has been a brilliant strategy. Ed Zeglarski, New Port Richey, Florida: 'Bill, I saw your baiting of Trump after the debate, it was negative.' Michele Parkenham, Wisconsin: 'O'Reilly, it pains me to say it, but your fawning interview with Trump after the debate was cringe worthy.' Increasingly in America, folks are hearing what they want to hear, not what's actually being said. That new wave of behavior is sweeping over the political process, and canny power seekers are harnessing emotion to achieve success. Power can be a very dangerous thing and Americans should think long and hard before bestowing it on anyone. It is my job to keep it all real and fair. I believe I am doing my job."

The Factor welcomed Charles Krauthammer, who contended that Donald Trump's domination could be showing some cracks. "We may be at a turning point now," Dr. K theorized, "and that last debate was the lowest political discourse we have experienced for decades. We are at a point where ordnance is being dropped on Trump in a way that it was not before. The last two debates have been all-out assaults on Trump and that's where negative information about Trump finally came out. All of these questions about Trump are emerging, and the only question right now is whether it will make a difference."
Impact Segment 
The Death of Nancy Reagan
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Returning for another segment, Charles Krauthammer spoke about the late Nancy Reagan. "She was a model of elegance, style, and steel," he said. "She brought dignity to the office of First Lady, but she was also the backbone, the person Ronald Reagan listened to. It was said that President Reagan had one close friend in the world, and that was Nancy." The Factor praised Nancy Reagan's eternal devotion to her husband: "I believe she is the most fascinating character in 'Killing Reagan' because she evolved into a great hero when President Reagan became ill. What she did in the latter part of his life was very quiet but stunningly noble."
Factor Followup 
Bernie vs. Hillary
Hillary Clinton and Bernie Sanders both appeared on Fox News Monday evening, speaking one after the other at a town hall hosted by Bret Baier. Juan Williams and Katie Pavlich evaluated their performances. "What I got out of this is that Hillary Clinton is already running in the general election," Williams stated. "She came across as almost hawkish in terms of the U.S. military's actions in Libya." But Pavlich contended that Clinton is still courting the left-wing of the Democratic Party, especially on the hot button issue of abortion. "She was asked specifically about late-term abortion, which 80% of Americans think should be banned. So if she was talking to the general electorate, you would think that she would be softening her extremist views on abortion. She also said again that she never sent classified emails, which is completely false!"
Unresolved Problems 
Alarming Report on Illegal Immigration
According to the Center for Immigration Studies, both legal and illegal immigration has soared over the past few decades. The Factor asked Brit Hume what the newly released report could mean for Donald Trump. "One of the most puzzling things about this election," Hume said, "is that you would think the issue of immigration would be very high on voters' agendas. But exit polling shows that immigration is a low priority for Republican voters. So maybe people look at Trump and decide that because he will be strong on immigration, he will also be strong on other things." The Factor added that Trump relied on the immigration issue to cement his popularity. "He got a lot of attention with the wall and he built a base, then expanded the base into national security.
Back of the Book 
The State of the GOP
Some Republicans fear their party will be destroyed if Donald Trump is their nominee. The Factor spoke about a new onslaught of anti-Trump TV ads with two leading Republicans. "I think the ads will have some effect," said former Arkansas Governor Mike Huckabee, "but I don't think they will derail Donald Trump. The people who are supporting him don't really care what Trump is saying, we are seeking nothing short of an overthrow of our government by people who feel the government has punched them in the gut." Current Louisiana Governor Bobby Jindal urged conservatives to reject Trump. "I agree that voters are angry, but I hope they will channel that anger and elect a true conservative. The most important decision the next president will make is replacing Justice Scalia, and I don't have any confidence that a President Trump would protect our First Amendment and religious rights."

Jesse Watters headed to Arizona and the annual convention of UFO true believers. Here is what a few of them had to say: "I've had a lifetime of experiences with extra-terrestrials" ... "I saw a classic extra terrestrial and it scared the crap out of me" ... "I was hit by a blast of energy and I think they took me aboard to revive me" ... "I've actually been abducted to the moon." Despite their scary encounters with actual illegal aliens, many voiced their affection for Donald Trump.
