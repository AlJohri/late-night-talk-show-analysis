Top Story 
President Obama Talks Tough
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Eric began Friday's program with President Obama's year-end, pre-vacation press conference, where he took a much tougher stance on ISIS. Democrat Nomiki Konst and Republican Adam Goodman evaluated the president's new hard line and the widespread disapproval of his anti-terror policies. "He is completely out of his element," said a dismissive Goodman. "In that press conference he had more passion for Star Wars than the war against ISIS. He has trouble reassuring the American public, he is uncomfortable in this role as commander-in-chief and it showed again." Konst, not surprisingly, disagreed wholeheartedly. "I don't think Adam watched the press conference until the end. The president explained what he has accomplished and what the administration has done with the rest of the world in attacking ISIS at its core. But the Republicans out there campaigning are busy fear-mongering and they expect the president to do the same." Eric concluded, "I didn't hear anything new today, I heard him say the strategy is working while people are dying."
Impact Segment 
Friends with Putin?
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Based on their recent comments, Donald Trump and Russian President Vladimir Putin apparently have formed a mutual admiration society. Eric asked Trump spokesperson Katrina Pierson and conservative columnist Kurt Schlichter about the wisdom of cozying up to a dictator. "This is terrible," Schlichter pronounced, "and I expect to see Trump riding down the beach with Putin, shirtless and holding hands. It's disgraceful - Putin is our enemy but I'm not surprised that a guy who can't tell us what the nuclear triad is would be slobbering over dictators. At least they're not leftist dictators like Obama prefers." Pierson defended her boss's unwillingness to condemn Putin. "There is a global risk of terrorism and Vladimir Putin is a global leader. He said he recognizes the leadership and strength of Donald Trump, it's that simple. When it comes to fighting ISIS, we have to work with other world leaders." Eric reminded Pierson, "Putin is a bad guy and maybe you'd want to push back on having someone like that endorsing you."
Campaign 2016 
Battle Over Immigration
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Republican Senators and presidential hopefuls Ted Cruz and Marco Rubio have been in a verbal war, with each accusing the other of being soft on immigration. Eric sorted out the dustup with columnist Ruben Navarette and Republican pollster Kellyanne Conway. "Ted Cruz is in real trouble here," Navarette asserted. "While he has consistently been strong on immigration and Marco Rubio has been inconsistent, those roles have now reversed. Cruz has muddied the waters regarding an amendment he proposed that would have legalized undocumented immigrants." But Conway insisted that Cruz has been a consistent foe of legalization and amnesty. "I disagree completely with Ruben. One of these two senators stood by Democratic Senator Chuck Schumer and the 'gang of eight.' Marco Rubio started this fight by standing next to Schumer, yucking it up, and pushing for amnesty."
Unresolved Problems 
Enraged Conservatives
Radio talk show host Rush Limbaugh has facetiously suggested that the Republican Party, which has approved a massive new spending bill, should simply disband. Eric spoke with Democrat Jessica Tarlov and Republican Cathy Lynn Taylor about growing disaffection among some conservatives. "The spending bill is egregious and irresponsible," Taylor said, agreeing with Limbaugh. "If we wanted to have no debt and lower taxes, every single American would have to pay $232,000 out of their pockets. So we have a problem! Spending is completely out of control, Americans are angry, and the GOP is like a ship missing in the night." Tarlov argued that Republicans were probably wise to help pass the spending bill. "63% of Americans want compromise in Washington and Speaker Paul Ryan made it clear that he wanted to work with Democrats. So I don't see why some conservatives are demonizing people who are doing what they were sent to Washington to do." Eric groused, "Every time there is a threat to shut down the government, the Republicans sign a check."
Campaign 2016 
No Spin Trump
Eric re-aired excerpts from Bill's Wednesday night interview with Donald Trump, including Trump's rationalization of his harsh criticism of Jeb Bush during the previous night's debate. "He's doing very badly," Trump said, "and I don't even know if he can continue. I don't like saying bad things about Jeb Bush, I feel a little bad for him, but he struck first. His whole thing was about going after me and I thought it was inappropriate." Trump also doubled down on his claim that it will be legally permissible to ban Muslim immigrants from entering America. "We're not talking about our citizens, we are talking about people coming from outside, some of whom are not looking at us in a very positive manner. It's a subject that should be discussed. I'm doing the right thing, I bring up subjects that are important. If I didn't bring it up, you wouldn't even be talking about illegal immigration."
Back of the Book 
New Poll Implications
A new Fox News poll shows Donald Trump with a huge lead among Republican voters. The front-runner is at 39%, eclipsing Ted Cruz's 18%, Marco Rubio's 11%, and Ben Carson's 9%. Eric crunched the numbers with political scientist Larry Sabato and reporter Caitlin Huey-Burns. "Donald Trump shows no sign of fading away," Huey-Burns observed, "and we are just seven weeks away from when voters start weighing in. This poll also shows that the field is narrowing, with Trump, Cruz, and Rubio in the top tier. Chris Christie is a wild card and he has momentum in New Hampshire." Sabato expressed some surprise at Trump's enduring dominance. "I've seen several other polls where he is in the low 40s. But the candidates that the 'punditocracy' thought did well in the debate - Jeb Bush and Chris Christie - are both at 3%, although they are higher in Iowa and New Hampshire. The Republicans need to thin the herd, there are just too many people remaining."
