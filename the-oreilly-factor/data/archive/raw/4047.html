Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Reaction to the Republican Debate
Watch more video in the Video Center...
"It is beyond question that many people who watched the debate last night believe the CNBC moderators were a bit arrogant toward the contenders. Some pundits opined that the questions were designed not so much to get information, but to create controversy. Thus, the moderators got hammered. One of the most controversial moments last night was when Jeb Bush criticized Marco Rubio's attendance in the Senate. You can decide who won or lost the debate, but there is no question that Jeb Bush is in trouble, and the odds are long that the governor will win the Republican nomination. He did an excellent job in Florida and is an honest man, but fire in the belly has been lacking. This time around, GOP voters not only want a president, they want an avenger, someone to dismantle the liberal philosophy. And on the subject of liberals, it is amusing to see how they framed the debate last night. Far left crank Dana Milbank wrote in the Washington Post that 'the Republicans seem to be testing a strategy of winning by whining.' That was the tone of it - that Republicans are dunderheads and complainers. It is always astounding to read liberal columnists who totally ignore the disaster that has befallen America, preferring to denigrate opposition to their preconceived points of view no matter what the facts may dictate. All in all, the Republican debate last night provided some perspective and entertainment. Next up is a Fox Business Network GOP debate on November 10th."

The Factor asked Donald Trump to opine on the debate and to rationalize his relatively low-key performance. "It wasn't really a strategy," Trump said. "I just wanted to do a good job and I was very happy with the results. The moderators were nasty but I'm used to it. They came at me and a couple of others much stronger than they should have, but I actually liked my answers when they did that." Trump reacted specifically to CNBC's John Harwood's implication that he is not a man of 'moral authority.' "My moral authority is better than his moral authority, that I can tell you. I thought it was an unusual question and, yes, they were very nasty last night. But CNBC has suffered because of that."
Impact Segment 
Ben Carson Debates the Debates
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Republican contender Ben Carson also sounded off on Wednesday's Rocky Mountain brawl. "The debate last night crystallized the fact that these 'gotcha' type questions are not very helpful in terms of helping people understand who the candidates are," he said, "so I have asked my staff to reach out to the staffs of the other candidates. Let's talk about different types of formats, perhaps something where you have a few minutes to talk about your policies and then be questioned about them. This debate was so bad that even the Republican National Committee will recognize it was a mistake." Carson also insisted that his flat tax plan, with a low 15% rate and few deductions, is mathematically workable. "We would do some real cost-cutting, we have 4.1 million federal employees." But The Factor countered, "You'll have to settle for a 21% or 22% rate if you're elected president."
Personal Story 
Chris Christie Piles On CNBC
Watch more video in the Video Center...
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie, who excoriated the CNBC moderators Wednesday night, entered the No Spin Zone with his next-day reaction. "They were awful," he pronounced. "Not only was John Harwood incredibly biased, but all of them did not control the debate. It turned into a free-for-all and no one could understand what the rules were. They contributed to the mayhem on that stage last night." Christie singled out a question posed about whether online 'fantasy' sports should be subject to federal regulation. "Who would think that they would bring up fantasy football at a presidential debate? It's ridiculous! We have a $19-trillion debt and al Qaeda and ISIS. How crazy liberal do you have to be to believe that the federal government should be regulating fantasy football?"
Unresolved Problems 
Rubio's Personal Finances
Marco Rubio, generally considered a big winner Wednesday night, has been questioned about his personal finances. The Factor asked Fox Business News anchor Stuart Varney to elaborate. "The questioner implied last night," Varney reported, "that Marco Rubio is unfit to run the country because he cashed in a personal retirement account. But Rubio has four children who are going to expensive private Christian schools and he needed the money. He also got $800,000 for a book advance in 2012. He put $100,000 into a savings account, $65,000 went to charity, $100,000 went to settle a student debt, and he bought a fishing boat for $80,000. The implication is that a man in his position should not spend money on a fishing boat." Varney suggested that questions about Rubio's finances are unfair and out of bounds. "This is a case of the elites saying to a striver like Marco Rubio that you are not fit to run the country. It is condescending and arrogant. They don't like Republicans!"
Unresolved Problems 
Sun-Sentinel vs. Rubio
For another of Marco Rubio, The Factor turned to editorial page editor Rosemary O'Hara of the Florida Sun-Sentinel, which has urged Rubio to resign from the Senate because he has missed too many votes. "When did it be okay for politicians to not do their job," O'Hara asked, "because they want to do another job? Marco Rubio is our Senator, he convinced us that he wanted to fight for us in the Senate. We have a lot of issues in Florida on which we need him to be representing us, but he is not at committee meetings and he has missed more votes than any other Senator." O'Hara insisted that the Sun-Sentinel is not just another liberal organ, saying, "We endorsed Marco Rubio for Senate and we endorsed Mitt Romney for president."
Miller Time 
Miller's Goodbye to 'Tammy Faye Boehner'
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Dennis Miller bade a not-very-tearful farewell to John Bohener, who has stepped down as Speaker of the House. "I would like to say," Miller began, "that they'll be looking back at this guy as a tepid individual in the history books. But the fact is that this country is so screwed that they're not going to be teaching American history in 20 years. This whole thing about there not being anything wrong with crying? Sure, but there's nothing right about it, either. Am I supposed to fall down and think you're an empathetic cat and I'm not because you're tearing up every two days? You didn't do the job right, John, you weren't good at it! You're the loyal opposition but you turned into the lapdog opposition. C'mon, wake up!"
