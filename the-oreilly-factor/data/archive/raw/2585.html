Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Fox News vs. Jon Stewart... again
"Jon Stewart of The Daily Show has emerged as a devoted critic of Fox News, and this is no small matter in the liberal media. The Daily Show is a key component of left-wing television because Mr. Stewart is a smart guy who has big-time credibility with younger Americans. I like Stewart but there is no question that he's a committed liberal. Apparently his main beef with FNC is that we are hypocritical, we slant the news to make the Obama administration look bad, and we generalize all day long about the American left. Enter Bernie Goldberg, a frequent Stewart target, who said the comedian is the one embracing hypocrisy. After hearing that, Stewart devoted eleven minutes of his show last night to mocking Bernie, me, and Fox News in general. There are two serious points here: First, why are there no conservative comedians with their own TV programs; second, I'm now convinced that Jon Stewart really likes us. A guy like Jon would be bored out of his mind watching CNN or NBC News, but here we have all kinds of views and debates. That's why Jon Stewart loves us and needs us, especially Bernie Goldberg."

The aforementioned Bernie Goldberg entered the No Spin Zone for his turn at bat in this extra-innings feud with Jon Stewart. "I don't have a problem with anything he did last night," Goldberg said, "and I'm flattered that he spent half his show talking about me. Clearly I got to him by saying he isn't as gutsy as he thought he was." But Goldberg lamented that some of Stewart's viewers have attacked him like rabid dogs. "My website is usually a civil place, but these people came out of the sewer with the most vile and hateful and vulgar stuff. Jon Stewart and I could a have a civil discussion about where we disagree, but his hard-core fans don't want any criticism of their guy." The Factor contended that Stewart, despite his relatively meager audience, wields vast influence: "With the decline of the New York Times and other newspapers, Jon Stewart has emerged as the point man for the left-wing media in America. He is far more influential than Bill Maher, Michael Moore, and all the other bomb-throwers."

The Factor pursued the Stewart-Goldberg story with liberal talk show hosts Leslie Marshall and Nancy Skinner. "Stewart is a comedian," Marshall said, "and the reason he's attacking you and Fox News is because you guys are number one. You are king of the mountain and he's glad you're talking about him." Skinner complained about the seemingly unbridgeable divide in America. "There's a dominant conservative worldview and a dominant progressive worldview. And when people have their own world view, which is their reality, and someone challenges that, they say you're out of touch with reality. I come on Fox News and I like the spirited debate we have here because it's the way to change hearts and minds."
Personal Story Segment 
Can Obama turn around his declining poll numbers?
President Obama's approval ratings, like those of most other presidents, have steadily declined since he took office. The Factor asked political strategist Dick Morris how he would advise the President to turn things around. "I would say to him," Morris stated, "that if you want to pursue your left wing agenda and cram things through Congress without regard to public opinion, you're going to get a Republican majority in both houses of Congress and you'll lay the groundwork for your defeat in 2012. But on the other hand, if you really want to move to the center, there are very real compromises where you can get a large part of what you want without polarizing the country. But I think Obama is a dedicated, committed left-wing ideologue socialist, and I don't think he's about to change."
The Kelly File Segment 
Arizona authorities expected to pass immigration reform
The state of Arizona is about to enact a law giving police the authority to detain anyone who can not prove citizenship. FNC anchor and attorney Megyn Kelly predicted a rash of legal challenges. "If this becomes law," Kelly said, "it could be challenged by the Obama administration, by private groups, and by the ACLU representing illegal aliens. They'll say that the feds have exclusive jurisdiction to regulate immigration and the states don't have any business trying to mess with federal laws." The Factor argued that the administration will face public opprobrium if it disputes the law: "If the Justice Department challenges this, President Obama is going to get hammered. People will say Arizona is trying to protect itself and the federal government is saying it can't." Kelly also visited the case of a 34-year-old Nevada woman who got a life sentence after asking a 13-year-old boy touch her breast. "She was convicted of lewdness, and under Nevada law that requires a life sentence. The judge had no choice." Kelly added that the woman will be eligible for parole after ten years.
Miller Time Segment 
Left-wing protesters heckle President Obama
The Factor invited Dennis Miller to weigh in on the hecklers who interrupted President Obama's speech with demands that he repeal the military's "don't ask, don't tell" policy. "He's a little dismissive when he doesn't dig what you're saying," Miller observed, "and right now he's like Wile E. Coyote on that tightrope that's eating in from both sides. The right's not agreeing with him, and he's getting heckled at Democratic events. If he thinks the tea party can get cranky, he hasn't lived until that first SEIU guy doesn't get his pension check." Miller turned to the poll showing that 60% of Americans are ready to legalize medical marijuana. "We're on a slow saunter over to 'Loserville.' I am buying Xbox 360 futures and I'm shorting shoe leather because we have a system now where you can get two years of unemployment, you can get Obama-care at home until you're 26, I'm pretty sure you can buy medical marijuana with the Obama-care, and then you can use food stamps for the munchies. This is a perfect loser storm."
Back of Book Segment 
Did you see that?
The Factor invited FNC anchor Jane Skinner to watch clips from the Fox TV show "Glee," which gleefully and repeatedly takes shots at conservatives such as Sarah Palin and Ann Coulter. "It's fair to say that the cheap shots most often come at conservatives," Skinner said, "but I'm not offended. At the end of the day people see it as entertainment, and the kids watching that show have parents who have a responsibility to make sure they offer other world views." The Factor was less forgiving of "Glee's" gratuitous and selective ridicule: "I don't know why they have to mention Ann Coulter in the same breath as Lindsay Lohan. Are they trying to indoctrinate young people?"
Pinheads and Patriots 
Demi Moore & Matthew McConaughey
Wednesday's Patriot: Actress Demi Moore, who visited children in a hospital in earthquake-ravaged Haiti. And the Pinhead: Actor Matthew McConaughey, who told an awards show crowd exactly when he and his girlfriend conceived their daughter.
