Talking Points Memo 
Are the Democrats in peril?
"With polls showing support for Democrats cratering, one would thing party leaders would do something dramatic to stop the bleeding. But top Democrats such as House Speaker Nancy Pelosi keep throwing salt on the party's wounds. Case in point: Her handling of tax cheat and ethical embarrassment Congressman Charles Rangel. Rangle, a longtime Democrat from New York, is Chairman of the Ways and Means Committee, responsible for writing our nation's tax laws. He's the subject of a wide-ranging investigation by the House Ethics Committee. They're examining his sweetheart deal with a developer, his condo sale in the Dominican Republican, and his lobbyist-paid trips. In his financial disclosure forms he neglected to mention accounts totaling several hundred thousand dollars. Despite all this, Pelosi refuses to do the right thing and relieve Rangel of his coveted chairmanship. That is outrageous, especially for a Speaker who promised a new era of accountability by 'draining the swamp.' She's not only not draining the swamp, she's polluting it by protecting Rangel at every disgraceful turn. Or are tax laws just for the little people? Madame Speaker, the American people are waiting for an answer."
Top Story 
White House to redouble health care reform efforts
In an effort to revive his health care initiative, President Obama will speak to a joint session of Congress next week. Laura asked conservative writer Andrea Tantaros and Democratic strategist Kiki McLean about the president's prospects. "We're 80 percent of the way," McLean boasted. "We've got bills out of four committees, and it will take a lot to get completely across the line and make the touchdown. That's what the president is stepping up to the plate to do." Tantaros denounced those Democrats who have injected race into the debate. "People don't care that President Obama is black, they care that he's green. They say, 'what is this guy doing?' Nancy Pelosi has taken ownership of this, and he has to come out and not only prove his points, he has to disprove all the concerns. I doubt if he'll be able to do it in the speech." Laura suggested that President Obama has made some serious political missteps: "This was the centerpiece of his presidency and he lost control of the debate because it wasn't framed by him. That's amazing for somebody as talented as President Obama."
Impact Segment 
New attack ad dredges up ancient history
The Democratic Party has produced an ad that ridicules former VP Dick Cheney for his erroneous predictions about the war in Iraq. Laura invited Democratic strategist Keith Watters to explain his party's strategy. "Cheney is on television every weekend bashing the Obama administration," Watters said, "so at some point it has to be addressed." Watters specifically denounced Cheney's defense of enhanced interrogation. "We're a nation of laws and we have to apply them to all people who come into our custody. I don't believe in enhanced interrogation, which is another term for torture." Laura responded with two rhetorical questions: "How many people could be sacrificed in this utopian, ivory tower approach to interrogation? And who would want to work for the CIA when they think they're going to be prosecuted?"
Personal Story Segment 
Van Jones on race and green jobs
President Obama's "green jobs czar" Van Jones, a former communist, has accused whites of various environmental crimes; he also signed a petition implying that the Bush administration engineered the 9/11 attacks. Laura welcomed media analyst Bakari Kitwana, who praised and defended Jones. "Van Jones is very accomplished," Kitwana maintained. "He's been recognized as a global leader and he's been an activist who has gotten many young people involved in civic engagement. His track record makes him a good choice." But Republican strategist Angela McGlowan questioned why a radical leftist is in the White House. "People like this guy use the race card to cause more racial division in this country. He's an oppressor and a victimhood vendor who puts race in everything." Laura complained that Jones "seems to have a deep-seated hatred of the fundamental values that make up the American way of life."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
New charges brought against David Earls
Bill has reported frequently on Oklahoma child rapist David Earls, who was sentenced to just one year in prison for raping a 4-year-old girl. Earls was about to be released, but Oklahoma Attorney General Drew Edmondson has obtained another indictment that will keep Earls behind bars. Laura analyzed the case with radio talk show host Pat Campbell. "We have to give kudos to Bill O'Reilly and Geraldo Rivera," Campbell said. "If it hadn't been for them and the national attention they brought to the case, this guy would have been released and would have had the chance to destroy one more child's life. Because Bill put David Earls' photo on Fox News, people came out who had been abused by him in the past, including his daughter. This guy is a serial pedophile with a history that goes back 35 years."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Disturbing photos of hazing in Afghanistan surface
Some private security guards at the U.S. Embassy in Afghanistan were photographed semi-naked and obviously drunk. Laura was joined by Fox News analyst Lt. Col. Ralph Peters, who condemned the guards and their actions. "We've got these clowns, these perverts," Peters began, "and the problem is this - those pictures are going to circulate throughout the Muslim world and they'll be billed as U.S. soldiers. But these guys aren't U.S. soldiers, they're thugs. There has to be a point where we say we won't hire mercenaries." Laura agreed that the contractors went far beyond what is acceptable: "In a war zone it's hard to blow off steam, but these guys went crazy and brought prostitutes in. It was just a complete mess."
Culture War Segment 
Should we worry about liberal indoctrination of kids?
Laura asked teacher Brian Crosby, author of "Smart Kids, Bad Schools," about President Obama's upcoming address to America's schoolchildren. "President Obama wants to reach out to young people," Crosby said, "and I think that's very admirable. But when teachers are given materials and are told to teach to this, that is not okay. Teachers are independent-thinking people and they know what's best for the curriculum." Laura reported that the Obama speech, however innocuous, has alienated some parents. "There's a real gulf of mistrust between parents and the government when it comes to education. This all smacks of an underhanded attempt to influence the way young people think about issues."
Back of Book Segment 
Nancy Pelosi's dismal job approval rating
Only 19% of Americans have a positive view of Speaker Nancy Pelosi's job performance, while 48% give her negative marks. Laura was joined by longtime San Francisco civic leader Angela Alioto, who defended her friend Mrs. Pelosi. "We are so proud of Nancy Pelosi that it isn't even funny," Alioto declared. "They have given jobs to millions of Americans, they are saving young couples from losing their homes, and with Nancy Pelosi's leadership, the president is about to have health care reform, one of the most historic pieces of legislation in history." Laura respectfully encouraged Alioto to re-enter reality: "You're saying this has been an unmitigated success, but unemployment is at a disturbing high. Nancy Pelosi's numbers are abysmal, and even independents are fleeing her. 'San Francisco values' sell in San Francisco, but not here."
