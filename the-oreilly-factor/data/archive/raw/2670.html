Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
A big misstep by the White House
"It is hard to believe, but President Obama has once again alienated millions of Americans by inserting himself in a controversy that can do him no good at all. Some Muslims want to build a mosque a couple of blocks from Ground Zero in New York City, but some 9/11 families believe it's insensitive, and 64% of Americans say it is wrong. President Obama is framing the issue as a 'freedom of religion' deal, but that's not what it's about. This is about sensitivity towards thousands of Americans who lost loved ones in the 9/11 attacks. The action is certainly inappropriate, and even some Muslims understand that. Once again President Obama is going against the will of the American people, but for what purpose? This is another in a long line of dubious decisions by the President, so here's my advice to the White House: Why don't you help these guys build a mosque, and then try the mastermind of 9/11, Khalid Sheikh Mohammed, in it? That way you can combine two insane things together."

The Factor welcomed two liberal Democrats - radio talk show host Mike Papantonio and political strategist Penny Lee. "I was talking to some Democratic campaign managers," Lee said, "and they are disappointed that they have to address this in their local elections. So there is some damage, but I think at the end of the day the election will still be about the economy and jobs." Papantonio praised President Obama for taking an unpopular stand. "What separates political leadership from a political hack is that the leader stands up when the mob mentality may not go along. The vast majority of Americans think this is the wrong move, so his choice was to act like a leader or cave in." The Factor questioned the President's judgment in this and other matters: "I've been fair to President Obama, but there's been one crazy thing after another. I don't think he understands the feelings of those who lost loved ones on 9/11."

FNC analyst Charles Krauthammer, an outspoken foe of the proposed mosque, reacted to the President's weekend statements. "President Obama set himself up on Friday's Ramadan dinner," Krauthammer said, "as the man who stands up for American principles, and he gave the distinct impression that he was in favor of the mosque. His acolytes and sycophants in the press hailed it as his act of greatest courage. Then, the day after, he contradicted himself and said he was only talking about the legality. If he was a man of courage, at the Ramadan dinner he would have told an audience of Muslims that he urges them to build the mosque elsewhere. Instead, he got all the applause at the Ramadan dinner and then, a thousand miles away the next day, he made his retraction. He's not a leader - he's a man who tries to please all sides."
Personal Story Segment 
Explaining Obama's motivation for participation
The Factor, admittedly perplexed, asked FNC analysts Mary Katharine Ham and Leslie Marshall why President Obama got involved in the mosque issue. "My rule of thumb," Ham opined, "is that as soon as the President says 'let me be clear,' we're about to get confused. He has a tendency to clod hop into these very serious and sensitive issues with big pronouncements, and then he steps back." But Marshall portrayed President Obama as a paragon of principle. "This is a man who practiced constitutional law for ten years and this is a mountain he's willing to die on. This is not about politics and the American people should be happy about that." The Factor concluded that "the White House is desperately out of touch with the folks."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
General Electric, the SEC and corruption
The Securities and Exchange Commission recently fined General Electric nearly $24 million for paying bribes in Iraq. Nile Gardiner of the conservative Heritage Foundation laid out the details. "GE violated the terms of the U.N. oil-for-food program," Gardiner explained. "This was a humanitarian program aimed at helping the Iraqi people, but it was monumentally abused by the Iraqi government and Saddam Hussein. Over 2,000 companies paid kickbacks to the Iraqi regime to secure contracts, including two subsidiaries of General Electric. The oil-for-food scandal was the biggest financial scandal of modern times, and the fact that GE was involved is hugely shameful." Shareholder activist Tom Borelli portrayed GE under CEO Jeffrey Immelt as morally bereft. "There's been a pattern of corruption at General Electric. They have also settled with the SEC over charges that they were cooking the books to boost their earnings, and right now the SEC is looking at whether Immelt misled investors during the Wall Street meltdown."
Weekdays with Bernie Segment 
The media and Obama's position on the mosque
How has the media been covering the proposed mosque near Ground Zero? Who better to ask than FNC media analyst Bernie Goldberg. "Big media has repeatedly described the imam behind the mosque," Goldberg complained, "as a 'moderate.' But not long after 9/11 this 'moderate' imam said America's policies were 'accessories to the crime.' That doesn't sound very moderate to me. If some mainstream Christian minister said anything that controversial, I guarantee the media wouldn't call him a 'moderate.' The American people have been told over and over to be sensitive to Muslim sensibilities, but it's about damned time that Muslims be sensitive to American values." Goldberg predicted that President Obama's fans in the media will remain in his corner. "They slobbered over him during the campaign and they think he is too historically important to fail. As his poll numbers drop they feel safe going after him, but when he runs against a real Republican all will be forgiven and they will circle the wagons around him again."
Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Obama at the Gulf Coast
Eight spectators were killed when a truck went out of control during a race in California over the weekend. The Factor's Check: "The California 200 is an unregulated road race and it is obviously very dangerous stuff. Police say they will not charge the driver of the truck, but there are calls to ban this kind of activity." The First Family did some swimming and sunning in the Florida panhandle over the weekend. The Factor's Check: "We would like to encourage everybody to visit the Gulf Coast and help your fellow Americans in the process." And finally, the reality show Jersey Shore has been wildly successful for MTV. The Factor's Check: "The most recent airing was the number one telecast in the country among viewers 12 to 34. That is age, not IQ."
