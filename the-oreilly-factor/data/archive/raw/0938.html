Talking Points Memo 
Fighting terror & keeping America safe
"The arrests of the terrorists in England has once again brought home the danger we all face from fanatical Muslim killers. Now an intense debate has broken out between the left and the right in the USA. The left wing New York Times urged that the terror arrests should not be turned 'into a political game plan.' It is understandable why The Times doesn't want to get into a partisan debate because that newspaper and the left in general opposes most information gathering techniques used by the Bush administration. Indeed, the right wing editorial page at The Wall Street Journal said this: 'The plot was foiled because a large number of people were under surveillance ... which leads us to wonder if Scotland Yard would have succeeded if the ACLU or the New York Times had first learned the details.' That sarcastic reference is about The Times exposing an entirely legal financial terror monitoring program the Bush administration had in place. Because of things like that, the left is faced with a decision - change its policies on terror or go on the attack against the Bush administration. Guess what Howard Dean decided to do? The DNC chair said this: 'The president ... Karl Rove and all those folks, they're trying to scare people again ... we have to stop trying to scare people in order to win elections.' There is no question that Iraq remains the weakest part of the Bush anti-terror strategy, leaving Americans with the same old question - which party will protect us best against the Islamic fascists?"
Top Story 
Press Secretary Tony Snow
Guest: White House Press Secretary Tony Snow

White House spokesman Tony Snow joined The Factor with the administration's take on the Middle East and the ceasefire. "The force that is going to be put into southern Lebanon," Snow pronounced, "is going to be more capable and more robust than what they've got now. The president's not going to sign on to anything that isn't going to push Hezbollah out of southern Lebanon. It's going to put an end to Hezbollah's ability to fire rockets into Israel, and to hold hostages of everybody in southern Lebanon by putting rockets in their homes." The Factor expressed doubt about the UN and its determination. "It was the UN troops that screwed this up in the first place. But you believe, and the president believes, that the United Nations is finally going to put a force in southern Lebanon that is going to be able to disarm Hezbollah? Does the president really believe that?" Turning to politics, The Factor reminded Snow that leading Democrats are accusing the President of trying to scare Americans. "I'm not aware that the president's ever tried to frighten anybody," Snow responded. "However, I believe some of his opponents have tried to frighten Americans into believing that we're weak, that we cannot win, and that everybody doesn't like us so we ought to walk away so they will like us. That's not a strategy. The President gets up every day and gets assessments of how scary the world really is. You want to get scared? Look at the stuff he looks at every morning."

Returning for a second segment, Tony Snow put forth the administration's latest views on Iran and Iraq. "For years Iran has been building up the capacity to spread terror throughout the region and trying to destabilize it. What's interesting is that the President was the first person to say you know what, this is what's going on. Remember when the president talked about the axis of evil and everybody was sort of stunned? And look what we have - Iraq with Saddam Hussein, North Korea with Kim Jong Il, and Iran with President Ahmadinejad, who is busy running around trying to foment discord within the region. The president understands that maybe people are going to be shaken by what they see on their TV, but he's not going to be shaken because he understands the benefits of winning, which is if you get a democracy in Iraq, you revolutionize that part of the world." The Factor told Snow that Americans are running low on patience. "This war has gone on longer than World War II, and the polls show 60 percent now oppose the action in Iraq. A lot of people have given up hope, they don't think that anybody can control the civil war between the Iraqis."
Impact Segment 
UN OK's Lebanon-Israel ceasefire
Guest: Fox News anchor Shepard Smith

Joining The Factor from the Middle East, Fox News anchor Shepard Smith reported on the ceasefire. "Bill, I think everybody got a little something out of this. In theory, Hezbollah gets off the border, and a peacekeeping force comes in. There are certainly hopes and prayers and aspirations for a long lasting peace, but history books say you don't defeat an ideology with muscle. It can't be done, and I'm not convinced at all that it has been done here."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
The inside security story
Guest: Dr. George Friedman, Stratfor.com

Security expert George Friedman expressed more doubts about the Middle East ceasefire. "There is a huge battle going on in Israel between the military, who really want to reject the cease-fire, and Prime Minister Olmert, who keeps changing his mind. And Hezbollah hasn't been heard from, they haven't accepted this cease-fire. There's something about this whole plan that simply doesn't make sense at this point." Shifting to the foiled airline terror plot, Friedman praised British and US counter-terrorism forces. "I was impressed by the way the British let this run - they didn't shut it down when they had the first couple of people. And we have not had another attack in the United States, and that was not because al Qaeda didn't try. We're getting better. But as this case shows, they keep trying and they're pretty smart."
Personal Story Segment 
Ideologues out of control on terror
Guests: Fox News analyst Ellis Henican & Laura Schwartz,
Democratic strategist

Far left radio host Lynn Samuels actually accused President Bush of conspiring with Tony Blair to manufacture the latest terrorism arrests. Democrat strategist Laura Schwartz worried that Bush hatred could hurt her party in 2008. "We want to win the House in November, and we've got to do it together united as a Democratic Party going forward. If we're pulled to the left just to win the House in November, we're going to have a much harder time in 2008." Fox News analyst Ellis Henican claimed that venom spews from both parties. "You can cite Lynn Samuels, I'll cite the whackadoos on the right hand side. There are right-wing versions and left-wing versions." The Factor agreed, but asserted that left-wingers are more malicious at the moment. "We have a very serious situation where Americans might have been killed on jetliners. Yet the far left continues to say conspiracy - Bush called up Tony Blair and they cooked it. Nobody listens to Lynn Samuels, but it gets out in the blogs."
Back of Book Segment 
Dems disagree on terror protection
Guest: Democratic strategist Jamal Simmons

Finally, The Factor challenged Democrat strategist Jamal Simmons to put forth his party's plan to defeat terrorism. Simmons pointed to strategies laid out by leading Democrats. "Al Gore wants to go after the terrorists wherever they are, not just in Iraq. Hillary Clinton also wants to go after the terrorists where they are. Wherever the terrorists are, that's where we should be focused." The Factor responded with a facetious question. "We know Hezbollah is in southern Lebanon. Does that mean that Al Gore sends American troops into Lebanon to fight side by side with the Israelis? And I'm assuming if Hillary is elected in 2008, we will then invade southern Lebanon and probably a number of other countries where we know terrorists are operating."
