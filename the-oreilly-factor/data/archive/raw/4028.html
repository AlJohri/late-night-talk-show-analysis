Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Portrait of a Killer
Watch more video in the Video Center...
"The Factor will not name the 26-year-old man who killed nine Americans in Oregon yesterday, wounding seven others. But it is instructive to know that yet another mass murderer shot dead by police fits the same pattern as many others. He was a mama's boy, sharing a one bedroom apartment with his mother. He was a loner, he liked guns and Nazi memorabilia, he admired previous killers, despised organized religion, and he had no criminal record that we know of. He attended a school for special needs children and was obviously disturbed. There are millions of people who fit that profile. The Oregon guy is similar to the maniac who shot down nine people in the Charleston church and the killer in Newtown. These maladjusted people want to kill themselves, but instead of just doing that they take innocent people with them because they have no respect for human life and no feelings for anyone but themselves. All the gun control laws in the world will not stop maniacs from committing murder, and as the world becomes more secular, civilized restraints to bad behavior drop. If you believe in nothing, anything goes. It seems the Oregon killer was targeting Christians. That doesn't surprise me as there is an intense battle between good and evil on this earth, and good people are now becoming targets. Summing up, another mentally unstable person commits mass murder, but it was really a suicide play."

The Factor asked former FBI agent and Navy SEAL Jonathan Gilliam to opine on President Obama's call for more gun control. "It's the same argument every time," Gilliam groused, "the same political rhetoric that offers no solution. Barack Obama has no effective plans or policies to combat any of this, it's just extremist talk. Stopping somebody from having guns is going to be a difficult thing, but screening individuals could be effective. Every time something like this happens, people say this guy was 'odd.' Also, 'gun-free' zones are not effective." The Factor reminded Gilliam that radical civil libertarians like the ACLU won't stand for singling out suspicious individuals: "This guy had 13 guns and he was obviously an unstable individual, but you can't just pick somebody off the street for being 'odd.'"
Personal Story 
An Oregon Update
Watch more video in the Video Center...
The Factor welcomed Wanda Mintz, whose nephew Chris is fighting for his life after being shot seven times after he charged the gunman in Oregon. "Chris was in class when he heard gunshots," Wanta Mintz said. "He tried to calm people and came face-to-face with the gunman, who shot Chris three times. Chris told the gunman it was his son's birthday, but he was shot more and tried to crawl away. He was in surgery for seven hours and it's a miracle that he survived." Janet Willis, whose 18-year-old granddaughter Anastasia Boylan was shot in the back, also spoke with The Factor. "They have finished the surgery and they believe it was successful. They were worried because the bullet went down her spine and was lodged in the bottom of her spine. She is pretty tired and pretty distraught, but they believe she'll be okay."
Personal Story 
Bush's 2016 Campaign
Former Florida Governor Jeb Bush, once the Republican front-runner, has fallen precipitously in the polls. He entered the No Spin Zone and assessed his candidacy. "My challenge is to share the Jeb story," he said, "and to talk about my record of accomplishment. I want to give people the sense that I can disrupt Washington the way I disrupted Tallahassee." Bush acknowledged that he can come across as a low-key guy. "I'm not angry, I believe we are on the verge of the greatest time to be alive. But I am passionate about fixing the things that are broken. I took on the teacher unions and government unions in Florida and I won." The Factor reminded Bush that this election cycle is characterized by anger: "The voters want you to be as angry as they are, and you are not! I'm angry because I don't want to see baby parts cut up and sold, I'm angry because a convicted illegal alien can kill a 32-year-old woman, and I'm angry because Putin is kicking President Obama's butt." Bush also took a parting shot at Donald Trump, saying, "I think he's not a serious person and you can't just keep saying that people are idiots."
Impact Segment 
The 2016 Campaign
Dana Perino, co-host of The Five and former aide to George W. Bush, critiqued The Factor's interview with Jeb Bush. "You were trying to say that he is not matching the mood of the country," she began, "but it's hard for someone to not be authentic. Jeb is not a guy who expresses anger well, and if you do it inauthentically you will get called on that. Think about his parents and how he was raised." The Factor reiterated, "He is caught in a time when voters want a champion who will vanquish the people who have caused America's decline."
Talking Points Memo 
Failure Overseas
U.S. foreign policy is generally considered a wreck, most notably when it comes to Russia, Iran, Syria, and Afghanistan. The Factor put forth this exposition: "It is clear that Putin and the mullahs in Iran are about to take over Syria. Iran is sending in ground troops, Russia is providing air power. The plan is to keep the brutal dictator Assad in power, but also to take over the entire country so Russia and Iran can dominate the Middle East. Putin says his air force is in Syria to attack ISIS, but the Russians are bombing moderate Syrians opposed to the Assad regime. Putin is really attacking American interests, but President Obama and Secretary of State Kerry have no cogent response. Also, the Taliban has captured a major city if Afghanistan, Iraq is still dominated by ISIS, and millions in North Africa and the Middle East are trying to get into Europe. All of this is happening because President Obama has not used American power effectively to blunt aggression and terrorism. All over the world America is in retreat."

Strategic analyst Lt. Col. Tony Shaffer entered the No Spin Zone and suggested that U.S. military leaders will begin to openly oppose President Obama. "You're going to see a very clear move towards trying to redefine our strategy," he predicted. "The president and his policies have prevented the military from doing its job. Our foreign policy is not a failure because of military capability or tactics, it is a failure because of the politicization of intelligence and operations. So I think we are at the point where generals will begin to walk away or go to Congress."
