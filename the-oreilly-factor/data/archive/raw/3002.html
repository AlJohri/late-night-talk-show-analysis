Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Occupiers are not going quietly
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guests: Sharon Crowley & Rick Leventhal

"Today marks the second month that the 'Occupy Wall Street' movement has been present, and a poll shows that 63% of Americans disapprove of the occupiers. But these protesters are not going quietly into the night; today in New York City they caused all kinds of mayhem. All over the country the occupiers are costing the taxpayers - you and me - tens of millions of dollars. Talking Points has been dead on in assessing the movement from the beginning. We predicted there would be death; there has been. We predicted crime would flourish; it has. And we predicted the cost to the taxpayer would be enormous; that has come true as well. Now the occupiers are starting to target individual Americans, people like Karl Rove. A group of them tried to shout him down while Rove was giving a speech in Baltimore. Once protesters start to infringe on the rights of individual people, the courts absolutely have to step in. Every American city is giving the occupiers plenty of time and space to get their message across, but that's not good enough for them. They want chaos, so here's another prediction: More people will die."

The Factor was joined by two reporters who covered the "Occupy Wall Street" protests Thursday in New York City. "I was surrounded on all sides by police in riot gear," Rick Leventhal reported. "Just standing in the middle of the intersection surrounded by all these angry protesters and all these heavily armored police officers was a pretty dramatic scene. There were confrontations not just between police and protesters, but also between protesters and people trying to get to work in lower Manhattan." Sharon Crowley added that Thursday's violence marked a new phase in the protests. "I was here the other night when the police evicted the protesters from Zuccotti Park and there was a lot of dismay. Today it has slowly been progressing with sporadic skirmishes and fights. One man was actually punching police officers and seven cops have been taken to the hospital. But the large majority of people are here to make a political statement and are not causing problems."
Week in Review from the Ingraham Angle Segment 
Newt on top in new Fox News poll; Cain drops 9 points.
Guest: Laura Ingraham

A new poll of Republican voters has Newt Gingrich, who was basically written off two months ago, with a slim lead over Mitt Romney. The Factor asked Laura Ingraham whether the former Speaker can actually win the nomination. "I think he can," Ingraham answered, "but it's going to be really difficult. Regardless of how much people want there to be an alternative to Romney, establishment types usually get their way in the Republican Party. And Newt Gingrich has to have a fairly strong organization in states beyond Iowa, New Hampshire and South Carolina. Romney has the operation on the ground but conservatives are not yet comfortable with him. People are desperate for passion, fight, and consistent conservatism."
Impact Segment 
Actress Ellen Barkin trashes Fox News & O'Reilly
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Joy Behar, host of a little-seen program on Headline News, had actress Ellen Barkin on her show this week. Barkin said this: "I'm now on Bill O'Reilly's radar ... he called me a washed-up, d-list, has-been celebrity." The Factor put forth this response: "Here's the problem with Ms. Barkin's statement - it's a flat-out lie. I have never mentioned Ellen Barkin on this broadcast and I would never call her names. We know Ms. Behar and what she does, and today HLN announced she will no longer be doing it on that network. Her show is finished at the end of this year. But Headline News and CNN, the parent company, have an obligation not to allow blatant lies on their air. So we have asked HLN to issue a written apology; we have also asked Ms. Barkin and Ms. Behar to do the same. The bigger issue is the tremendous decline of the American media. It's now totally acceptable in many precincts to print or broadcast flat-out falsehoods. The media is now in business to destroy, not to enlighten!"
Culture Warriors Segment 
'Tis the Season: War on Christmas begins again?
Guests: Margaret Hoover & Gretchen Carlson

Culture Warriors Gretchen Carlson and Margaret Hoover looked ahead to the Christmas season and whether the secular assault on the holiday will resume. "I think the war on Christmas is largely receding," Hoover declared. "95% of ordinary Americans celebrate Christmas and 51% say they do so with strong religion, so you can claim a Factor victory." But Carlson provided a stark reminder that the battle requires eternal vigilance. "Just this week Santa was thrown out of a cancer center in South Carolina. He was a volunteer Santa who came to give good cheer to chemotherapy patients, and the center threw him out because they get state funds and they though there might be a conflict of interest. But the public won because in a newspaper response 98% of the people said this was the wrong thing to do, and within two days Santa was reinstated." The Factor pointed out that "no other media people fought the war, and in fact most people in the liberal media attacked me for sticking up for Christmas."
Kelly File Segment 
Federal judge rules in favor of school that banned American flag shirts
Guest: Megyn Kelly

Back in May of 2010, when a California high school banned students from wearing the American flag on Cinco de Mayo, Fox News anchor and attorney Megyn Kelly predicted that the school would be slapped down in the courts. But in fact, a federal judge ruled last week that the school was within its rights, so The Factor invited Kelly to dine on a heaping helping of crow. "The ruling said the school had the right to tell the Caucasian students to go home," Kelly conceded. "The school had to show that there was the likelihood of 'substantial disruption' by the kids wearing the flag. But this was viewpoint discrimination because the vice-principal only sent home the kids wearing the American flag t-shirts and not the kids wearing the Mexican colors. This was a boneheaded decision!" The Factor defended the judge and did some boasting in the process: "The judge made the right ruling. I taught in a school that was similar to this one and I know how explosive these things can get. The bottom line is that the non-lawyer O'Reilly, was correct against the board certified lawyer Kelly."
Back of Book Segment 
News Quiz: The Thanksgiving Edition
Guests: Martha MacCallum & Steve Doocy

Steve Doocy and Martha MacCallum, each looking to kick the stuffing out of the other, entered the No Spin Zone for another News Quiz. Among the questions: "What cartoon character's balloon has appeared in the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade most often?" ... "What year did the first Thanksgiving take place?" ... "What state leads the nation in turkey production?" After being squashed by MacCallum, Doocy stormed off the set in a mood most fowl.
