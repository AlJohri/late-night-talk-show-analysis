Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Assessing the new Pope
Guests: Fox News analyst Father Jonathan Morris & Father James Martin, America Magazine

"Joseph Cardinal Ratzinger is now Pope Benedict XVI. The new Pope has not been a big fan of the US in the past. He opined that the United Nations was the only authority that could legitimately remove Saddam Hussein, he saw the priest pedophilia scandal as being driven by the American press, and he generally feels that the US embraces a culture of death. Now why should you care if you're not Catholic? Well, unfortunately we Americans are all targets and we're not getting much help throughout the world. The Holy See has tremendous influence in the world and if it would actively condemn al Qaeda and other terror groups that could only help the USA. If Pope Benedict XVI were to take an active interest in defeating global terrorism the world would be a much safer place. Sadly, I don't see this happening. I believe the new pope will continue the polices of John Paul II and the Catholic Church will stand pat. Talking Points expects the American media to go after the new pope because he's conservative and that will confirm his opinion that we're all a bunch of godless secularists over here. Remember, it is the American Civil Liberties Union which is behind abortion on demand, euthanasia, and - perhaps coming soon - infanticide for impaired babies. What do you think the Vatican thinks when it reads about the American Civil Liberties Union opposing parental consent for minors getting abortions? How about all those ACLU attacks on Christmas? America, of course, is much more than the ACLU, but our image is being defined by the media, much of which is in synch with that radical group. I hope and pray that the new pope will look beyond that and embrace the USA, which remains the world's best hope for freedom."

Transcript/Video: FoxNews.com

The Factor was joined by two priests with differing opinions of the cardinals' choice. First, Fox News analyst Father Jonathan Morris praised Cardinal Ratzinger as the ideal man for the job. "What the Church needs is a man of God, and from my experience with Cardinal Ratzinger he is a man of God. He's going to be repeating that there are certain things about the Catholic Church that if we change them it will no longer be the Catholic Church." But Jesuit priest Jim Martin expressed some reservations about Pope Benedict XVI. "I don't know if he's the person I would have picked," said Father Martin. "I'm surprised they didn't pick someone who is just as conservative theologically, but who is less contentious. He's kind of a lightning rod in the church." The Factor said church leaders may have been sending a message with their selection. "I think the cardinals are angry about what's happening in Western Europe and America, where people are leaving the spiritual and moving to the secular. I think the cardinals are so furious that they want a guy like Ratzinger who is going to say no."
Impact Segment 
Moussaoui pleading guilty
Guest: Terrorism expert & author Steve Emerson

According to reports, accused 9/11 conspirator Zacarias Moussaoui will admit to his role in the terror attacks. "He's going to plead guilty to conspiring to kill Americans," explained terror expert Steve Emerson. "I have no doubt that he was directly involved in the orchestration of the plot." Emerson also commented on the continuing danger posed by America's porous borders. "The best type of security is at the borders, keeping the bad people out. But in the conservative movement there are pro-business types who want the immigrants and the labor. And on the left you have the Democratic Party, which doesn't want to enforce the laws." The Factor again urged the administration to protect Americans by cracking down on illegal immigration. "If there's another terror attack and it's traced back to the Mexican border, Bush goes down as one of the worst presidents of all time. You would think that out of self-interest he would say I'm going to take my last three years and secure these borders. But he won't."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Criminal justice system breakdown in FL
Guest: Fox News chief judicial analyst Judge Andrew Napolitano

As The Factor has reported, some horrific crimes have been committed in Florida by criminals who were free after judges set low bail. Most recently, Judge Walter Heinrich released David Onstott on cash bond of just $100 after he failed to register as a sex offender. Onstott, who had a long history of violent offenses, then allegedly murdered 13-year old Sarah Lunde. Fox News legal analyst Andrew Napolitano said Judge Heinrich made a terrible error. "If a suspect's background shows violence, particularly sexual violence, you do not give him a bail he can meet. You intentionally keep him incarcerated. This was an easy decision because Onstott had a long history of violence. Hindsight is 20/20, but this judge was not doing his job."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Making law vs. interpreting law
Guest: Cass Sunstein, Chicago Law School

There is a raging battle over the judiciary, with conservatives clamoring for judges who interpret the Constitution rather than make new laws. On the other side, some liberal legal analysts have called for a "second Bill of Rights" that would guarantee all citizens certain economic protections. Law professor Cass Sunstein is one of that proposition's strongest advocates. "What I'd like to do is recover the vision of Franklin Delano Roosevelt," Sunstein told The Factor, "who felt there was a right to an education, the right to protection from a monopoly, the right to protection in the event of unemployment and disability. The question is what are our basic national commitments. Every citizen should share certain commitments and respect for one another."
Back of Book Segment 
The upcoming media assault on the Pope?
Guest: Fox News contributor Liz Trotta

Because Cardinal Ratzinger is conservative on issues such as gay rights and women priests, he may be a target for many in the mainstream media. Fox News contributor Liz Trotta said the attacks have already begun. "I sensed watching all three networks that there was almost a palpable sense of disappointment when Ratzinger's name came out. Some stations are interviewing only people who don't like the new pope. The press has brought up his German background and the fact that he was a member of the Hitler Youth Movement. I suspect we'll see more of that, and we'll see the homosexuality refrain from ex-priests." The Factor argued that the new pope will be disliked by many in the media because of one issue. "Abortion is so fanatically embraced by the liberal media in the United States, and we know Pope Benedict will be strongly anti-abortion. That is the red flag."
