Talking Points Memo 
Attacking Pope Benedict XVI
"Pope Benedict has begun his six day visit to America and we hope his message of peace, love and compassion will resonate. But the rise of secularism in America has made it very difficult for any religious leader to spread his or her message. Benedict is facing a semi-hostile press, millions of Americans still angry over the priest pedophilia scandal, and in some quarters there is a is a shocking element of disrespect toward Pope Benedict. Comedian Bill Maher accused the Catholic Church of abusing 'thousands all over the world' and compared it to the polygamist cult in Texas. Can you imagine if Maher had castigated a Jewish or Muslim leader like that, or if he had branded a minority group with that description? The American media would slaughter him. But it is open season on Christians in America. You can demean, you can defame them all they want, and the media will laugh along with you. No doubt Pope Benedict is praying for those people and even for Bill Maher. That's what the Pope does, even when he's being castigated."

Top Story 
Hillary to PA: Obama is an elitist
The Clinton campaign has produced a TV ad criticizing Barack Obama for his comments about "bitter" Americans who "cling to religion." The Factor was joined by former Speaker of the House Newt Gingrich, who analyzed Obama's words. "What was revealing about his comments," Gingrich said, "was that he clearly felt that if you didn't vote for liberals, there had to be something psychologically wrong with you. This hurts him because, combined with Reverend Wright and having gone to the Million Man March, there are pieces coming out that make him more and more left wing." Gingrich also denounced Bill Maher for defaming the Catholic Church. "I think he should be suspended. If he had said that as an anti-gay comment, he'd be off television. Why is it tolerated in the elite media to be viciously anti-Christian and get away with it?" The Factor had a ready answer to that hypothetical question: "There's no leadership in the American Catholic Church, no cardinal to stand up and say that's wrong. In order to mobilize Christians there has to be leadership."

Impact Segment 
Rev. Jeremiah Wright: Not what he seems?
Jeremiah Wright, who has disparaged America's middle class lifestyle, is about to move into a 10,000 square foot house next to a golf course. Reporter Amanda Wright elaborated on Wright's heavenly new abode. "The house will include an elevator, four bedrooms, and room for a theater or swimming pool. It's in a predominantly white community where only about 2% of the population is black, which seems strange considering the black liberation theology he has preached over the years." The Factor questioned Wright's own belief in his Afrocentric preaching. "Some people have said that he became more radical because the church he joined was not doing well and he needed to attract people from the south side of Chicago. He changed it into a politically themed church and attendance skyrocketed."

'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Living large... and owing back taxes
Some celebrities, among them Dionne Warwick, Nicholas Cage and O.J. Simpson, continue to live well despite owing millions in back taxes. The Factor asked FNC legal aces Megyn Kelly and Lis Wiehl how they get away with it. "It's just blatantly irresponsible," Kelly responded. "They don't want to pay and they're getting away with it in the same way you or I would get away with it for a little while if we tried it. The IRS is understaffed and underfunded and you only get audited by chance." Wiehl explained the difficulty in catching tax cheats. "I was at the other end of this, I prosecuted tax evasion cases and I didn't get those cases until they were in the system for about seven years. They first have to go through civil assessments and liens, and celebrities can hire the big lawyers and accountants." The Factor joked that "Dionne Warwick is psychic, so she should have known the IRS was going to look at her."

Factor Follow Up Segment 
Kids taking explicit pictures of other kids
There is a disturbing trend across America - young children taking explicit photos of other kids and sending them via cell phone. The Factor welcomed Ohio detective Brian Marvin, who has investigated the phenomenon. "Most of these photos," Marvin said, "wind up on line and become part of a pedophile's collection. But when the kids are caught, they're usually angry with me for confronting them, or have an amazing lack of concern for what they've just done. And the parents don't seem to know what their children are doing." The Factor stressed the danger of the photos. "These aren't innocent pictures, these are hard-core sex acts. This is child pornography and it's happening in the heartland of America."

Miller Time Segment 
Miller on Colbert and Larry King
Dennis Miller began with the accusations of "elitism" being leveled at Barack Obama. "I think this," Miller suggested, "has a lot more sticking power than the Reverend Wright thing. This is his voice, his own tone, and he was hobnobbing with the swells in the Bay Area, talking about what the 'regular people' need." Miller turned to Pope Benedict and his malicious critics. "People can bash Catholics because they know Catholics won't kill them. I have my own qualms about the Catholic Church, but I will say this - for the Pope to say he is 'deeply ashamed' of the pedophilia, that's to the point and I think it healed some wounds." Finally, Miller remarked on Stephen Colbert's appearance on Larry King's show, where he joked that King once dated Claudette Colbert. "King looked a little ruffled. Maybe Colbert hit close to home, maybe Larry did date her way back in those halcyon pre-Viagra days when the only libidinal accentuation a man could have was to wear his suspenders way, way too tight." The Factor and many viewers are still laboring to understand that last sentence.
Back of Book Segment 
The Great American Culture Quiz
Steve Doocy and Martha MacCallum met again in the Great American Culture Quiz that included these questions: "Actress Faye Dunaway was married to the lead singer of which rock group?" ... "In 1968 the U.S. ship Pueblo was captured by which country?" ... "What was Elvis' final number one sing?" After being trounced last week, Doocy came back to eke out a single point victory. If you want to test your knowledge against the duo, the entire GACQ is posted here on BillOReilly.com under "Fun Stuff."
