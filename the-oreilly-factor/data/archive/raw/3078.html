Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Obama driving the country toward a 'fiscal cliff'?
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guests: Democratic fundraiser Robert Zimmerman & Tamara Draut, VP at Demos

"The major issue of the presidential campaign in 2012 is not jobs, and it's not Iran or the jihadists. The most important issue facing Americans is the chaos in government spending.

"Listen to this -- Fannie Mae, the government-sponsored agency that provides housing loans to lower income Americans, is now asking for $4.6 billion more in federal aid. At this point, Fannie Mae has cost the American taxpayer $100 billion, yet the agency wants more.

"And then there's another looming green disaster. You already know about Solyndra, which cost the American taxpayer about $500 million, but have you heard of "Abound Solar" based in Colorado?

"Nearly two years ago, President Obama said this: 'Today, I'm announcing that the Department of Energy is awarding nearly $2 billion in conditional commitments to two solar companies...The second company is Abound Solar Manufacturing, which will manufacture advanced solar panels at two new plants, creating more than 2,000 construction jobs and 1,500 permanent jobs.'

"Well those permanent jobs weren't so permanent. This week Abound Solar announced it's laying off about half its work force, nearly 200 employees. Another fiscal disaster."

Following Talking Points, Mr. Zimmerman admitted he'd like to see Fannie and Freddie shut down, but with so many Americans facing foreclosure right now, he thought that would be a real fiasco. He also warned that we can't just walk away from the solar industry, and pointed out that our competition overseas is being funded by their governments so we have to do it too.

But the Factor pressed his guests to explain why our private sector can't take the lead when it comes to solar power. Tamara explained that the government loans indicate to the private sector that this will be a viable market, and then she cautioned that you can't say solar is not working out based on these two companies.

The Factor quibbled with the two liberals over whether there are any green success stories, and concluded by saying he's tired of the terrible mismanagement of taxpayer money.
Week in Review from the Ingraham Angle Segment 
Trouble for yet another green company that got government money
Guest: Laura Ingraham

In responding to the top story, the Factor pointed out that all he wants is government efficiency. Laura: "The idea that we're going to compete with China [on solar energy] by throwing government money at this is obscenely ridiculous."

The Factor worried that the GOP candidates are focusing too much on unemployment, while he believes the mismanagement of the American treasury should be the key campaign issue. Laura agreed that this is the preeminent situation facing all of us, despite ideology.

On the race for the White House, Laura acknowledged that going into Super Tuesday, Mitt Romney has the wind at his back.
Personal Story Segment 
Santorum's Super Tuesday strategy
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guest: Rick Santorum

According to the latest Rasmussen poll, Romney now leads Rick Santorum 40% to 24%. Santorum disclosed that his campaign received a funding boost in February from grassroots donors, as opposed to the good ol' boys he insists finance the Romney campaign.

The Factor asked Sen. Santorum to assess President Obama's religious beliefs vis a vis the birth control issue. Santorum claimed Obama's religion is not his concern; he's only interested in how the President is trying to enact policy that would insult the faith of millions of American Christians. When pressed, Santorum stated he thinks Barack Obama the man is a good husband and father, but a hard-core ideologue who wants to impose his views on the masses.
"Did You See That?" Segment 
Lindsay Lohan says she's not like Whitney Houston
Guest: Juliet Huddy

Twenty-two Americans vacationing on a Carnival cruise line were robbed at gunpoint when they disembarked in Puerto Vallarta. The Factor has been reporting on the danger of visiting Mexico, even while some people maintain the tourist areas are perfectly safe.

Juliet spoke to a victim, who said they haven't heard a peep from Carnival. She described an excursion that involved a hike into the jungle, where a guide took them off the beaten path. An armed man showed up out of nowhere, demanding everyone - except the tour guide - empty their wallets. This bit of information led the Factor to conclude that this was a setup.

Changing gears - Matt Lauer asked the troubled star Lindsay Lohan if Whitney Houston's death registered with her on a personal level. Ms. Lohan didn't want to go there. Juliet contended that celebrities are surrounded by sycophants, and when you're in the throes of addiction, you can't tell the difference between right and wrong. The Factor renewed his calls for public pressure on famous people who are suffering from addiction.
Kelly File Segment 
Girl drops out of high school and moves in with her former teacher
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Guests: Megyn Kelly

In Modesto, California, 41-year-old James Hooker, who taught computer classes, has quit his job, left his wife and children and moved in with an 18-year-old former student. The couple appeared on "Good Morning America" denying the sexual relationship started before she turned 18 and claiming they are sharing a very normal life together.

On the legal front, Megyn said you can't realistically make a statutory rape charge against him if the girl was truly 18 when the relationship started. But she revealed that there are thousands of text messages between them that started before the girl turned 18. Outraged, Megyn described this girl as brainwashed by a man who was in a position of power over her. She hoped the authorities in California figure out a way to charge the former teacher with endangering the welfare of a minor.

The 17-year-old shooter who allegedly murdered three students in Ohio earlier this week has been charged as a juvenile. Ms. Kelly clarified that this won't stick, it's just procedure, and the perp will eventually be charged as an adult.

The assistant of the late Dr. George Tiller, the late-term abortionist who was assassinated in Kansas, has had her license revoked. The judge ruled she was merely rubber stamping everything. The Factor reminded viewers that he predicted this would happen.
Back of Book Segment 
News Quiz: The Political Extremist Edition
Guests: Martha MacCallum & Steve Doocy

Both contestants missed the first question, incorrectly identifying Greece as the country where Hitler's Nazi symbol originated. The correct answer was India.

The quiz kids redeemed themselves, effortlessly answering that "Revolution" was the Beatles song with lyrics critical of the followers of Chairman Mao.

On the final question, Martha prevailed and broke the tie by accurately answering that Che Guevara was trained as a doctor before he became a militant in the Cuban Revolution.
