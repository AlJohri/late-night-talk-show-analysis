Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
The trashing of Sarah Palin
"Last night Fox News correspondent Carl Cameron broke the story of some McCain campaign people trashing Sarah Palin. I told Carl I thought some of the stuff was trivial, and I never respect people who bad-mouth others while refusing to give their names. Some Factor viewers didn't like the report at all, but this is a news program and McCain people dumping on the vice presidential candidate is big news. If The Factor were to ignore news like that, we couldn't portray ourselves as a news-based opinion program, we'd be an entertainment show. I'm skeptical of the McCain people because dealing with them during the campaign was chaotic. I have offered Sarah Palin thirty minutes on The Factor on Monday to say whatever she wants to say. I hope the governor will accept our invitation. During the campaign I consistently defended Governor Palin against unfair attacks, but it is not my job to suppress the news. Once any news operation decides to suppress stories for ideological reasons, it's done."

The Factor asked Fox News analyst Laura Ingraham for her assessment of the McCain staffers who are anonymously disparaging Sarah Palin. "One word," Ingraham declared, "cowards! These people are making these claims without attribution, I find it disgusting and the person should step forward. I spent a lot of time with Sarah Palin, and this doesn't sound like her. She is the only person who has the ability to energize the electorate from coast to coast." The Factor agreed that skepticism is warranted. "When Sarah Palin was announced as John McCain's running mate the media sent hundreds of people to Alaska. They were looking for scandal and stupidity and greed and they came up with nothing. But if the governor has national aspirations, she needs to come on Monday night and explain what happened."

Culture Warriors Segment 
Proposition 8 passes in California
Even though Barack Obama carried California handily, state voters passed an initiative banning same-sex marriage, with the vast majority of blacks opposing gay marriage. The Factor asked Culture Warriors Margaret Hoover and Monica Crowley to analyze the results. "Black Americans believe in traditional marriage," Hoover said, "and they do not believe in gay marriage. But the youth vote was overwhelmingly in favor of gay marriage, my generation does not think that people should be treated differently." Crowley portrayed the vote as a victory for conservatism. "The Hollywood community poured millions of dollars into this, but this country remains center-right. Even though you had Democratic victories in the White House and Senate, when it comes to cultural issues this country remains conservative." When Hoover pointed out that gay marriage remains legal in Massachusetts, The Factor had this response: "That was imposed by judges, and they won't let the people vote in Massachusetts. This was fascism."

Impact Segment 
Shep Smith's smackdown of Ralph Nader
During an interview on election day, Ralph Nader questioned whether Barack Obama will be "Uncle Sam or Uncle Tom." The Factor was joined by FNC anchor Shep Smith, who confronted Nader about his racially inflammatory comment. "Ralph Nader may have saved many lives decades ago," Smith told The Factor, "but now he chose to use a phrase that seemed to be misplaced on the day the first African American president was elected. I asked him if he'd like to change that phrase, but he didn't. He was irrelevant in this election." The Factor agreed that "the 'Uncle Tom' comment hurt people and was totally inappropriate."

Factor Follow Up Segment 
Obama, The View and the White House
Pollster Frank Luntz once again wired up a group of voters and concluded that two of Barack Obama's comments may have won him the election. The first came during a Democratic primary debate, when Obama declared the election is "not about gender or religion or race ... it's about the past versus the future." Luntz's analysis: "This transcended ideology and politics and it took the wind out of Hillary Clinton's sails. He looked like a forward-looking person." Luntz also singled out Obama's mention of "affordable, accessible health care" at the Democratic convention in Denver. "He did so well on the issue of health care that it erased any doubts about him." Finally, Luntz had his voters watch The Factor's recent comments about excessive TV watching during an appearance on The View. "You talked about personal responsibility," Luntz reported, "and most Americans want parents to take responsibility for raising their kids. They think that television is often a negative, and you put it right on the line."
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Investigating Joe the Plumber... but why?
As reported previously, Ohio official Helen Jones-Kelley examined the personal files of Joe "the plumber" Wurzelbacher, looking for damning information on the McCain supporter. The Factor asked attorneys Lis Wiehl and Megyn Kelly whether Jones-Kelley broke any laws. "We have reports," Kelly said, "that officials in Ohio also checked his DMV records and perhaps other records. It's not that it's illegal, but it's inappropriate and unethical. You must have some sort of reason to look into somebody's records. Everyone on the left wanted dirt on Joe the Plumber." But Wiehl suggested that a crime may have been committed. "I do think there is something potentially illegal here. Her taking the time to look into this is a misuse of public funds, and there is an investigation." The Factor posed a rhetorical question: "Where's the ACLU on this, aren't they privacy people? Can you imagine if a Republican had done this to a Democrat?"

Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Oprah on the Obama win
Oprah Winfrey celebrated Barack Obama's victory on her talk show, joking that she would have required hospitalization had Obama lost. The Factor provided this reality check: "Ms. Winfrey's an entertainer, it's her program and she has earned her right to say whatever she wants." Meanwhile, director Spike Lee accused the Republican Party, saying "their base is totally white." The Factor's check: "Why make a racial thing out of this, Mr. Lee. John McCain and the Republicans didn't, but you consistently paint everything in black and white terms." Turning to "journalism," MSNBC's Chris Matthews declared that it is his job "to make this new presidency work." The Factor's check: "At least Mr. Matthews is honest, unlike some of his cohorts."
