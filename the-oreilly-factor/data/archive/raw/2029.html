Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Chaos surrounds Gov. Sarah Palin
"John McCain had a good first 48 hours after announcing that Governor Sarah Palin would be his running mate. But the announcement that Governor Palin's 17-year-old daughter is pregnant took much of the joy out of Mudville, and the left-wing press gleefully swooped in. For the fair-minded media, this is a tough one. The public has a right to know about Governor Palin's life, but Americans are very protective of families, so the questions have to be fair. But 'fair' is a word that ideological press people will never understand. Here's an example: Writing in the Chicago Sun-Times, race-baiting columnist Mary Mitchell wrote that 'conservatives like Palin have been hard on young, unwed, pregnant women in urban areas.' That is brutally unfair. Critics of teen pregnancies have a perfect right to put forth that when children give birth to children many bad things can happen. Millions of families are dealing with teen pregnancy, and as long as society doesn't have to support the mother, father or baby, it is a personal matter. It's true that some Americans will judge Governor Palin and her family, and she will have a hard time running for vice president if there is much more chaos. For the sake of her and her family, we hope things calm down. This country needs a vibrant policy debate, not a soap opera."

The Factor asked political strategist Karl Rove about the controversy surrounding Sarah Palin. "I'm surprised it's taken the tone that it has," Rove said. "Most Americans are saying that someone in our family or in our friendship circle has had to go through this. Did we see these same kind of commentaries when John Edwards entered the presidential contest when his wife was suffering from cancer? Or when Nancy Pelosi, a mother of five, entered politics?" Rove also took some jabs at Democratic VP candidate Joe Biden. "This guy is overrated when it comes to foreign policy. He has a reputation as a foreign policy expert and I don't see it. He suggested that the United States should unilaterally go in and divide Iraq into Kurdish, Sunni, and Shi'a." Returning to the Palin situation, The Factor questioned the media's motives. "They're trying to wipe out Sarah Palin early so Barack Obama can win the White House."

For a Democratic perspective on the Palin issue, The Factor turned to former Clinton spokesman Howard Wolfson "I don't think that the number of children a candidate has should be an issue," Wolfson said. "Questions about her qualifications and record are appropriate, but questions about her children should be out of bounds." Wolfson actually defended the DailyKos web site, which is trying to destroy Palin. "I still maintain that the DailyKos is a site where a lot of people come and add their voices and participate in the process. There is a constituency in our Democratic Party that is on the blogs and this is an important community." When Wolfson said he would again advise a Democratic candidate to attend the DailyKos convention, The Factor was shocked: "You would go back and legitimize this? I'm stunned! It would be like any of these Republicans going into David Duke's convention. It's that level of hatred."
Impact Segment 
Palin controversy overshadows RNC speakers
The Factor welcomed pollster Frank Luntz, who has been interviewing independent voters about Sarah Palin. "It is fascinating," Luntz began, "to watch them react as they learn more about her. Voters see that she was mayor and governor and they don't think that holds a candle to Joe Biden. Until they hear about her stopping the 'bridge to nowhere,' challenging a sitting governor and defeating him, and having an 80% approval rating. And when they hear she's a mother of five it's a very powerful combination." The Factor predicted that attacks on Palin may backfire. "No one wants to see a nice woman, a mother of five, a civil servant who has challenged the establishment ripped to shreds. The crazy left-wing media that wants to elect Barack Obama is hurting him by attacking Palin."
Personal Story Segment 
Protesters running amok in Minneapolis-St. Paul
While Republicans meet in St. Paul, some protestors have been vandalizing buildings and defying police. Minnesota Governor Tim Pawlenty told The Factor that many of the troublemakers are from out of town. "There is a sub-group of goofballs," Pawlenty said. "These aren't necessarily Minnesotans, these anarchists and nihilists come in from other states. We've had 285 people arrested, they will be prosecuted and will go to jail or pay a fine if convicted." Pawlenty also dismissed the possibility that Minnesotans will elect Al "Stuart Smalley" Franken to the Senate. "He's running against Norm Coleman, a great senator who is well financed and deserves re-election. Norm will win."
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
A double-standard for women in politics?
FNC legal experts Lis Wiehl and Megyn Kelly scrutinized the media treatment of Sarah Palin. "Of course there's a double standard," Kelly argued. "This debate about whether being the mother of five disqualifies her - if she's a good mother, that's her and her family's business. Who has asked whether Barack Obama will be a good father to his 7- and 10-year-olds if he gets to be president? And there are plenty of women piling on, which makes it so disgusting." Wiehl concurred that the double standard is a glaring one. "We would never have this discussion about 'juggling' family and work if we were talking about a guy, and that's why women don't get a break. If they're aggressive, then they're 'shrill.' And if they're soft and feminine, then they can't take on dictators. We can't win." The Factor reiterated that many in the media are driven not by sexism, but by ideology: "They're going after Palin to marginalize her and make Barack Obama president."
Back of Book Segment 
Newt Gingrich on the RNC
The Factor welcomed former House Speaker Newt Gingrich, who scrutinized the presidential race. "Two or three weeks from now," Gingrich predicted, "we're going to be in one of two worlds. Either the elite media will have succeeded in crippling Sarah Palin or she'll be standing firm. If it's the former, I think Senator Obama has a good chance of winning; if it's the latter, the Democrats are in for a long and painful October." Gingrich also analyzed the harsh press treatment of Governor Palin. "The media has figured out that she is the greatest threat since Clarence Thomas, because a conservative professional woman is a mortal threat to the left's ability to define and legitimize American politics. They are going to come after her with everything they have." The Factor questioned whether the bias is intentional or subconscious. "There are editorial meetings at these newspapers and I have to wonder if it is as overt as you're saying. But it would almost have to be to have three front page anti-Palin stories in the New York Times. You'd have to orchestrate something like that."
