Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Supreme Court & terror tribunals
Guests: Fox News chief judicial analyst Judge Andrew Napolitano & Fox News correspondent Megyn Kendall

"The Supreme Court ruled 5-3 that President Bush can not try prisoners at Guantanamo Bay using the military tribunal system. Four liberal justices plus Justice Kennedy said the President has overstepped his authority and must get Congressional approval for military tribunals. So what does this mean to you? The President could seek Congressional approval to use tribunals, and I hope he does. But either way Gitmo stays open - the court says detainees can pretty much be held there indefinitely. The disturbing thing about the ruling is that it demonstrates how America is divided on the terror war. The left believes the administration is the enemy, and doesn't trust it to wage a legal war. The right believes the Bush administration's policies have prevented attacks on American soil and is willing to give the President the benefit of the doubt. All the polls say most Americans trust the President more than any Democrat to protect them from terror. If Mr. Bush wants military justice at Gitmo, the Republican-controlled House and Senate would most likely okay it. All he has to do is ask."

Fox News Video: FoxNews.com

Fox News correspondent Megyn Kendall elaborated on Thursday's ruling. "What we didn't anticipate was the court finding that Al Qaeda is protected by the Geneva Conventions. That part of the ruling could change the way the US does business overseas." FNC's Judge Andrew Napolitano explained that the administration is now barred from using secret evidence, which may mean some Guantanamo prisoners will be released. "The President has to decide whether to use classified evidence against the detainees or free them. My prediction is that the administration will not want to declassify this evidence, therefore it is going to be forced to let some of them go. That doesn't mean they're going to walk the streets - they'll go to prison in Afghanistan where they were arrested." The Factor concluded that Thursday's ruling is a setback in the terror war. "Giving terrorists Geneva Convention protections is pretty shocking. The Supreme Court has constrained the war on terror and handcuffed the Commander in Chief."
Impact Segment 
Supreme Court playing politics?
Guest: Jonathan Turley, Geroge Washington University

Some observers accuse the Supreme Court of taking political sides in the war on terror, but professor Jonathan Turley defended the judges and Thursday's decision. "We are hopefully going to develop proceedings that give the defense a few more rights than they currently have, but we will try these individuals. We are viewed as a rogue nation, and we can not fight for the rule of law when we are viewed as violating the rule of law." The Factor reiterated that this unprecedented war requires special rules. "What is the right way to try people who are trying to blow up babies and kill Americans? You're making it almost impossible for the United States government to bring these people to justice."
Personal Story Segment 
Exclusive with John Kerry
Guest: Senator John Kerry

For the first time since he lost the 2004 election, John Kerry entered the No Spin Zone for an exclusive interview. Senator Kerry began with the classified information that was leaked to the New York Times and other papers. "I hate leakers, I hate leaks, and they ought to be prosecuted. But this administration has a record of breaking the law with respect to eavesdropping, and there is secrecy embedded in the administration." Kerry also pronounced that Iran should never be allowed to have a nuclear weapon, and implied that US military action may be necessary as a last resort. "You never take any option off the table. But if you talk to any of the military planners here in Washington or the smartest security people, everyone will tell you what a risky and dangerous option that ultimately is."

Senator Kerry also stressed the need for border security. "The first step is to tighten our borders and enforce the law with respect to illegal employment. You have to increase the number of Border Agents." The Factor brought up a related subject, the recent Senate vote that would have endorsed English as the national language. "I was shocked, Senator, when you voted against recognizing English as the national language." "The way that amendment was phrased," Kerry explained, "was to purposefully try to stick it to people, and I thought that was an inappropriate use of the Senate floor." Finally, Senator Kerry claimed the US is not energy independent largely because oil companies wield too much power. "They have fought to protect their business interests with campaign money. The entire political system is too dependent on campaign contributions, and as a result our security has been put at risk." The Factor criticized both parties for not making ethanol a priority long ago. "If it is better for every American not to be energy independent, and in 30 years Congress could not make it happen, you're telling me that the Congress has sold us out, and so have four presidents. The bottom line is that you guys couldn't get it done."
Thursdays with Geraldo Segment 
Top stories with Geraldo
Guest: Geraldo Rivera

Geraldo Rivera weighed in on some stories in the news, beginning with the feud that erupted on ABC's "The View." Co-host Star Jones resigned on air, surprising executive producer Barbara Walters and her colleagues. "The story is the self-destruction of Star Jones," Rivera said. "Allegedly Star and her husband got free perks for their wedding, in exchange for surreptitious mentions of products on 'The View.' Then she has this dramatic weight loss and tells the audience it's exercise, when in fact it was a gastric bypass. Barbara Walters has been an institution for decades, and she has been embarrassed by this. That's why so many millions of people are intrigued." Rivera also commented on the ordeal of radio host Rush Limbaugh. "What they did in Palm Beach to Rush Limbaugh was the cheapest of cheap shots. They find Viagra when he's coming back from the Dominican Republic, and what do they do? They call the Associated Press and humiliate the guy." The Factor stressed that Limbaugh was selectively targeted. "The authorities in Palm Beach were trying to set him up, and it's an abuse of power by authorities trying to ruin a man's life. This is dirty."
Back of Book Segment 
Superman opens strong
Guest: Jim Hambrick, Super Museum

The new Superman movie had an opening day box office that would impress the Man of Steel himself. Jim Hambrick, curator of the Super Museum, explained Superman's enduring allure. "Everyone needs a hero, and it's part of American popular culture. Superman came out during the Depression when we really needed a boost in morale, and he has always been there." Hambrick praised the new movie as first-rate. "I went in skeptical, but it's an excellent film. Trust me, I wouldn't say that if I didn't believe it."
