Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Demonizing questioners of global warming
"Denmark is hosting the largest 'greeniac' blowout the world has ever seen. The Obama administration has sent scores of high-level delegates to convince the world that we've made a u-turn from the troglodyte days of the Bush administration, when climate change 'denial' was all the rage. Yet the biggest deniers are the ones who refuse to confront the truth about dishonest scientific practices. Purloined emails show that top scientists conspired to falsify climate data because they saw global temperatures were actually declining. This is one of the biggest scandals in modern science, yet everyone from Al Gore to Tom Friedman has dismissed the emails as irrelevant. Gore claimed the emails were ten years old; in fact, one of the most incriminating emails was from October and others were from the last few years. This is the same Al Gore who asserted that the earth's core temperature is 'several million degrees.' Well, in fact, it's several thousand degrees. If Gore can be wrong about these basic facts, why should anyone trust him about climate change? Enter Sarah Palin. In an op-ed piece, she tackled the 'Climategate' issue and said we should be wary about what comes out of this politicized conference. Left-wingers and global warming fanatics not only attacked Palin personally, but they trashed the Washington Post for publishing her. Since when is it anti-science to ask questions? The people most guilty of willful ignorance in this debate are those who ignore evidence of a blatant scientific coverup as they shout down anyone who disagrees."

Laura welcomed environmentalist Tyson Slocum, who disputed the thrust of her Talking Points Memo. "This is not about Al Gore or Sarah Palin," Slocum said, "this is about peer-reviewed science, and a handful of stolen emails does not undermine science. We can always trot out a handful of scientists who claim global warming is not real, but they are not undergoing the peer-reviewed process. It is clearly established that burning fossil fuels like coal and oil has released historic levels of greenhouse gases that are leading to climate change." Laura countered that the purloined emails are critically important. "Scientists at the core of the research are dumping emails and they're concerned that temperatures are not increasing. When we're about to change our economy from the top down, it's important for us to have all the information."
Culture War Segment 
Canadian writer pushes for one-child plan
Laura was next joined by Canadian journalist Diane Francis, who has praised China's one-child-per-family policy. "I'm a fan of population control," Francis explained, "and China has taken very tough action. I want the conversation in Copenhagen to get off 'cap-and-trade' and the science argument, and get on to conservation and population control. There are too many people having too many children they can't afford." Laura reminded Francis that China's policy has exacted a terrible toll: "The suicide rate of women in China is among the highest in the world. Maybe it's because they're being forced by a totalitarian state to abort their children. For someone who says she's a 'feminist' to be in favor of this is stunning to me."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Mixed reactions to Obama's Nobel acceptance speech
Some conservatives have lavished praise on President Obama's speech in Oslo, in which he declared that war is sometimes necessary. But John Bolton, former U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations, disagreed with his colleagues on the right. "I thought it was shallow and sophomoric," Bolton declared. "He didn't say anything new, and this speech was filled with fundamental errors that pose a grave danger to the U.S. He said America 'can not act alone,' which is fundamentally wrong and reflects his 'post-American' mindset." Bolton singled out the administration's approach to the world's most populous country. "We're playing softball with China and they're playing ice hockey. They are pursuing their national interests and that's what we ought to do. But President Obama doesn't believe in a clash of national interests and doesn't believe in using American power to advance those interests."
Personal Story Segment 
Growing violence at Mexican border
Ultra-violent Mexican border cities are the focus of an a special on the National Geographic Channel. Laura welcomed NGC's Lisa Ling, who outlined the danger to Americans. "There is a very violent war that is raging just south of our border," Ling reported. "The tiny town of Ciudad Juarez saw 1,800 murders last year, many of them decapitations and dismemberments. This is considered to be the most violent city in the world, and we're starting to see examples of the violence spilling over to the United States." Laura added that all Americans have reason to fear the drug wars to the south. "Even in Virginia there have been killings in the schools. A lot of the carnage is coming from violent gangs that cross our border into the United States."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Black lawmakers in dispute with Obama
The Congressional Black Caucus is demanding that President Obama do something to reduce stratospheric unemployment among black Americans. Laura asked Rev. Eugene Rivers whether the president is losing support among blacks. "The black community loves Obama," Rivers replied, "but if a year from now the numbers are similar, you're going to hear a very different tone. He has to do something or he's going to lose his base. There is enormous economic pain." Political strategist Tara Dowdell took issue with those black lawmakers who are criticizing the president. "I reject the notion that President Obama isn't doing anything for the black community or society in general. This is a very difficult environment that we're in and African Americans, more than most people, do understand that change takes time. The stimulus package has stopped the pain, stopped the bleeding, and right now our economy is stabilizing."
Impact Segment 
Drone strike reportedly kills top al-Qaeda operative
Rocket fire from an American drone reportedly killed Al Qaeda big shot Saleh al-Somali this week in Pakistan. Laura asked former CIA terror expert Michael Scheuer about the significance of the killing. "It's important," Scheuer said, "because any dead Al Qaeda is good. So it's an important tactical victory, but is largely irrelevant to the strategic situation." Scheuer warned that some American Muslims are becoming increasingly radicalized and dangerous. "The incitement efforts of Al Qaeda and its allies around the world have affected young male Muslims in every part of the world, and we're crazy to think it's not going to happen here. It's a problem for America and it's a threat that we don't want to talk about. We have not killed the enemy who attacked us, and if we don't kill them they will certainly kill us."
Back of Book Segment 
Former madam pointing fingers at Tiger Woods
The latest shoe to drop in the Tiger Woods case is the high-heeled stiletto of former Hollywood madam Michelle Braun, who claims she furnished him with high-priced escorts. Laura welcomed TMZ.com's Mike Walters, who provided the latest details in the Woods saga. "Michelle Braun has come out," Walters reported, "and said that two of the women who claim to have had affairs with Woods were escorts working for her. The insinuation is that they were prostitutes." Walters added that Woods' legal team has obtained an unusual injunction in Europe. "The injunction says that nobody in the UK can publish any nude photos or video of Tiger Woods. If he's being that aggressive, it seems like an admission that there's something there. It seems this is the first time he is admitting that he could have been in situations where someone took photos of him in the nude."
