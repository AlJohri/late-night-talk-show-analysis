Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Federal economic bailout plan ready to go
"President Bush has painted a doomsday scenario if the federal government does not prop up the chaotic financial industry, and I think we have to believe him. Some conservatives are screaming 'socialism,' and the bailout certainly is not a free market play. But as Talking Points has explained, federal oversight is needed to make certain the markets are not rigged and greedheads don't hurt the folks. That oversight did not happen and hard-working Americans are getting hammered. If the government doesn't bail out the financial industry, foreign investment will cut and run. That will lead to a worldwide depression - you might lose your job and you'll certainly lose money in your house and investments. Ideological theory has to walk when faced with fact. The bailout sends a message to the world that the government will not let the economy collapse, there is no other choice. Talking Points understands the danger and has to support the atrocious bailout because there is no other way. So there you have it - hear it and weep."

The Factor welcomed ABC News host John Stossel, a libertarian who opposes the federal bailout. "The government's responsibility," Stossel asserted, "is to get out of the way, because when government doesn't guarantee institutions market discipline protects us. You say 'government oversight' with such faith, but the government can't even count votes correctly. Maybe we'll go into a panic if there is no bailout, but how do we know? Crisis is the friend of the state, and they have always used it to get more power and more of our money." The Factor argued that a bailout is the only option. "The government has to do the bailout because foreign investment in the United States is enormous. If they pull that money out we're going to go into a depression and a panic. That's why they have to do it."

Impact Segment 
Who's to blame for the financial mess?
The Factor welcomed FNC contributor Laura Ingraham, who blamed the crisis on government actions taken more than a decade ago. "The problem here is government intervention in the free markets," Ingraham said. "In 1995 Bill Clinton decided to push all these institutions to lend to minority communities, many of them risky loans. That was perhaps a noble idea, but it certainly wasn't following free market principles. This was not a failure of the free market, it was a failure of the government trying to do social engineering." The Factor reiterated that everyday folks need to be protected. "I don't want people getting hurt from some mistake in the Clinton administration. The Bush administration didn't practice oversight, either."

News Link: How the left is to blame for financial crisis
Top Story II 
A view of the bailout from the left
The Factor further explored the proposed bailout with Obama supporter and professor Marc Lamont Hill. "All of us in are agreement that this bailout has to happen," Hill said. "The question becomes what strings do we attach - do we just give big business a handout and allow them to go unchecked, or do we put on more regulation? To say this is not a failure of free market is ridiculous - the free market doesn't work for poor people. And providing people access to adequate housing is not 'social engineering,' people are desperate for the American Dream, so let's not demonize these people." The Factor told Hill that individuals, not government, are ultimately responsible for their lives. "You have to work for the American Dream, you have to get educated and skilled."
Personal Story Segment 
Vicious campaign ad running... only on MSNBC
A pro-Obama group has produced a grisly ad about John McCain's history of cancer, implying that he is not long for this Earth. The Factor spoke about the ad, which was rejected by all networks except MSNBC, with FNC's Megyn Kelly. "I spoke with the Obama campaign," Kelly reported, "and they had nothing to do with it. There are groups who can take donations and use the money for things like this, and it's totally legal as long as they don't 'coordinate' with the campaigns. This is a vile attack on John McCain and it's cruel, but I'm sure the Obama campaign would say some of the ads connecting him with characters in Chicago are cruel also." The Factor pointedly named the people behind the anti-McCain ad. "This was put out by two despicable human beings - far-left hatchet man Robert Greenwald, a failed Hollywood director, and James H. Dean, the brother of Democratic boss Howard Dean. This continues the pattern of behavior sanctioned by GE CEO Jeffrey Immelt and NBC boss Jeff Zucker."

Culture Warriors Segment 
The gay marriage debate in California
The Factor was joined by "Culture Warriors" Margaret Hoover and Monica Crowley, who discussed the upcoming vote - fervently opposed by many in Hollywood - that would ban gay marriage in California. "I am not a Hollywood elite," Hoover began, "and I disagree with everything they think politically. But even a broken clock is right twice a day, and it's wrong to say that gay people and straight people can't have the same rights. This is the civil rights issue of our generation." Crowley predicted that the proposed ban will fail. "Public opinion in California is shifting because there are so many people in America who are seeing these people getting married. Once they see couples doing this, they shrug and say 'what's the big deal?'" Both Culture Warriors predicted that John McCain and Barack Obama will go ahead with Friday's debate in Mississippi.

Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Sarah Silverman on the campaign
CBS's Katie Couric did her best to play "gotcha" with Sarah Palin, asking for specific examples of John McCain's past regulatory efforts. The Factor put forth this reality check: "Would Al Gore have known what regulations Bill Clinton had passed in Arkansas? C'mon! These questions get us nowhere. What we should be asking Governor Palin is how she would solve complicated world problems. The McCain campaign would be wise to set up an interview with The Factor and allow Sarah Palin to define herself." Speaking of Palin, Democratic Congressman Alcee Hastings told a crowd that "anybody toting guns and stripping moose don't care too much about what they do with Jews and blacks." The Factor's reality check: "In addition to being grammatically incorrect, that statement is dumb on a colossal scale." Finally, comic actress Sarah Silverman created a video beseeching young Floridians to get their grandparents to vote for Barack Obama, reminding them of what happened in Florida in 2000. The Factor's reality check: "She called him 'a little man named Al Gore.' Not good!"
