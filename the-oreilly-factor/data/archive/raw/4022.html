Top Story 
Slipping Away?
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Eric began Friday's show with new polls showing that the Donald Trump juggernaut may be losing some steam. Tea Party leader Scottie Nell Hughes and political reporter Caitlin Huey-Burns analyzed Trump's loss of momentum. "We're starting to see his numbers take a little bit of a dip for the first time," Huey-Burns reported, "but he is still leading the Republican pack. Other candidates like Carly Fiorina and Marco Rubio are increasing their numbers, and the question is how long this trend lasts. I think Trump has to be a little more creative in his public appearances, he has to change his show." Hughes, an unabashed Trump supporter, dismissed the notion that his campaign is on the decline. "It's absolutely laughable when anyone says that he's slipping. He has the highest percentage of a presidential candidate and he has consistently stayed above his opponents by double digits. People like him because he fights back and punches at the establishment."
Impact Segment 
John Boehner Resigns
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Speaker of the House John Boehner stunned the political world Friday with his announcement that he will step down as Speaker and resign from Congress next month. Former Speaker Newt Gingrich joined Eric to analyze Boehner's bombshell announcement. "I really didn't see this coming," Gingrich admitted. "He is a very devout Catholic and I think his highlight as Speaker will always be yesterday when he met Pope Francis, so I can't imagine a better day for him to make this decision and announcement. He said he went home last night, thought about it, and told his wife this morning." Gingrich pointed out that Boehner was under severe pressure from House conservatives. "He has 30 or 40 guys on his right who are constantly attacking him and he has several hard issues coming up, including the funding of Planned Parenthood. I think he just said, 'I've done my share, I've served my country, and I'm going home.'"
Unresolved Problems 
The Effect of Boehner's Decision
Fox News political correspondent Carl Cameron, checking in from the Values Voter Summit in Washington, reported on the reaction to John Boehner's resignation. "These evangelical Christians and social conservatives are thrilled," Cameron said. "They are rejoicing, they think that now there is a chance to get a real conservative in to get things done. And the effort to oust Speaker Boehner wasn't just from conservative members of the House, it has also been driven by the 2016 Republican presidential candidates. They've been talking about how they have to defund Planned Parenthood and how they were willing to shut down the government." Cameron also theorized how Scott Walker's departure will affect the Republican race. "There's an indication that Iowa may now be a little more open, which has encouraged John Kasich to spend time and money in Iowa. Marco Rubio has also said he'll step up his presence in Iowa."
Factor Followup 
More Trouble for Hillary
On the Democratic side, Bernie Sanders has opened up a 16-point lead over Hillary Clinton in New Hampshire. FNC's Ed Henry reported on the Clinton campaign's reaction. "They have to be worried about the poll numbers," he said, "because New Hampshire is the first primary in the nation. Can she recover? Yes, on Super Tuesday in the South there are a lot of states where she will probably do better than Bernie Sanders. But politics is about momentum, and if you lose either Iowa or New Hampshire, that can be devastating to a campaign. Among white men, Hillary Clinton's unfavorability rating is 74%, so she needs a massive turnout from women in the primaries and the general election." Eric added, "The new Quinnipiac poll shows that she loses head-to-head to Bush, Carson, and Fiorina, but ekes out a win over Trump."
Personal Story 
Candidates Visit the Factor
Bill spoke with some of the leading Republican presidential candidates over the past few days. Carly Fiorina talked about her recent surge: "I'm running to win this nomination ," she declared. "and even though far fewer people know me than know Mr. Trump, more and more people support me. They know we need a no-nonsense leader who has a track record of actually producing results, solving problems, and challenging the status quo. I intend to beat Donald Trump!" Senator Marco Rubio used his appearance to reject the astonishing smear by Democratic Party boss Debbie Wasserman Schultz, who accused Rubio of associating with a Nazi sympathizer. "This didn't surprise me," Rubio lamented. "Debbie Wasserman Schultz tends to do these sorts of things, and this was such a despicable attack that even liberal media outlets have criticized her. It is indicative of the levels of desperation in the Democratic Party." Also, Dr. Ben Carson defended his controversial suggestion that an Islamic person should not be president. "I said quite clearly," he protested, "that I have no problem with anyone, whatever their religion, who subscribes to American values and is willing to put the Constitution above their religious beliefs. I would not advocate that we put a Christian in the White House if their goal is a theocracy."
Back of the Book 
Putin on the March
Russian President Vladimir Putin is sending military forces to Syria and actively supporting Syrian tyrant Bashar al-Assad. Foreign policy analysts Ric Grenell and Simon Rosenberg scrutinized Putin's belligerence and President Obama's apparent reluctance to respond. "If Hillary Clinton is the nominee," Grenell said, "she will not be able to run away from President Obama's feckless foreign policy. Putin is directly challenging the U.S. policy that Assad should go, he's saying Russia will prop him up. That is unbelievable - Russia is dismissing the Hillary Clinton 'reset' button and Barack Obama's policy. Being challenged on the global stage and not doing anything about it makes Democrats look very weak." Rosenberg, head of a left-leaning think tank, conceded that the Obama administration has seemed impotent. "The combination of this and the refugee crisis in Europe is making it clear to the administration that they have to do more. They have to get serious about a political settlement in Syria and there has to be a stepped-up military campaign. I hope President Obama leads us there." But Eric concluded with a pithy lamentation, saying, "I don't have any idea what the Obama position is, it's a mess!"
