Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Poverty and taxes in America
"A new report from the Census Bureau says 15% of American families now live below the poverty line...but that stat is misleading because most Americans are in debt...While the median income is about $50,000 in the USA, the amount of money we owe can't be covered by salaries, especially because of the tax situation...Consumers can't buy stuff without incurring more debt. Punishing taxation is bleeding us. And these taxes continue to go up because the states and cities are bankrupt. Why are they bankrupt? Because of pensions, health care costs, corruption and general irresponsibility with our tax dollars. The politicians have built a society dependent on government and now our freedom is being eroded because of that."

To debate the merits of the TP memo, the Factor welcomed Dennis Kneale, a correspondent for Fox Business, and Nomi Prins, a liberal economic analyst. Ms. Prins argued the biggest problem with the economy is that not enough revenue is coming into the states because the housing market got crushed and corporations aren't paying their effective share of taxes. The Factor countered that going after corporations will only make the economy worse.

Dennis Kneale concurred with the Memo's thesis that Americans are being strangled by taxes. He continued by going after our entitlement culture: "Since Lyndon Johnson declared war on poverty, we've sunk almost $12 trillion into anti-poverty programs. A war is supposed to have a beginning and an end."

Once again, the Factor made its case for a 2% across-the-board consumption tax, but neither guest would get on board with that plan.
Impact Segment 
Is Rick Perry doomed?
Guests: Dick Morris

A new Field poll shows the President is in major trouble in California, with his approval rating dropping to just 46%, down 8 points since June.

The Factor declared that while California is in bad economic shape, Obama will still carry the state in 2012. But political analyst Dick Morris cautioned him not to underestimate how many people are abandoning Obama in droves. As evidence, he pointed to the fact that the President only jumped one point in the polls after his major jobs address. "The country is tuning out."

Morris also said certain GOP candidates, without naming names, still have the ability to drive people back to the incumbent. He sees Rick Perry getting himself into deeper trouble with each passing debate, citing the notion that independents, old people, the Christian right and immigration people all may be turning against him.
Factor Follow Up Segment 
More revelations in Solyndra investigation
Guests: Brian Ross, ABC News

The Energy Department, with the encouragement of President Obama, provided more than $500 million in loans directly to the Solyndra solar panel company. The business is now bankrupt and the White House is in hot water.

ABC News investigative reporter Brian Ross: "It goes right to the door of the White House. New emails that came out today at a Congressional hearing reveal the pressure put on people at the White House to move on the timing , the pushback from budget analysts saying we're getting rushed here...I was stunned to learn today that earlier this year the White House became aware that the company was about to go bankrupt." Ross claimed the government then restructured the loan so private investors would receive any money left over from the bankruptcy before taxpayers.

The Factor asked where the buck stops, if it goes all the way up to the office of the President or whether his underlings are really responsible. Ross: "The loan was put on hold by the Bush administration on January 9, 2009, and then six days after the new administration took office, it was back on a fast track."
Body Language Segment 
The GOP debaters' body language
Guests: Tonya Reiman

Rep. Michele Bachmann accused her rival Rick Perry of cronyism with a drug company that donated to him. Gov. Perry fought back: "I can't be bought!" Tonya Reiman, the Factor's body language expert, noticed Perry's anger by the way he tucked his chin into his body.

Next up - Hillary Clinton was asked by a CNN reporter if there's a chance she'd challenge Obama in the Democratic primary. The Secretary of State responded by saying, "it's below zero," followed by her signature laughter. Tonya determined this was sincere because of the way Mrs. Clinton angled her body to distance herself from the question.

Finally, Tonya examined the recent shoot-out between O'Reilly and White House spokesman Jay Carney. The Factor jested: "I made a complete fool out of myself as usual, correct?"
Miller Time Segment 
Why does the left hate America?
Guests: Dennis Miller

On the 10th anniversary of 9/11, some far-left bomb-throwers used the opportunity to verbally attack the U.S. New York Times columnist Paul Krugman led the charge against former Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld.

Dennis Miller opined: "What does the Times matter anymore? I mean, I line my parakeet cage with it and my parrot wouldn't take a dump for three months...There's a nuclear winter coming for guys like Krugman...If these guys hate America so much, I guess they should look for another place."

On the Obama jobs plan, Miller skirted the specifics and put forth that it's time for the President to go in November. After the President failed to chide Jimmy Hoffa Jr. for calling Tea Party members "sons of bitches" at a Labor Day event, Miller decided Obama doesn't care about 50% of Americans and won't defend people like him against "idiots" like Hoffa.

On some Columbia University students lunching with Iranian leader Ahmadinejad, the sage of Southern California and the Factor vehemently disagreed. Miller said he'd never lunch with the dictator; but the Factor claimed he would break bread just to give Ahmadinejad some jazz.
Back of Book Segment 
Did You See That?
Guests: Juliet Huddy

It was high tension on "The View" when conservative co-host Elisabeth Hasselbeck faced off against uber-liberal filmmaker Michael Moore, who claimed Usama bin Laden deserved to be tried in the USA.
Moore: "We did a very smart thing putting the Nazis on trial..."
Hasselbeck: "But the war was over."
Moore: "The war IS over."
Hasselbeck: "To say Osama bin Laden, responsible for the slaughters of thousands of individuals, deserves a trial in New York City...absolutely not!"
Moore: "What are we afraid of?"
Fox News analyst Juliet Huddy called Michael Moore a waste of time. She said his notion that we're a flawed country for taking out al Qaeda guys with drones and shooting UBL makes him completely irrational. "He drones on like the teacher in the Peanuts."

The Factor switched gears to the MTV program "Teen Mom." One of its stars, 19-year-old Jenelle Evans, got into a fight, which was caught on videotape. She was arrested and convicted on a misdemeanor affray charge. After explaining the story, Juliet complained that the show glamorizes pregnant teens whose lives are going to hell.
