Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Illegal immigration issues explode
"Both Barack Obama and John McCain have put out Spanish-language political ads; both are untrue and ridiculous. Obama's ad accuses McCain of being allied with Rush Limbaugh in disliking illegal immigrants. That is absurd - Limbaugh has hammered McCain for years over the issue. The bottom line is that McCain has been very accommodating to immigrants, both legal and illegal, and Senator Obama did not tell the truth in his ad. McCain's ad says that Obama is one reason the McCain-Kennedy immigration bill failed. That's not true - Obama voted for the bill and had little to do with shaping it. Do these guys not know this stuff is going to be flagged by me and others. Talking Points believes Americans want a president who is a standup guy, who will try not to put out misleading ads. Instead of attacking each other in Spanish, both senators should tell the people what they're going to do about immigration. Tell them in English, Italian, Russian and Chinese, but tell them straight."

The Factor analyzed the Spanish-language ads with strategists from both sides of the political divide. "I'm not here to defend McCain's ad," said Republican Kellyanne Conway. "There are easier ways to go after Barack Obama. He is for abortion in the extreme, and that's a Hispanic issue. The best way to win the Hispanic vote is on issues, and this ad was not presidential." Democrat Tanya Acker also admitted that Obama's ad is deceptive. "This ad is a bit over the top, particularly because Rush Limbaugh and John McCain have not been known to be allies. This tries to imply some broader alliance between McCain and the right wing. Both Obama and McCain are pandering to a community that is very much up for grabs." The Factor implored both campaigns to raise the level of discourse. "These ads are ridiculous, totally false, and they are both at fault. Let's have no more of this garbage!"

Impact Segment 
Professor assigns anti-Palin writing project
Andrew Hallam, a professor at Metro State College in Denver, assigned his students to write an essay criticizing Sarah Palin. The Factor spoke about the professor and his assignment with two of Hallam's students. "There were about five of us," said Jared Keele, "who completely disagreed with the assignment. It became a free-for-all with the professor and the rest of the class against us. What he did was unethical and over the line." Janna Barber added that Hallam had already made his political views abundantly clear. "He had previously said that Bush-bashing is one of his favorite things to do. My goal was not to have him fired, but just to bring this to people's attention. I don't want opinions forced upon me." The Factor contended that professors, whatever their political leanings, "should not impose" their views.

Personal Story Segment 
McCain campaign redeploys Palin
Some observers say Governor Sarah Palin has subtly changed her speaking style over the past two weeks. The Factor was joined by pollster Frank Luntz, who analyzed Palin's oratory and presence. "There's one word people use to describe her," Luntz declared, "and that is 'authentic.' They see themselves in her and that she's compassionate. I believe she will now slow down her speech pattern, which will give her more gravitas. She's becoming more explanatory, less oratorical." The Factor added that Palin has generally avoided hostile questioners. "I thought she looked very relaxed with Sean Hannity, and she has to show that same kind of relaxation with other journalists who will be trying to trip her up."
Fridays with Geraldo Segment 
Latest on the OJ Simpson trial
Geraldo Rivera began with Casey Anthony, whose infant daughter is missing and whose house is under siege by protestors. "The public has concluded," Rivera declared, "that Casey Anthony is responsible for the disappearance or the death of her baby. The police have not charged her, so there is enormous pent-up frustration. But what the cops are doing is extra-judicial - they are allowing these confrontations to happen by not patrolling that neighborhood. It is intolerable, this is vigilante justice." Rivera also reported the latest in the O.J. Simpson kidnapping and robbery trial in Las Vegas. "There may have been a crime committed, and he may still go down because guns were involved. But the odds of him being convicted have dropped to below 50% because everyone involved in this case is a convict or a lowlife."

Week in Review Segment 
Politician's son suspected in Palin email hack
Laura Ingraham entered the No Spin Zone with her take on the week's major stories, beginning with the revelation that a 20-year old Tennessee man, son of a Democratic politician, is suspected of hacking into Sarah Palin's e-mail. "Imagine if a Republican had hacked into Michelle Obama's or Nancy Pelosi's personal account," Ingraham said. "You would hear wails of protest from all these privacy advocates we hear from every time there is terrorist surveillance. Now they're suddenly silent. We should ask who put this kid up to this and was there any political motivation?" The Factor also asked Ingraham why so many self-proclaimed feminists loathe Sarah Palin and her pro-life stance. "They hate the fact that she exists," Ingraham replied, "because she lives her pro-life stance. Her personal life story reveals what she really believes, and she lives her principles. That is very threatening to people who believe abortion rights are the Holy Grail of politics." The Factor lamented "the hatred that Governor Palin has directed toward her. It disturbs me as an American."

Back of Book Segment 
American TV Icons: The women of Baywatch
What has happened to the women who played eye-catching lifeguards on the series "Baywatch?" The Factor put that burning question to entertainment reporter Jared Shapiro, who began his rundown with Pamela Anderson: "She has made a lot of money off of being Pamela Anderson, but she hasn't really furthered her career. Nothing topped her appearance on 'Baywatch,' but she never set out to be an Oscar winner." Alexandra Paul: "She has spent most of her time fighting against the war in Iraq. She went to jail for protesting on federal property, and she's very big on human rights and the environment." Yasmine Bleeth: "She dropped off the face of the Earth in terms of Hollywood. She was arrested for cocaine, went to rehab, and was in an accident. But in rehab she met her future husband, so there may have been a happy ending." Traci Bingham: "This is someone who was a 'Baywatch babe' and parlayed it into an entire career. She went into reality shows and endorsing casinos. She said I'm a 'Baywatch babe' and this is what I'm going to do."
