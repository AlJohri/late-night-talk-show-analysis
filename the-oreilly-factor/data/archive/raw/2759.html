Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Riots, government spending and last-minute deals
"Last night Talking Points urged you to call Congress and tell them to vote against the trillion-dollar-plus spending bill. Just moments before that, Senator Harry Reid pulled the corrupt bill from the Senate floor. So Reid is humiliated and the country is wising up fast. The USA owes about $14 trillion and the head of the Fed, Ben Bernanke, says we better start paying that debt down or there will be a fiscal disaster. Riots throughout Europe this week demonstrated that plenty of folks want other people to pay for their lives, and if they don't get that they will turn violent. This could happen in America, but I don't think it will. The November election, the tax compromise, and the corrupt spending bill being pulled demonstrate that American people value self-reliance far more than Europeans. Fair-minded people support government safety nets, but cradle-to-grave entitlements are simply impossible. So give yourselves a round of applause - thousands of you contacted your elected officials last night. Harry Reid pulled the spending bill and, believe me, if anything like that comes back again, you will know about it in a heartbeat."

For a view from the left, The Factor asked political science professor Caroline Heldman to delineate what the government owes its citizens. "We are owed life, liberty and the pursuit of happiness," Heldman replied. "Life would be health care, the pursuit of happiness would mean that everyone who sought gainful employment would get it, and certainly higher education and a poverty safety net, especially for home makers who are taking care of children at home." To pay for the government munificence, Heldman suggested a tax rate of 50% on the wealthiest Americans. The Factor questioned the professor's math and logic: "You don't understand the enormous cost of cradle-to-grave health care for everybody. We can't raise that kind of money, there's just not enough money to pay the bills of everybody who gets sick and to educate everybody who's qualified to go to college."
Impact Segment 
Krauthammer on Obama's comeback
Writing in his syndicated column, FNC contributor Charles Krauthammer declared that President Obama's tax compromise was a brilliant political move that will help him get re-elected. The Factor invited two prominent conservatives to respond. "There's no question the tax bill is good policy and smart politics," said Bay Buchanan. "President Obama has moved to the right, and any time you have a left-leaning president fighting the left, he comes across as somebody that independents can take another look at. But this one act is not going to change things." Nancy Pfotenhauer quarreled with Krauthammer's assertion that President Obama has boosted his re-election chances. "Charles says that the President, no matter what he does, is going to hold on to the liberal base because they have nowhere else to go. But the left does not have to embrace another candidate, all they have to do is stay home."
Lou's the Boss Segment 
Lou Dobbs on investing for the future
Fox Business anchor Lou Dobbs entered the No Spin Zone with his investment advice for 2011. "The business environment is improving quickly," Dobbs asserted, "and the refusal to go ahead with the omnibus spending bill is a major development in returning responsible government to Washington. If it lasts, the bond markets and equity markets and investors will love that. There has been a 10% or 11% appreciation in the stock market this year, and I think we'll see at least that kind of gain in 2011." Dobbs lauded President Obama's recent policy moves. "We are hearing this administration talk business-friendly for the first time. They're talking about doubling exports and they're talking about manufacturing, which are very healthy possibilities for investors right now." The Factor scolded any conservatives "who don't want the economy to get better because they don't want Obama to be re-elected."
Fridays with Geraldo Segment 
Weather reporter charged with lying about rape
New York City weather presenter Heidi Jones has been charged with falsely reporting that she was sexually assaulted. FNC's Geraldo Rivera elaborated on the bizarre tale. "Heidi Jones worked hard to get to New York," Rivera began, "and was working weekends on Good Morning America. She told cops that someone attempted to assault her in Central Park and she described the assailant as a Hispanic man. But the cops are very sharp, they found inconsistencies in her story, and it turns out that the incident never happened." The Factor theorized that "if somebody would do this and there was no rape, there's something wrong with them."
Personal Story Segment 
The dumbest things of the year
FNC's Greg Gutfeld and Arthel Neville joined The Factor to name the stupidest statements of the year gone by. Coming in at #5, Nancy Pelosi, who said this about health care reform: "We have to pass the bill so that you can find out what is in it." "Every time she speaks," Gutfeld joked, "I keep waiting for a house to land on her." #4, former BP CEO Tony Hayward, who notoriously said "I want my life back." "What's even dumber," Neville added, "is that this guy walked away with $17 million in severance." #3, Senate candidate Sharron Angle, who told a class of students that "I've been called the first Asian legislator in our Nevada State Assembly." #2, Attorney General Eric Holder, who admitted he had not read the Arizona immigration law that he vigorously opposed. And the absolute dumbest statement was uttered by Congressman Alan Grayson, who said "Republicans want you to die quickly if you get sick." The Factor pointed out that "Congressman Grayson's job died very quickly when he was voted out after one term."
Back of Book Segment 
The smartest statements of the year
And what were the most brilliant utterances of 2010? This was Charles Krauthammer on what President Obama should have said to Muslims about the proposed Ground Zero mosque: "Yes, I stand for the Constitution and you have every right. However ... I would urge you to build the mosque elsewhere." Bernie Goldberg on the liberal media: "Reporters aren't happy simply covering stories, they feel a need to champion causes." Dennis Miller on radical priest Michael Pfleger: "This guy looks self-aggrandizing to me. Beware the man who leads the flock with rhinestones on his staff." Sarah Palin on the need for government to emphasize national security above all else: "That's what we need to be funding, instead of these other things on the periphery that get in the way of the private sector's progress." Brit Hume on the success of Fox News: "I always feared that if our audience began to grow other media outlets would say, 'these guys are doing something right, we ought to emulate them.' But they've gone the other way." And The Factor on the proposed mosque: "There is not one construction crew that is going to lift a hammer down there. It's not going to get built."
