Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Talk radio and the culture war
"41 Democratic Senators took the time to condemn Rush Limbaugh for a cloudy statement he made on the radio. You may remember that Senator Barack Obama made some comments about the fight in Afghanistan -- we gave Senator Obama the benefit of the doubt. But there was no doubt when NBC News analyst William Arkin insulted American forces. Now, if Senator Harry Reid and the others were really concerned about insults to the military, they would have condemned Arkin. They said nothing, as did NBC News. This whole Limbaugh thing is another partisan charade engineered by the far-left. The fact that elected Senators would buy into these smear tactics is extremely troubling. This country is under siege by ideologues who use fascist tactics and a corrupt main-stream media to spread their defamation. It is well past time for all Americans to revolt against this kind of insanity. Only the folks can stop the madness -- don't support the smear-merchants in any way."

The Factor welcomed Geoffrey Mallard for another take on the situation -- he's the head of the Washington, DC chapter for Iraq Veterans Against the War. "I actually agree with you," Mallard started, "that the Senate is wasting their time talking about Rush Limbaugh, just like they wasted their time talking about the MoveOn ad... instead of doing what the people want and bringing an end to the war in Iraq." The Factor wanted to know "as a veteran who spent 13 months in Iraq, as you did, do you really think it's a waste of time for the US government to tell a smear operation like MoveOn, 'You're wrong?'" Mallard stuck to his guns, saying that any move by the Senate to condemn an anti-war organization was a waste of time. But he conceded that the troops didn't take offense at Limbaugh's comments: "I spend my time emailing my buddies in Iraq right now -- I asked around, 'Are any of you guys concerned about this Rush Limbaugh stuff?' And they said 'No.'"
Impact Segment 
How dangerous is Iran?
Liberals like Jimmy Carter and comedian Bill Maher are playing down the threat from the country of Iran. Dr. George Friedman, the CEO of private intelligence firm Stratfor, came to offer analysis of the level of danger that Iran poses to the US. "Iran is the largest military power in the Persian Gulf, aside from the US. If the US leaves, Iran will dominate the region," Friedman started. That being said, Friedman thought that the nuclear threat was being overplayed a little bit, and was still at least 10 years away from posing any danger. The Factor was worried that "the far left is banging the drum that for the United States to confront Iran is foolish or dangerous. And I'm thinking that idea's gaining a little currency here." Friedman was struck by Jimmy Carter's statements on Iran: "The idea that the United States has nothing to fear from Iran is extraordinarily simplistic... their interests and our interests are very different."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Anna Nicole's boyfriend sues author for $60M
Howard K. Stern, the former boyfriend of Anna Nicole Smith, has sued author Rita Cosby for $60 million. The suit stems from allegations made in Cosby's book that Stern had had a gay affair with photographer Larry Birkhead, also a former boyfriend of Anna Nicole. Fox News anchor Greta Van Susteren joined The Factor to explain the situation. Greta said that she'd "be a little bit nervous if I were Rita Cosby. If that [gay sex] tape doesn't exist and if Rita Cosby is thought to have been reckless, then she's got some big problems." That gave The Factor an idea: "If you're right, I should be suing everybody -- because the reckless disregard for the truth that these media outlets are showing towards me is provable." Greta offered some free legal advice to Stern: "I would only sue if the allegations were false -- I wouldn't sue if there's actually a tape out there. But if this is actually untrue, Larry Birkhead and Howard Stern will win."
Factor Investigation Segment 
Chester Stiles still wanted for child rape
Child rapist Chester Stiles, wanted for sex with a 3-year-old, is still on the loose. The Factor asked Ed Miller from America's Most Wanted to give the latest on the situation: "We can confirm to you tonight that Las Vegas police are investigating the whole situation in terms of the mother and the companions, about who may have known what, and when." Miller also pointed out that the little girl had shown signs of abuse that it would have been hard for her mother to miss. The Factor was worried about the investigation: "Are you confident that the Vegas police are doing all they can here? It seems to me that everyone knows that there's something wrong with this case. It looks to me like this may have been an organized thing." Miller wasn't sure about that -- but he speculated that "lots and lots of pedophiles cozy up to the mother. The mother thinks she's getting a boyfriend out of the deal -- in reality, he's looking at the kid."
Miller Time Segment 
Dennis Miller on Limbaugh
From one Miller to another -- comedian Dennis Miller stopped in to offer his take on the week's news. On the battles against Media Matters: "You guys are big boys, you can take care of yourselves. And I kind of enjoy watching. But it must be exhausting for you." On Britney Spears' children: "I don't know what the legal definition of unfit is, but something tells me it's when they choose to give your kids to Kevin Federline instead of you... If her choreography is going to be anything in the future, it better be a 12 step." On the Illinois Ramadan decorations in school celebration: "If you wanna do yourself a big favor in the eyes of America, when you celebrate this Ramadan thing have a lot of fun with it. Because we don't quite know what to make of you now. We watch you on your holidays, you've got Schwinn bicycle chains, you're beating yourselves with them... so have some fun. Take Bin Laden's beard and make it white for the day or something. You always look like you're bummed out."
Back of Book Segment 
Policing the 'Net: Actresses hammered online
Internet cop Mary Katharine Ham watches cyberspace for The Factor -- she came in for her weekly report. The first topic: Teen actress Miley Cyrus was smeared by online gossip sites who falsely reported that she was pregnant. Ham said that "it's really hard to tell exactly who did it. There's a thousand, gajillion people out there who have this program Photoshop who can do this kind of thing." The Factor pointed out that this obviously made it extremely difficult if Cyrus wanted to sue the culprit. The next topic: Actress Eva Longoria turned the tables on the gossip industry by leaking a hoax sex tape on a website. "Not nearly as bad as the Miley Cyrus thing," The Factor opined. Finally, Mary Katharine Ham unveiled her video spoofing Alicia Silverstone's nude PETA ad. In the ad she climbs out of a pool (fully clothed) and announces her love for rare venison: "I'm mocking Alicia Silverstone's self-righteous attitude. PETA compares animal killing to the Holocaust and slavery. These guys deserve to get ribbed." The Factor wanted to know: "Ribbed? Is that a pun?"
Pinheads & Patriots 
Who's helping and who's hurting?
Wednesday's Patriot is Factor viewer Karina Szypulski, who wrote in to say how upset she was by media lies. Karina is a legal immigrant, and loves America -- for this, she's a Patriot. And the Pinhead: Larry King for asking Whoopi Goldberg a question about Media Matters' smear campaign, but not putting the question in proper context. Larry is a Pinhead -- but The Factor still likes him.
