Talking Points Memo 
Rosie O'Donnell and MSNBC
"Huntley, Brinkley, Chancellor, Brokaw, and now ... Rosie! As Talking Points has been telling you for more than a year, NBC president Jeff Zucker is desperate. NBC is collapsing in the ratings on all fronts, and now Mr. Zucker has turned to Rosie O'Donnell to prop up MSNBC. Just think about this: NBC News, once a prestigious operation with hundreds of excellent news people, now wants a woman who claimed that 'explosives were involved' in the collapse of World Trade Center # 7, and implied that Americans are the real 'terrorists.' The Factor contacted Tom Brokaw for a comment on Rosie, but he had nothing to say. Tim Russert? Nothing to say. Brian Williams? Nothing. All this madness is the responsibility of Jeffrey Immelt, the CEO of General Electric, which owns NBC. Mr. Immelt also had no comment on Rosie O'Donnell, but GE stockholders should have a comment. On Immelt's watch, the NBC network is in dire trouble. Talking Points was spot on when we told you that NBC News had embraced the radical left agenda in pursuit of ratings. There is no doubt any longer."

Top Story 
Hillary slips in polls, Obama gains
Senator Hillary Clinton's poll numbers have dropped since last week's debate, during which she gave various answers to the question about drivers' licenses for illegal aliens. Democratic strategist Flavia Colgan suggested that Senator Clinton now looks vulnerable. "This played into something that the public already has a visceral perception about, which is that Hillary Clinton is inauthentic, that she double-speaks. It's a problem for her because this is the exact type of issue that Iowa voters care deeply about." But Clinton supporter Kiki McLean expressed confidence in her candidate. "She's clearly the front-runner in national polls. And it's not a surprise that when there is a front-runner and all the other candidates turn their guns on her, she'll take some heat on that." The Factor advised all candidates to stop evading tough questions. "When they zeroed in on a very simple question, she could not answer. But I still don't think Barack Obama can overtake Hillary Clinton unless she continues to not answer questions."

Impact Segment 
The media and drivers' licenses for illegals
The vast majority of Americans oppose giving illegal aliens drivers' licenses, but most media outlets take the exact opposite position. Immigration attorney Susan Church tried to rationalize the great divide between the elite media and moat Americans. "There is a tendency of urban newspapers," Church said, "to be more understanding of issues relating to immigrants, and large cities depend absolutely on immigrant labor. This is a safety concern. I want the people driving next to me to be registered, to have insurance, to have passed the safety test." But The Factor took issue with Church's analysis. "This is a security issue, not an insurance issue. A terrorist can take a phony passport, show up at the DMV and get a driver's license. They accept passports that you can buy anywhere, so this is open season for terrorists."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Cheerleading coach in photo scandal
In still another high school scandal, this one in Blanchester, Ohio, a 19-year old cheerleading coach posed topless with a 15-year old freshman girl during a party at the home of a football coach. Cincinnati radio host Bill Cunningham reported the local reaction, or lack thereof. "The reaction," Cunningham said, "has been 'so what?' I think the sexualization of children is a major issue, but here it's like business as usual. And the girl is not being condemned, she's being applauded." The Factor pointed out that this occurred in middle America. "I don't understand the lack of outrage in the community. This isn't San Francisco, it's Ohio."

News Link: Cheerleader scandal rocks Ohio high school
Personal Story Segment 
Body parts for profit
An upcoming National Geographic special examines the global market for organs, and The Factor welcomed the show's host Lisa Ling, who explained what she discovered. "Close to 100,000 Americans are waiting for organs, and thousands of people will die while waiting. The Internet has opened the whole world, so some people are going to places like India or Pakistan to buy kidneys. If you are willing to pay the price, you can probably find the organ. We pose ethical questions about how far someone would go to secure an organ for their loved one."
"Is it Legal?" Segment 
Ozzy Osbourne vs. the cops
FNC legal experts Megyn Kelly and Lis Wiehl analyzed various issues, including the thorny question of whether waterboarding qualifies as torture. "I actually think," Kelly opined, "that you have a leg to stand on when you say waterboarding may not be torture. There are clear definitions of torture - it has to be prolonged and severe physical or mental pain and suffering." But Wiehl argued that waterboarding is clearly against the law. "Torture is illegal, and in my estimation waterboarding is torture. You don't know if waterboarding or any of these techniques work - you get false information." The Factor pointed out that CIA officials credit waterboarding with saving lives. "My position is simple - only the president should be able to order waterboarding, and only under extraordinary circumstances."

Shifting legal gears, The Factor reported that rocker Ozzy Osbourne is unhappy with police officers in North Dakota, who lured fugitives to a "free concert" by using his name, then arrested the felons when they showed up. Kelly applauded the sheriff for executing a "brilliant move," and Wiehl ridiculed Osbourne's claim that the sting will "damage his reputation." The Factor asked the obvious question: "Can Ozzy's reputation be any more damaged."

Back of Book Segment 
Great American Culture Quiz
Steve Doocy and Martha MacCallum met again in the Great American Culture Quiz, hoping to atone for their disgraceful showing last week when neither could come up with a single correct answer. As always, The Factor asked five questions, including these: "Who is actress Tippi Hedren's famous daughter?" ... "Under which President did the CIA begin to operate?" In a matchup that rivaled the Patriots-Colts showdown, Doocy answered the final two questions correctly to eke out a one-point victory.
Pinheads and Patriots 
Who's helping, and who's hurting?
Tuesday's Patriot: Mike Huckabee, who has reached 10% in some Republican presidential polls. The Factor, who had predicted that would never happen, commended Huckabee for running a "determined campaign." And the Pinhead: PBS host Bill Moyers, who questioned why the media barely covered recent anti-war protests in various cities. The Factor posed this question to Moyers: "Why haven't you covered the improving security situation in Iraq? The surge is working, and that is a far more important story than these protests, which averaged less than 10,000 people per city."
