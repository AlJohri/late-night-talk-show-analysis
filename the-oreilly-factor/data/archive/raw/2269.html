Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Is the tide turning against political correctness?
"Last week I made fun of Helen Thomas for asking a dopey question at Barack Obama's first press conference. For years Ms. Thomas has accused the U.S. of 'murdering people,' and she has consistently played down the terror threat. So in the spirit of political satire, I compared her voice to that of the Wicked Witch of the East. Soon after that, the far-left kooks cranked up and a women's group demanded that I apologize to Ms. Thomas. I asked you about that, and a whopping 93% say I should not apologize to Helen. What I'm getting from this is that many Americans are fed up with out-of-control PC nonsense. In your face demonstrations are becoming more common in America, and Talking Points believes these displays are angering fair-minded Americans. So while fair Americans will give President Obama a chance, it looks like far-left lunacy is being slapped down. Let's hope that continues."

The Factor asked FNC analysts Mary Katharine Ham and Juan Williams whether they discern a backlash against far-left folly. "Fair-minded Americans generally act out against this kind of thing," Ham began, "but what's interesting to me is that I thought President Obama would politically neuter these far-left antics more than he has. His inability to control the situation in the stimulus bill has signaled to folks that he's not going to be shutting them up any time soon." Williams accused self-proclaimed "progressives" of possessing one-sided outrage. "These are people who can't take a joke, and that says something about them. If you think the far-right can be close-minded, try the far-left. You can tell a joke about Sarah Palin, but don't try to tell one about Helen Thomas. They're overprotective and they're trying to skewer you for 'picking on' poor old Helen."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Octuplets: $2 million and growing
The Factor invited author Ann Coulter, whose latest book blames single mothers for many societal ills, to opine on "octo-mom" Nadya Suleman. "It's interesting that everyone is angry," Coulter pointed out, "even liberals who would defend a welfare mother who had 14 children by 14 different men. The government is paying for welfare moms and it's paying for the octo-mom. If you didn't have the welfare system subsidizing the narcissistic choices of women like this, these 14 children would have a much better life. If the government were not going to be paying for these children, they would be put up for adoption and they would grow up in wonderful homes." The Factor again urged California to take extraordinary steps to protect Suleman's children. "We believe this is child abuse and child neglect, and we've petitioned California to have an agent there on a daily basis."

Personal Story Segment 
Student suing professor over speech
Los Angeles college student Jonathan Lopez is suing his professor because the teacher berated him in class for opposing same-sex marriage. The Factor explored the case with Lopez' attorney David French. "The professor silenced Mr. Lopez," French claimed. "He threatened him with expulsion and called him names, all because Mr. Lopez gave an informative speech about his Christian faith and one aspect of that faith. He read from a dictionary definition of marriage and was called a 'fascist.' The school isn't even disputing that." The Factor agreed that Lopez seems to have a strong case. "If the professor really called him a 'fascist bastard,' that has legal implications because the professor is intimidating the student. But the college will say this student was proselytizing in the classroom."

Unresolved Problems Segment 
Whoopi Goldberg: Expectations for Obama
Actress and talk show host Whoopi Goldberg entered the No Spin Zone and put forth her expectations for the Obama presidency. "More than anything," Goldberg said, "I want him to remind us how to talk to each other, and that it's okay to disagree with people you like. And I want to hear from him, even if I don't agree with what he's doing." Goldberg disputed the notion that she is a Hollywood elitist. "I hang with my own people who are middle of the road, some of them are conservative and some of them are liberal. I own guns and I am for abortion." The Factor reminded Goldberg that her signature program is not exactly unbiased. "The show you host every day, 'The View,' is seen as being a left-wing show. John McCain was treated differently than Barack Obama was."
Culture Warriors Segment 
5-year-old still missing in Florida
Culture Warriors Margaret Hoover and Monica Crowley led off with 5-year-old Haleigh Cummings, who has been missing for a week in Florida. "The more I investigated this case," Crowley said, "the more the chaos in the home became obvious. You have a 25-year-old father with a 17-year-old live-in girlfriend living with Haleigh and her 4-year-old brother. The father and the girlfriend took polygraph tests and claim they 'passed,' but according to Fox in Orlando the girlfriend's stories are not consistent." Hoover moved to the 13-year-old British boy who fathered a child with his 15-year-old girlfriend. "Cultures start to fall apart," Hoover warned, "when babies have babies. Our culture has glorified sex without teaching any responsibility along with it. Children are not taught anything by their parents." The Factor complained that "there is no massive outcry in Great Britain, and I'll guarantee you that Alfie and his parents are getting high-fives."

Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: The Fairness Doctrine
Some Democratic politicians are calling for a return of the "Fairness Doctrine," which could force conservative radio stations to provide equal time to liberal talkers. The Factor offered this reality check: "Check can't believe Barack Obama wants the government to intrude into the media industry. If that happens, they'll have to close PBS and NPR, won't they? I think nothing will happen here because Republicans have enough votes in the Senate to block this nonsense." On another subject, the financial publication Barron's criticized General Electric CEO Jeffrey Immelt for presiding over GE's decline in prestige and profitability. The Factor's check: "Barron's is saying Immelt is a disaster, but the fact that this man has remained in his position for eight years says that our financial system is rigged and Americans should be very wary about buying stocks in this environment. And by the way, we flagged you on Immelt and GE more than a year ago."
