Talking Points Memo 
Hillary Clinton and illegal immigration
"We are fair to Senator Clinton, we treat her the same way we treat all the other candidates. But her performance on illegal aliens getting driving credentials has been abysmal. After two weeks, she now says she would not give drivers' licenses to people in the U.S. illegally. But why? The senator will not say. Talking Points went to her web site, but there was no mention of why no licenses. We contacted her press secretary, but he could not tell us why. That's what bothers me about Senator Clinton - I can't figure out what she believes. Millions of Americans will vote for Hillary Clinton no matter what she says; millions will vote against her no matter what she says. But for those of us who take an independent view - and we will decide the presidential election - the failure to address issues in a clear way is a huge deficit. I strongly believe Senator Clinton believes she can become president without ever having to reveal who she really is or what she really thinks. This illegal license deal reinforces my belief."

Top Story 
San Francisco defies federal government... again
The city of San Francisco will issue ID cards to illegal aliens so they can establish legal residency there, and Mayor Gavin Newsom says his police will not cooperate with Homeland Security. Congressman Peter King of New York portrayed San Francisco as a rogue city. "What San Francisco is doing is despicable and it undermines federal law. There should be legislation that cuts off assistance to cities that do not cooperate with the federal government in going after illegal immigrants. San Francisco is clearly declaring itself a law unto itself." Congresswoman Marsha Blackburn recommended that San Francisco be denied money it receives from the federal government. "We're getting to the point where we just have to cut funding off, the power of the purse is one of the things we can do quickly. Why would we want to give an ID card to people who have entered the country illegally?"

News Link: SF issues IDs to illegals
Impact Segment 
Death due to airline chaos?
A 40-year old man went berserk after being delayed more than ten hours at the Vancouver airport. After being subdued by a taser gun, the man died from unknown causes. The Factor blamed the death on general airline anarchy. "I'm laying this right at the door of airline chaos. Airports are a zoo from the moment you get into the terminal, and I'm just fed up with it. The airport is a torture chamber." But aviation consultant Marian Boyd accused The Factor of jumping to the wrong conclusion. "This incident was very much a one-off, it was one poor gentleman who was confused and didn't speak English. The Canadian authorities handled it poorly and obviously overreacted, but only 1/100th of 1% of passengers complain about how bad it is. I fly a lot and 95% of the time I arrive on time and I arrive with my bags."

News Link: Video: Man tasered to death at airport
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Angelina Jolie and international adoption
Angelina Jolie is facing a legal issue surrounding a child she and Brad Pitt adopted in Ethiopia two years ago. Entertainment journalist Tom O'Neil explained the circumstances. "When Angelina Jolie took custody of this child, she announced it was an AIDS orphan. Now it turns out the baby isn't an orphan, mommy is around, and mommy wants her baby back. Angelina is in a tough spot." The Factor advised Jolie to fight to keep the infant. "The best thing for the child is to stay there, I would not give the baby back. These people are looking for money, and this is a shady deal. What's best for the baby is to stay in the USA."

Culture War Segment 
Scientists clone monkey embryos in Oregon
Scientists in Oregon claim to have cloned a monkey for the first time, possibly paving the way for the cloning of human embryos. Physician William Morrone, an advocate of medical experimentation, expressed his unqualified delight. "I want as many tools as possible to treat and comfort. It's just that simple. We're talking about the possibility of cell replacement, and that's what we need for Parkinson's Disease, Multiple Sclerosis, and for spinal cord injuries." But Republican strategist Christine O'Donnell worried about a brave new world. "The group that cloned the monkey proudly stated that this will lead them to human cloning. So at the very core of the debate is dignity versus commodity." The Factor wasn't buying O'Donnell's "slippery slope" argument. "If there is a possibility that people might be cured, why the objection? This is a way for scientists to get something going without human cloning."

Weekdays with Bernie and Jane Segment 
O.J. Simpson and the media
The Factor welcomed media analysts Bernie Goldberg and Jane Hall, who debated how much coverage of O.J. Simpson is appropriate. Goldberg worried that news organizations will give far too much attention to the case. "Is it a news story when a famous person is arrested? Yes, of course it is. But if a news program spends too much time on O.J. or Britney or Paris, then it's not a serious or important program." Hall elaborated on the enduring fascination with O.J. "People are invested in the narrative and it's like watching a train wreck. Is he finally going to go to jail? Is he going to be put away for a long time? But this is not as bad as the Anna Nicole Smith story - it was obscene how much coverage that got." The Factor tried to put the Simpson story in perspective. "Here is a man that many people, including me, believe got away with murder, and is probably one of the top three villains in the world today. I'm going to cover this in a responsible way, but I'm going to give it some coverage."

Back of Book Segment 
The Dhue Point
"Ombudswoman" Laurie Dhue read opinions from viewers, some of whom complained that it's inappropriate to say O.J. Simpson is a killer when he was acquitted. The Factor's response: "Because I'm a commentator, I can have an opinion on the case. In my humble opinion, I think the man was guilty as sin and I'm going to say that." Another viewer criticized the Great American Culture Quiz as frivolous. The reply: "We are a news analysis program and we want to have a little light stuff. We give you five heavy segments, and why can't we have a little fun?" Anyone with grievances or questions can write to dhuepoint@foxnews.com.
