Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Are Pelosi & Congress endangering our troops?
"Congress is voting on funding the wars in Afghanistan and Iraq, and Senators Lindsey Graham and Joseph Lieberman added a provision that would ban the release of so-called 'torture photos' involving U.S. military people. The ACLU, the New York Times and other far left organizations want those pictures put out for the entire world. Some members of Congress also want the photo ban eliminated from the bill, even though the Defense Department and CIA say releasing the pictures could be very dangerous to our military men and women. There comes a time when ideology can cause death, and I am so tired of zealots, primarily on the left, putting their insane personal agendas above the safety of their fellow Americans. Nancy Pelosi has an obligation to get the loons in her party under control. Today the photo provision was stripped by a House committee, and that is on Mrs. Pelosi. So I am writing to Mrs. Pelosi tonight, and you should too. If you really support the troops, we have to get to Nancy Pelosi, and fast."

The Factor was joined by political strategist Dick Morris, who explained the politics surrounding the photo ban. "Obama wants the ban," Morris speculated, "but it also poses a danger for him. He was elected as an anti-war president and he's now facing the prospect of having to increase troop strength in Afghanistan, probably keep troops in Iraq longer than he wants, and possibly send troops to Pakistan. The left is not going to sit still for that, particularly if he is unable to deliver on health care reform. He could have a serious problem on the left of the Democratic Party." The Factor accused those who want the photos released of outright treachery: "Everybody knows these pictures will put our men and women in danger, and our goal is to save the lives of our military people overseas."
Impact Segment 
Gas prices on the rise once again
The price of a gallon of gasoline has gone up by more than a dollar since December, a rise The Factor blamed on speculators in the oil futures market. Fox Business anchor Stuart Varney agreed that gas prices are fluctuating irrationally. "A year ago I would have said prices were a matter of supply and demand," Varney admitted, "but now I think there is something wrong with the internal workings of the market that allows this kind of bubble, burst, and then another bubble. But it's not a conspiracy by the oil companies." The Factor called for restraints on the financial wizards who speculate in oil futures: "I'm a free marketer, but I want the government to put in new regulations so it's harder for the speculators to exploit the market."
Policing the 'Net Segment 
72-year-old grandmother Tasered twice
A Texas deputy pulled over 72-year-old Kathryn Winkfein for speeding, then tasered the woman three times when she grew belligerent. The Factor asked Internet cop Amanda Carpenter how folks have reacted to the incident. "A lot of people disagree about this case," Carpenter said. "I think the cop probably did the right thing, because if he would have physically detained her he could have easily broken her wrist. So even though the tasing looked bad, I think it was better than him tackling her." The Factor disagreed with Carpenter's analysis: "The woman was out of control, but he could have cuffed her gently and put her in the back of the car without tasing her." Carpenter also reported on a new web site honoring slain abortionist Dr. George Tiller: "This was created by a woman who is a counselor at a women's center, she's encouraging women to hold up signs saying 'I am Dr. Tiller.' The most disturbing thing is that there is a child who looks about four years old holding one of these signs."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Imbalance in the media: Tiller vs. PVT Long
As reported previously, the mainstream media provided wall-to-wall coverage of the Tiller murder, but largely ignored the killing of Army Private William Long. Long was shot by American Muslim Abdulhakim Muhammad, who now says what he did was "Islamic justified." The Factor debated the coverage imbalance with Fox News analyst Ellis Henican. "I would have also given more coverage to the abortion killing," Henican asserted. "There was the prominence of the victim and the incendiary aspects of the underlying debate. This is at the heart and soul of one of the most important arguments in the country." But The Factor attributed the disparity to sheer ideology: "The abortion controversy and the Islamic terror controversy are the same in intensity, but Katie Couric on CBS didn't cover Private Long, and Charles Gibson on ABC didn't say a word about it. They didn't cover it! Your problem is that even when the facts overwhelm your argument, you don't give in!"
Miller Time Segment 
Dennis Miller on... gay penguins
The Factor invited Dennis Miller to comment on the homegrown Muslim assassin who murdered Pvt. Long. "He killed one of our guys in this country," Miller said, "because he didn't agree with him politically, but liberals are worrying about three fat guys in beards that we waterboarded six years ago. We're not even the weak horse any more, we're the Shetland pony and we better wake up." Miller somehow managed to segue into a report of lesbian penguins frolicking at a German zoo. "I don't know much about gay penguins," Miller quipped, "but if a couple of gay 'guins want to swap a little beak in Germany, I couldn't care less. Maybe they are pre-op transsexual penguins." The Factor reported that the zoo is trying to transform the penguins into straight birds, and magnanimously suggested that the penguins should be left alone because "if they're happy, they're happy."
Back of Book Segment 
Sarah Palin fires back at David Letterman
David Letterman spent part of the past week mocking Sarah Palin, at one point "joking" that Palin's daughter had been "knocked up by Alex Rodriguez" during a baseball game. Palin called the quip "disgusting" and "sexually-perverted." The Factor analyzed the feud with FNC's Alan Colmes and Monica Crowley. "I think Letterman's writers got the daughters mixed up," Colmes suggested. "It was not the older daughter Bristol who was with her mother at the game, it was 14-year-old Willow. But either way, I don't think you should go after the daughters of public officials." Crowley posed a rhetorical question: "Where is the new era of civility that President Obama's election was supposed to bring? People like David Letterman and Katie Couric continue taking these gratuitous hits at Sarah Palin because she is a woman who is pro-life and pro-gun and she lives her life according to those values. She represents an existential threat to liberalism, therefore she must be destroyed!"
Pinheads and Patriots 
Van Merchant & Kevin Miller
Wednesday's Patriot: Illinois farmer Van Merchant, who donated $25,000 to the Wounded Warrior Project and will receive the jacket given to Bill by the Reagan Library. And the Pinhead: 41-year-old Ohioan Kevin Miller, who was arrested for public indecency and was coincidentally wearing an unflattering woman's bathing suit.
