Talking Points Memo 
A victory in the war against illegal immigration
"Eight years ago, long before the border alien controversy erupted as a major issue, I said 'the military has to secure the border.' I got hammered for that opinion, but after six years, under heavy pressure from you, President Bush finally ordered 6,000 National Guard to the border. Fast forward to now. An article in the very liberal New York Times says the National Guard has been very successful in working with the Border Patrol. In fact, border incursions are down 40%. There's no question the National Guard stopped a lot of bleeding at the Mexican border, both literally and figuratively. Talking Points is happy to report the success in the fight against immigration chaos because the campaign has been so bitter. Clear thinking Americans realize that immigration must be an orderly process, especially now that the economy is receding and there is more competition for jobs. So we, together, have won an important battle in this campaign. The border is much more secure than it was, and the National Guard helped a lot."

Top Story 
WSJ poll: Obama leads McCain by 6 points
At this early stage in the campaign, polls indicate that Barack Obama has an edge over John McCain. The Factor asked political analyst Karl Rove for his assessment of the race. "It's going to be a tough struggle for McCain," Rove asserted. "The advantage is to the Democrats this year, which is why Obama's campaign strategy is unusual. People want to know if he's up to the job, and right now he's spending too much time attacking McCain, particularly in areas where McCain has an advantage, such as Iraq." Rove also criticized McCain for painting himself into a corner on energy. "McCain has closed off a real option to himself by opposing drilling in ANWAR. There is a good argument to be made that if America showed a willingness to tap its resources, prices would come down." The Factor predicted that gas prices will be the single most important issue, and that "the first candidate to come up with a very clear vision on how to alleviate the suffering will win."

News Link: Obama up by 6 points in latest poll
Impact Segment 
Newt Gingrich on the 2008 race
For another take on the campaign, The Factor turned to former Speaker of the House Newt Gingrich. "John McCain should focus on the price of gasoline," Gingrich said, "and the problem with purchasing energy from foreign dictators. He ought to challenge Obama to get off the campaign trail, come back to the Senate, and help pass legislation to reduce the cost of oil." Gingrich also urged Congress to allow drilling in the Arctic and off our coastlines. "Norway, Denmark, and Holland all drill off shore, and these are very environmental countries. We need to bring down the price of oil by drilling here and drilling now. McCain should challenge Obama head-on." The Factor added that automakers should be forced to play a role in any new energy policy. "I want flex-fuel vehicles and I want them mandated by law. That's the only way we're going to get out of this."

News Link: Gas prices a critical issue in campaign
The Kelly File Segment 
Judge in trouble for porn on computer
In a 5 - 4 decision, the Supreme Court has ruled that the Bush administration's treatment of suspected terrorists violates the Constitution. FNC legal analyst and anchor Megyn Kelly elaborated on the court's decision. "Guantanamo Bay detainees can now come into federal court," Kelly said, "and challenge their detention and their designation as enemy combatants. We're giving them more rights than the Nazis got at the Nuremberg trials, and Justice Scalia said the nation will live to regret this." Kelly turned to the case of 9th Circuit Court Judge Alex Kozinski, who was presiding over a pornography case in California when porn was found on his own computer. "He's apparently a big fan of porn and has some crazy pictures of weird stuff with animals. But he has not committed any crime and I don't think anything's going to happen to him." The Factor called this a case of divine justice. "This is why I believe in God. The extremely liberal 9th Circuit Court has been doing a lot of harm to the country, and now this comes along."

Factor Follow Up Segment 
Global warming and fierce weather
Despite recent tornadoes, flooding, wildfires and heat, meteorologist Joe Bastardi dismissed any connection between the harsh weather and "global warming." "It was a very cold May across the United States," Bastardi reported, "and every time we get a cold May we get tornadoes. We are in a time of climactic hardship like the 40's and 50's, so it's maddening to someone like me when everything is blamed on global warming. We've had these kinds of things before. The wildfires are occurring because the Pacific is cooling and California is dryer than normal." The Factor praised Bastardi for his ability to "separate myth from reality."

Week in Review Segment 
Laura Ingraham: Obama's
The Obama campaign has created a web site designed to refute any rumors about the candidate or his wife Michelle. The Factor asked radio host Laura Ingraham about the strategy. "I think it's brilliant," Ingraham replied, "and I think John McCain should take a lesson. Sometimes you really have to correct the record, and if someone feels they have the right to impugn you, you have the right to defend yourself." Ingraham also weighed the pros and cons of criticizing Michelle Obama. "It's pretty risky because she's a very attractive person and carries herself quite well. However, if she's being put out there as a political surrogate, then we have the right to question her. But will nasty personal attacks on Michelle Obama win you votes? No."

Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Katie Couric on Hillary & sexism
CBS anchor Katie Couric has complained that Hillary Clinton was the victim of sexism and "unfair, hostile coverage." The Factor offered this reality check: "Senator Clinton lost to Obama because she was out-campaigned and hammered by an unfair pro-Obama press. Sexism was the least of Hillary Clinton's problems." Meanwhile, actor John Cusack has recorded a TV commercial in which he denounces John McCain for voting against the new GI Bill of Rights. The Factor's reality check: "Senator McCain supports a new GI Bill, but he has his own version. He wants to tie benefits to longer service, an incentive to keep people in the military. Mr. Cusack is peddling propaganda."
