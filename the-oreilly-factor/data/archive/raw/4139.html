Talking Points Memo 
The Media & Donald Trump
Watch more video in the Video Center...
"It is becoming clear that many in the media believe journalists are not acting responsibly unless they are condemning Donald Trump, labeling the man a bigot or worse. Not since the pre-civil rights South has the vicious head of bigotry been raised so prominently in political circles. No question the racist tag will be front and center in the upcoming presidential campaign should Donald Trump get the nomination. That will be the centerpiece of attacks against him. Trump himself does not seem fazed, nor do his supporters. But in order to become president, he will need about 65 million Americans to vote for him, and his opposition believes the racist tag will drive minority and on-the-fence voters away. The question then becomes, how do responsible and fair-minded press people cover the Trump campaign when the race card is being used as an axe? Here's The Factor record and you can look it up: In the beginning of his campaign I directly told Mr. Trump that Mexico would not pay for the border wall, even if economic reprisals were taken against that country. I suggested to the candidate that a mass deportation of illegal aliens would be tied up in federal court for years, effectively shutting that policy down. Same thing with temporarily barring Muslims from entering the USA. Courts would block that, the hearings would take forever. We also investigated Trump's statement about thousands of Muslims in New Jersey celebrating after the 9/11 attack and found it to be exaggerated. In addition, I suggested to the candidate that Muslim nations are needed to defeat the jihadists and America would be wise not to alienate them. Also, I told Donald Trump that John McCain is a hero, that Carly Fiorina's appearance was no issue, that Megyn Kelly's question about women was legit, and just last night that rhetorical mudslinging will not make America great again. I could list dozens of other issues where I have challenged Trump, sometimes to his great annoyance. But I also allow him his say and, to his credit, he comes into this tough forum. He's not afraid, as many other politicians are. Trump is accessible to the voters, a very good thing. The press does not intimidate him and he curries no favor. It is my job to give you insights into those seeking power. Donald Trump is the most important political story in decades. We will cover him fairly, allowing you to decide whether or not he has the right stuff to be president. For that we will be demonized and attacked by those who despise Trump. He deserves fair coverage, as do all the other candidates, including Hillary Clinton. But few media outlets will provide fair coverage in this election cycle. Trump has changed all the rules because he has broken many of them himself. Some press folks are out to get him. We are not, we are out to cover him."
Impact Segment 
Analyzing the Press Response to Trump
The Factor asked Geraldo Rivera and Eric Bolling to scrutinize the Talking Points Memo and overall media coverage of Donald Trump. "To pretend that Trump suddenly introduced race into the political mix," Rivera said, "is intellectually sloppy and hypocritical. Race is at the foundation of American politics and to pretend that Trump brought it into the fray is wrong. He is not a racist and I predict that he may get as much as 25% of the African American vote." Bolling portrayed the media as going through the five stages of grief. "They denied that Donald Trump would ever run then they denied that he would win. Then they went to the anger stage and we have not come to the bargaining stage. The establishment media are now attacking Trump and it is the last stage before they get into depression and acceptance."
Factor Followup 
Democratic Response to Trump
Some far left organizations, among them MoveOn, have banded together to accuse Donald Trump of fomenting violence. Former Bush advisor and 'The Five' co-host Dana Perino opined on the situation. "MoveOn knows that they can rile up their base by using Donald Trump as their foil," she said, "and one of the ways they make money is by exploiting someone. So MoveOn has a particular interest so they can raise money and stay in business. They need the enthusiasm because Republicans are swamping Democrats when it comes to turnout, especially in large states like Florida. Democrats are worried because Donald Trump is such a wild card and this is such a non-traditional year. This will be a tight race to the end."
Unresolved Problems 
Another Illegal Alien Outrage
Watch more video in the Video Center...
A 19-year-old illegal immigrant in Nebraska, after killing a woman while driving drunk, slipped through the hands of authorities and vanished. Republican Senator Ben Sasse of Nebraska entered the No Spin Zone and elaborated on the sordid situation. "ICE has had three different stories," Sasse groused, "and none of them has made any sense. They say they didn't have enough time and then that they didn't have enough resources, they keep shifting and none of the excuses are good enough. Lawlessness is corrosive to a civil society, this undermines the public's confidence in government." The Factor denounced Nebraska Judge Jeffrey Marcuzzo, who went very easy on the drunk driver: "The judge had to know this guy was a flight risk - he was from Honduras and his blood alcohol level was through the roof."
What the Heck Just Happened? 
The Trump Phenomenon
Guys Friday Greg Gutfeld and Bernard McGuirk showed up one day early to opine about accusations that Donald Trump is a racist. "There is plenty to criticize Donald Trump about without calling him a bigot," Gutfeld said, "but that seems to be the reliable defense when you're a leftist because you know it's a permanent smear. I'd rather go after Donald Trump with simple questions, I'd like to ask him how much an iPhone would cost if it was made in the United States. The answer is $2,000." McGuirk smacked conservatives for joining the 'racist' bandwagon. "Using the racist crap is the last refuge of a skunk with no argument. You have people like Mitt Romney talking about Trump's bigotry, Glenn Beck calling him a Nazi, and Charles Krauthammer blaming Trump for the Brownshirt bullies storming the rally in Chicago."
Watters' World 
Combining Watters and Heavy Metal
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Jesse Watters took in a concert put on by the venerable heavy metal band Megadeth. Here is what a few of the fans told him about the political scene: "I'm not voting for anyone" ... "I think Donald Trump just wants to rule everyone with an iron fist" ... "Bernie is a lot more honest than Hillary Clinton." Back in the studio, Watters described the crowd. "There were some old fogies there collecting dust, but I just talked to the people who looked fresher. I got trampled a little bit, my cameraman fell down, and we got thrown out because I was a 'security risk.'"
