Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
The political march continues for the Dems
"Right now Senators Obama and Clinton are basically tied, which is truly amazing after all the campaigning. The Democratic Party's most powerful media arm, the New York Times, scolded Hillary Clinton for going negative on Obama. The Times is not in the tank for Obama, but it recognizes the damage being done to the Democratic Party itself. And since the New York Times is in business basically to promote that party to power, it wants no internal warfare. It will be interesting now to see how the pro-Obama media tries to re-ignite the senator's candidacy. For example, NBC News has basically set itself up as Obama headquarters and now must figure out a way to help him. Both Hillary Clinton and Barack Obama have big negatives, but both have shown pluck and determination, and the only thing that separates them is personality and style. So again, it's a tie, and will likely stay that way until the convention in August. I see chaos rather than clarity for the Democrats. Somewhere John McCain is smiling."

News Link: NYT dismisses Hillary's win

The Factor was joined by former Bush strategist Karl Rove, who theorized that Barack Obama was badly wounded by his remarks about "bitter" working-class Pennsylvanians. "In the manufacturing towns," Rove pointed out, "Hillary Clinton got 61% of the vote. And if you look at the 50 smallest counties in the state, home to the people Senator Obama was talking about, she won 65% of the vote. So it had a big impact because it resonated with what people thought was an arrogant and condescending tone toward people like them." Rove then looked ahead to the next two primaries. "Indiana has a significantly larger black population than Pennsylvania, but there are also a lot of rural, small-town, blue-collar Democrats who are culturally conservative. And it's smart on Clinton's part to give up on North Carolina. In the Democratic primary it's probably 40% or more African American, and she just doesn't have the money."

News Link: Obama suffers from working class blues
Top Story II 
Dick Morris on the primaries
The Factor's version of Murderer's Row continued with strategist Dick Morris, who maintained that Hillary Clinton's chance of winning the nomination is approximately zero. "This race is over," Morris declared. "Obama is going to have 140 more elected delegates than she will, and the superdelegates know that if they take this nomination away from the guy who won the most elected delegates, there will be a war. Howard Dean, Al Gore, John Edwards, and Nancy Pelosi will tell the superdelegates to declare by June 15th because they can't let this go to the convention." The Factor wasn't quite buying Morris' conclusion. "You're basically saying all these millions are being spent and all the angst is a facade, a total waste of time."
Top Story III 
Jesse Jackson, Jr. on the Democratic race
Congressman Jesse Jackson, Jr., an Obama supporter, entered the No Spin Zone with his post-Pennsylvania analysis. "At no point did anyone expect Barack Obama to win the state," Jackson averred. "He was down by nearly 25 points and he managed to cut that in half. And his delegate lead makes it virtually impossible for Hillary Clinton to pass him." Jackson assessed the damage done by Obama's past association with Reverend Jeremiah Wright and his comments about people who "cling to" religion and guns. "Certainly the Wright comments did not help and the San Francisco speech did not help. But Barack Obama continued his message of hope and exceeded expectations." The Factor reminded Jackson that Wright will appear on PBS later this week. "You know Wright, you know Chicago, and you know he's not helping your guy. I'm just flabbergasted that Wright doesn't take a vacation for eight months."
Personal Story Segment 
Why politicians can't change oil prices
The Factor explored the record high price of oil with commodities expert John D'Agostino, who blamed the immutable law of supply and demand. "We have a weak dollar," D'Agostino said, "we have a high global demand. The price of oil is set on exchanges and the free markets are right now setting the price of crude oil at about $120 a barrel, and gasoline prices are directly related. The only thing we can do is use less energy." The Factor urged viewers to send a message by driving less. "Shell and ExxonMobil are making record profits, so they're prospering from the chaos. I say the folks should be angry about that and should cut back 10 percent of their driving."

Miller Time Segment 
Dennis Miller on politics and pain
Dennis Miller began his observations with Hillary Clinton's victory in the Keystone State. "What we've learned," Miller quipped, "is that you can never count Hillary Clinton out of a fight unless it's Iraq, in which case you can count her out of a fight. I think black people in this country are about to get piled on again, and the Democrats are going to try to take this away from Barack Obama." The Factor then showed Miller a commercial ridiculing John McCain's age, which was produced by a new anti-McCain group funded by George Soros. "I don't think they're getting any bang for their money," Miller said. "Yeah, some people might view John McCain as turgid, and some people might view Barack Obama as callow, but young doesn't necessarily play with me." The Factor encouraged Miller, who just had his appendix removed, to donate the inconsequential organ to his friend Bill Maher, perhaps hoping to infuse the Catholic-bashing Maher with some common sense.

Back of Book Segment 
Policing the 'Net: Miley Cyrus embarrassment?
Internet watchdog Mary Katharine Ham reported on the mildly risqu photos of teen star Miley Cyrus circulating on the web. "She's a good kid," Ham said, "and she has done a good job of not making a spectacle out of herself thus far. Her stardom is predicated on being this clean-cut kid, and I hope she sees that as the way to go." Ham also commented on Michael Moore, whose web site accused Hillary Clinton of "smearing the black man." "Moore wants to make an impact," Ham joked, "because he had so much success in getting guns out of the country and defeating Bush. The super-liberal latte drinkers that love Michael Moore are the Obama base, but elderly voters really dislike Moore. This is not a great move for Moore or Obama." Ham then showed the disturbing video of a little boy threatening to kill President Bush. "This was banned by YouTube, but then showed up on a hip-hop web site. It's eight minutes of this kid ranting and it's really disgusting." The Factor urged the feds to get involved. "The Secret Service should arrest the parents of this kid and the purveyor of the web site. This is child abuse."
