Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
What do you do with a crazy Congressman?
"Last year the good people of Orlando, Florida elected Alan Grayson to serve in Congress. Since that time, Mr. Grayson has developed a reputation as a nut, a guy who's simply out of control. Grayson called Linda Robertson, an aide to Ben Bernanke, a 'whore.' He also said the Republican health care plan is for people to 'die quickly,' and called Fox News 'the enemy of America.' During a stop in Florida last night, President Obama called Grayson a 'wonderful elected official' and an 'outstanding member of Congress.' Grayson is an embarrassment, and the president doesn't seem to understand that radical people are not acceptable to most Americans. The White House is furious with Fox News for reporting on far left guys like Congressman Grayson, but radicalism seems to be acceptable to the president, and that is becoming a major problem."

For a different take on the Grayson story, The Factor welcomed Democratic Congressmen Anthony Wiener and Joe Sestak. "I think Alan Grayson is a good Congressman," Weiner said. "He went too far with his comments and he would admit that, but he's making an argument about health care that is not wrong, that the Republican plan of doing nothing is indeed killing people." Congressman Sestak lamented the huge gulf separating Democrats and Republicans on Capitol Hill. "It's the Hatfields and McCoys here and I don't understand it. This is why Americans are losing trust and confidence in our public officials." The Factor urged President Obama to distance himself from bomb-throwers like Grayson: "President Obama seems to love these radical guys, which hurts him with moderates. It is outrageous when the president has a resume of people he associates with who are far-left kooks. You guys are telling me this should be a more civil country - shouldn't that come from the top?"
Stossel Matters Segment 
Public option is now back on the table
Fox News anchor John Stossel opined on the resurrection of the so-called "public option," under which the federal government will compete with private insurance companies. "Obama's absolutely right that the current system is unsustainable," Stossel began, "but Obamacare would make it worse. They want to say insurance companies have to cover everybody, which is like telling car insurance companies that Lindsay Lohan and I have to pay the same premium. There are people who are truly desperate and need some government help, but most people are not in that category and more government makes it worse. One thing I've learned in forty years of reporting is that the free market makes everything better. We have to pay for more of our own health care with medical savings accounts and higher deductibles."
Barack and a Hard Place Segment 
Another radical Obama appointee surfaces
Obama-watchers Alan Colmes and Monica Crowley explored the president's nomination of Judge Edward Chen for a spot on the Federal District Court. "Chen was an ACLU lawyer for a long time," Crowley reported, "and he has basically accused the United States of being a country of bigots and racists. Based on his statements and his previous rulings, this guy is using the bench to do social engineering." Colmes, however, defended the Chen nomination. "I don't think he is a radical. The ACLU has defended Oliver North and Rush Limbaugh and the Second Amendment. The ACLU is totally mainstream - this is another attempt to besmirch another Obama appointee and President Obama himself."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Racial tension brewing over hotelier's decisions
New Mexico hotel owner Larry Whitten is under fire for ordering his Hispanic workers to speak English and Anglicize their names. The Factor framed the issue this way: "There's no doubt this is reprehensible - you don't ask an American citizen whose name is 'Marcos' to change his name to 'Mark.' That's degrading and demeaning and unacceptable. However, the law is on Whitten's side." But attorney Jennifer Smetters argued that Whitten may be vulnerable to a civil suit. "There has to be a safety issue for English-only to be legal, and it has to affect the efficiency of the business. But what we have here is a gentleman who doesn't want anything but English spoken in his presence. To me, that says he has a cultural bias - this is not about efficiency, it's nothing but his own personal preference."
'Is it Legal?' Segment 
Orly Taitz fined by Georgia court
FNC legal analysts Lis Wiehl and Kimberly Guilfoyle looked at the case of California lawyer Orly Taitz, a leader of the "birther" movement that claims President Obama is not a natural-born citizen. "Taitz represented a Georgia Army officer," Wiehl explained, "who said he wouldn't be deployed to Iraq because President Obama is not legitimate. The judge dismissed the case as frivolous and fined her $20,000." Guilfoyle turned to Jennifer Lopez and her ongoing feud with former husband Ojani Noa. "This guy is a shakedown artist who should get a life and quit trying to live off her. He wants to make a movie about his life with Jennifer Lopez, which he's calling a 'parody.' He's not going to be allowed to do it." The legal duo also examined the case of Manuel Vega, a Florida father who, along with a friend, brutally beat another man who allegedly molested Vega's 3-year-old son. "This is a crime of passion," Wiehl said, "and if this ever gets to court a jury will give him a pass." But The Factor argued against Vega's brand of vigilantism: "I am obviously sympathetic to the father, but authorities have to prosecute this. You can't have two guys beating another guy into a coma with concrete slabs."
Back of Book Segment 
Great American News Quiz
Quiz Kids Martha MacCallum and Steve Doocy met again in the Great American News Quiz. Among the questions: "What was Elvis' first Grammy Award-winning record?" ... "Which Fox News anchor had a run-in with a pro wrestler back in the 1980's?" ... "Who was president during the last large outbreak of the swine flu?" The contest came down to the final question, which both contestants muffed. Doocy, however, regained his title by winning the nerve-wracking tie breaker. The entire GANQ can be found here on BillOReilly.com under 'Fun Stuff."
Pinheads and Patriots 
Ruben Navarrette & paparazzi pursuers
Tuesday's Patriot: Syndicated columnist Ruben Navarrette, who defended Juan Williams against the racial insults that have been hurled his way. And the Pinheads: The paparazzi whose pursuit of Brad Pitt caused the actor to fall from his motorcycle.
