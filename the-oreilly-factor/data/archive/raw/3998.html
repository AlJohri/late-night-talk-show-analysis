Talking Points Memo 
Trumpians vs Democrats
Watch more video in the Video Center...
"As I mentioned this week, Donald Trump really isn't a Republican - he's a Trumpian, marching to his own beat. Trump's timing is good because this presidential cycle should spell doom for the Democratic Party, which has lurched so far left most Americans are appalled. Some facts: Despite spending trillions trying to redistribute wealth, the poverty rate for women, blacks and other groups has gone up since President Obama took office. Overseas, most Americans believe the Iranian nuke deal is bad, while ISIS continues to run wild. Iraq has collapsed, Afghanistan may follow, Ukraine has been battered by Putin, and the Middle East is one giant mess. Here at home, Democrats oppose keeping illegal immigrants out of the USA by building an effective wall and they oppose holding foreign nationals who commit violent crimes responsible. Dems even oppose investigating Planned Parenthood for alleged illegal activities by selling the organs of babies. All of those things should make it easy for the Republicans to win the presidency, but that is not how things are shaking down. Donald Trump has bludgeoned the GOP establishment by seizing on a number of emotional issues, and his brand of straight talk blows traditional politicians away, making them seem small and dull. But the GOP may have a very hard time coming together when the nominee is selected. Also, there is an over-the-top element to Trump's support. As we have reported, there is no question that Supreme Court decisions have upheld that portion of the 14th Amendment that says any person born in the USA is entitled to citizenship. Some are trying to parse the amendment, but it is a waste of time. It also gives the left ammunition. The left is hoping to turn defeat into victory by portraying Trump and the GOP as racist brutalizers. There is a way to legally revoke the anchor baby law, and it should be done as it is undermining federal immigration law. But there's also a way for Republicans to lose the upcoming election if they out-extreme their far-left competition."
Top Story 
14th Amendment Debate
The Factor asked constitutional scholars David Rivkin and John Yoo for their opinions on the 'anchor baby' controversy. "Donald Trump is flat wrong," Yoo proclaimed. "The text of the 14th Amendment is clear and this has been the rule in American history. Everyone born in this country is an American citizen." Rivkin urged Trump's Republican rivals to confront him on this issue. "We have a number of presidential candidates, including Jeb Bush, who are taking a robust position against this. This is a great opportunity to differentiate themselves from the rest of the pack." The Factor added, "The New York Times and others on the far left are going to use this issue to say you can not give power to Trump and conservative Republicans."
Campaign 2016 
Illegal immigration Battle
Watch more video in the Video Center...
Senator Marco Rubio, a leading Republican candidate, also weighed in on the anchor baby situation. "I do not support repealing the 14th Amendment," he said, "and the prevailing belief is that anyone born in the United States is a citizen. But it's a legitimate issue that families are coming in to have a child so that child has citizenship. I am open to exploring ways to look into that." Rubio also commented on Donald Trump's effect on the GOP. "In 2008, 2010, and 2014 the American people voted to change things and nothing has changed. They're angry and Donald Trump has tapped into that. That should motivate us, but we can't allow anger to define the Republican Party or our nation. This is by definition an optimistic country."
Campaign 2016 
Clinton Favorability
With Hillary Clinton's favorability ratings plummeting, The Factor spoke with Fox News correspondent Ed Henry about reports that Joe Biden may enter the race. "Right now Joe Biden has a sympathy factor," Henry said, "because his son died at such an early age. There is also the factor that he is seen as someone who connects with voters, unlike Hillary Clinton. But as people dig in, they will see that he is gaffe-prone and he doesn't have any money." Henry also dismissed rumors that President Obama is working against Hillary Clinton behind the scenes, saying, "I'm not convinced that the Obama White House is going after Hillary Clinton."
Personal Story 
Dr. Cornel West on Black Lives Matter
Watch more video in the Video Center...
The Factor welcomed professor Cornel West, a vocal supporter of the Black Lives Matter movement. "We need jobs with a living wage and decent housing," West said, "but you can't overlook the legacy of white supremacy. I am a revolutionary Christian who looks at the world through the vantage point of the weak and the vulnerable, and I want to make sure that every person made in the image of God can live a life of decency. You can't do that when 40% of your children are living in poverty. Money goes to the military instead of education, and 1% of the population has 42% of the wealth!" The Factor urged West to focus on fatherlessness: "I want you people in the Black Lives Matter movement to begin with the fabric of the black family. You can't continue to have 75% of black babies being born out of wedlock!"
Impact Segment 
Obama vs. Hillary?
Monica Crowley has theorized that President Obama is hoping that Hillary Clinton is forced out of the presidential race and that Joe Biden jumps in. She entered the No Spin Zone to elaborate. "There is a long history of bad blood between the Obamas and the Clintons," she said, "and there is a more important reason why Barack Obama does not want Hillary Clinton to be the nominee. He has been 'joking' about wanting a third term, and the only way he can get that is by installing his vice president." Fox News contributor Meghan McCain essentially agreed that President Obama prefers Joe Biden to Hillary Clinton. "If the Clinton email scandal blows back," McCain said, "it could be the Watergate of our generation and it would reflect on him. She did this under his watch and I don't think Monica is being a conspiracy theorist."
Watters' World 
Best of Watters
The Factor introduced a few of Jesse Watters' greatest hits from recent months. In June, Watters headed to DC to find out what terms are acceptable and which are offensive. One young man took umbrage at the adjective "dashing," while a woman said complimenting a female on her legs is downright offensive. Watters then ventured to Big Sur, long known as a capital of 'new age' philosophy. Some locals expressed rather unconventional views: "We're living off the land and not eating animals" ... "We should make everything out of hemp" ... "I don't know how I feel about borders, but I believe in love." One Big Sur old-timer offered Watters the ultimate compliment, saying, "You're amusing and gloriously non-combative."
