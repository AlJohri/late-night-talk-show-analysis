Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
President Obama and leadership
"Unless you are a hard-core Obama hater, you have to feel a bit sorry for the president. On his first day of vacation, his Attorney General announces an investigation of the CIA, something Obama has publicly stated he doesn't want. Then the administration announced the deficit is $2 trillion more than the White House thought it was. Some analysts believe the president has not just lost control of the health care situation; he has lost control of just about everything. That's not exactly fair, but it's true that President Obama's leadership is being tested in a major way. Health care is in chaos, Afghanistan and Iraq are getting worse, the deficit is $2 trillion more than anybody thought, and Gallup reports that consumer confidence in the economy continues to drop. It seems to me it would be very hard to relax on vacation with all that staring you in the face."

The Factor probed the prospective CIA investigation with Reva Bhalla of Stratfor.com and Peter Brookes of the conservative Heritage Foundation. "This may be a victory for the left," Brookes said, "but it is certainly not a victory for our country. It's going to throw people in the intelligence community off their game - they are worried about a witch hunt, which will make them risk averse out in the field, and it will be a major distraction for CIA director Leon Panetta." Bhalla focused on the deteriorating situation in Afghanistan and Iraq. "In Afghanistan you have an extremely fragile government, an increasingly sophisticated insurgency, and it will take a lot of time. The Taliban may live in caves, but they understand what drives U.S. politics. And in Iraq, the United States is walking on eggshells trying to keep together the very fragile power-sharing agreement among the Sunnis, the Shi'a and the Kurds. Any way you look at it, the United States will not be free of the Iraq problem any time soon."
Impact Segment 
Is single-payer health care constitutional?
Some legal scholars contend that government-mandated health insurance may be unconstitutional. The Factor explored that issue with FNC legal analysts Lis Wiehl and Megyn Kelly. "There are some real questions," Kelly stated, "and they've been totally ignored by most of the media. Everyone assumes that President Obama and the Democrats can say we have to get health insurance, but there is a real question about whether Congress has the power to do that." Wiehl theorized that government-mandated health insurance will pass constitutional muster. "I say it is constitutional. Article One of the Constitution says Congress has the power to regulate interstate commerce, and the medical supplies and equipment go through various states." But The Factor argued that "Obama-care" faces legal hurdles: "The federal government doesn't have the power to force an American to buy anything. I'm going on record and saying this is unconstitutional."
Weekdays with Bernie Segment 
The Dan Rather scandal: More to the story?
FNC media analyst Bernie Goldberg entered the No Spin Zone with a revelation about former CBS anchor Dan Rather and his producer Mary Mapes, who aired a report claiming that President Bush pulled strings to avoid serving in Vietnam. "Dan Rather went on the air," Goldberg recalled, "with the story that George W. Bush joined the Air National Guard to avoid going to Vietnam. But I looked at the CBS investigation of this, and it says Mary Mapes knew in advance of putting the story on the air that George Bush volunteered to go to Vietnam and fly combat missions. The central point of the CBS piece was that he tried to avoid service in Vietnam, but she knew he volunteered and didn't share it with us. She had an obligation to put that in the story and she didn't."
Personal Story Segment 
Obesity, PETA and your rights
The animal rights group PETA has put up a billboard ridiculing obese people and encouraging them to go vegetarian. The Factor tackled the weighty issue with FNC analyst Tammy Bruce and radio talk show host Leslie Marshall. "PETA is in the market of shock value," Marshall said, "and this is very shocking and effective. If even one person eats less meat for health reasons, this could have a good outcome." But Bruce accused PETA of nutritional deception. "Diets with animal protein are where you can lose weight. Just look at the panda bears - they're vegans and they need to lose some weight. And people need to ask whether there are business interests associated with what PETA and other left-wing non-profit groups are promoting." The Factor predicted an escalating war against heavy folks: "People choosing to be a little hefty are going to be taxed - there will be taxes on sugar and soda and fast food."
Miller Time Segment 
The best of Dennis Miller
With Dennis Miller on vacation, The Factor re-aired some of his greatest hits. This was Miller on Barney Frank's recent visit to the No Spin Zone: "Did you say you two might go camping together? If you do, I want you to invite Dick Cheney and me because I'll be laughing so hard that Cheney will have to shoot me in the head to put me down." Miller on a proposed tax on plastic surgery: "I don't want Pelosi and Biden making calls on plastic surgery - if she were pulled any tighter she'd be an unopened Braunschweiger tube, and he looks like his head was attacked by a squid in a Jules Verne novel." And Miller on the suggestion that hot dogs carry a warning label: "I know hot dogs are crap, that's what I dig about them. I don't need a warning label, and when people who smoke cigarettes say there was no warning label, you want to say, if you didn't know smoking was bad for you, you're lying through the hole in your trachea. I'm going to start eating hot dogs with cigarettes in them."
Back of Book Segment 
Body language: Bill Maher, Michael Vick
Body language interpreter Tonya Reiman analyzed a clip of Bill Maher giving an on-air political lecture to Conan O'Brien. "O'Brien was crouched down," Reiman observed, "and it looked like he was saying to himself, what happened to my power, where did it go. Bill Maher was very dominant in this debate and he was angrier." Reiman also watched Michael Vick expressing remorse for his involvement in dog fighting. "He stuck his tongue out three times, which typically could be a cue for deception. And he uses the phrase 'you know' five times in 25 seconds, which is a sign of vulnerability. My impression is that he is ashamed that others viewed him as being wrong."
Pinheads and Patriots 
Nick Jonas & Bill Maher
Tuesday's Patriot: 16-year-old teen idol Nick Jonas, who eloquently addressed the National Press Club. And the Pinhead: Bill Maher, who defended his calling America a "stupid country."
