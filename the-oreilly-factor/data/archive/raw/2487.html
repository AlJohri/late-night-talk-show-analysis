Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Should Dems & Republicans alike be scorned?
"According to a recent Fox News poll, just 26% of the American people have a favorable view of Congress. That means both Democrats and Republicans have lost an amazing amount of credibility. A new Rasmussen poll confirms the disenchantment: If 'tea party' protesters organize a new party, they would get 23% of the vote. Sarah Palin would be a natural to head the 'tea party' because she's a small-government populist with a charismatic common touch. The pinheads on the far left mock Governor Palin, but she does command attention and can only get stronger on the national stage. But a third party deal could help Barack Obama - if the 'tea party' becomes a reality, it would take votes away from the Republicans. There's no question that millions of Americans are fed up with both established parties and want something new and vibrant that reflects their values and beliefs. So three cheers for three parties. Whatever the new party is called, it can't do any worse than the old parties."

The Factor welcomed former Speaker Newt Gingrich who assessed the viability of a third party. "There's a tremendous upsurge of anger and resentment and fear," Gingrich said. "People feel the Republicans haven't provided the right leadership, and they are bitterly opposed to the liberal Democrats. The third party movement is not about Sarah Palin, it's about an enormous tidal wave of Americans who are in a rage over government spending, taxes, the arrogance of politicians, and corruption. But the challenge is that third parties are traditionally protest vehicles, not a path to power." The Factor reiterated that a third party movement would help re-elect President Obama. "The only politician in America who can pull that third party together is Sarah Palin. Through sheer force of personality, she could make the third party a reality, which would make Barack Obama very happy."
Impact Segment 
Winning the war in Afghanistan
Less than a week after President Obama said withdrawal from Afghanistan will begin in 18 months, his top aides are backtracking from a definitive timeline. The Factor scrutinized the Afghanistan strategy with Fox News analysts Juan Williams and Mary Katharine Ham. "I think it's important for President Obama to say this withdrawal is 'etched in stone,'" Ham said. "His anti-war left hears that and is happier. But Secretary of Defense Gates and General Petraeus are saying there is not a deadline. So there are a lot of different nuances." Williams criticized the president for trying to pacify everybody. "This was mush from the middle. He was trying to give something to the Europeans, but at the same time say it's a vital national interest to send more troops. You can't have it both ways, it's lacking in logic." The Factor added that President Obama's straddle was entirely predictable: "I said from the jump that this deadline was bull. It was pure theater to placate the Europeans and the far left. And when you send out so many mixed signals, you erode confidence in your leadership."
The Hume Zone Segment 
Global warming conference kicks off in Copenhagen
World leaders have convened in Denmark for a global summit on climate change. Fox News analyst Brit Hume entered the No Spin Zone and questioned the very premise of the gathering. "The Earth has been warming and cooling since the inception of the planet," Hume opined, "and what's dubious is whether man-made emissions are the principle cause of that. An awful lot is being put at stake based on an idea that man is responsible for global warming and we should make these tremendous adjustments in the way we live. That's a very risky proposition." The Factor reported that President Obama will attend the summit next week: "Barack Obama is going over there to lend his prestige to this conference. I'm not sure it will help him in America, but it will help him overseas."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Italian court finds American student guilty of murder
22-year-old Seattle college student Amanda Knox has been sentenced to 26 years after being found guilty of sexually assaulting and murdering her roommate in Italy. The Factor spoke about the case with CBS correspondent Peter Van Sant, who vehemently argued Knox's innocence. "There is a total lack of evidence," Van Sant maintained. "The case began when whe was grilled for fourteen hours with no food, no water, and no representation. This interrogation was done by people who are trained to break the Mafia. The prosecutor says there was a knife that had Amanda's DNA on the handle and the victim's DNA on the blade. We had nine DNA experts look at this, and they said this would never be admissible in the United States. With all my journalistic profession and all my integrity, this woman is absolutely innocent."
Weekdays with Bernie Segment 
Climate change and the media
The Factor asked FNC media analyst Bernie Goldberg to evaluate coverage of the "global warming" theory. "A few years ago," Goldberg recalled, "Scott Pelley of '60 Minutes' did a totally one-sided story on global warming. He later said, 'If I interview a Holocaust survivor, does that mean I have to run out and interview a Holocaust denier?' That tells you a lot about how they see this. They confuse what they want to be with the facts, and they ignore things that don't fit the liberal agenda." Goldberg shifted to the CBS promo in which 'Frosty the Snowman' discusses his 'porn collection' and sexual conquests. "I'm not against this on moral grounds," Goldberg said, "but I'm against it because I think it's pathetic when a corporation tries to be cool and hip by having 'Frosty' talk about his porn collection. This is like some old guy going to a club wearing tight jeans and a backwards baseball cap. It's just pathetic."
Back of Book Segment 
Reality Check: Bill on Good Morning America
The Factor devoted Reality Check to his Monday appearance on Good Morning, during which he graded Barack Obama on three issues. First, health care: "He gets a D. You can't be putting out a 2,000 page bill and not be able to explain it. I want to understand the bill that's going to cost a trillion dollars." Next, jobs: "We had chaos and Obama stepped in and stabilized the economy to some extent. So I'm giving him a 'B' on the jobs front." Finally, Afghanistan: "He gets a C because it took him far too long to make the decision. That helped the Taliban and rattled Pakistan. And his speech at West Point was not good - cadets were falling asleep." The Factor also generously handed out B+ grades to GMA hosts Robin Roberts, Diane Sawyer, and Chris Cuomo simply for showing up so early in the morning.
Pinheads and Patriots 
Jeopardy! & Aretha Franklin
Monday's Patriots: Jeopardy and host Alex Trebek, who included a question about Bill in a recent program. And the Pinhead: Perhaps "Queen of Soul" Aretha Franklin, who looked like she was being swallowed up by her massive fur coat at last week's Rockefeller Center Tree Lighting.
