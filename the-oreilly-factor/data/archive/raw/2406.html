Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
Democrats on the attack
"White House Press Secretary Robert Gibbs keeps saying that the town halls are not 'representative.' It's just all a lot of 'chatter.' And Team Obama says that the people are just 'misinformed.' The left has spent a lot of time, effort, and money trying to discredit the town hallers as the yahoo racist fringe. Well this strategy has clearly backfired. Democrats have been caught flat-footed by this popular uprising, but all those Harvard grads at the White House should have known better. Powerful arguments against socialized medicine have been around for decades. Independents, moderates, and now even Democrats who are senior citizens are saying no to a government managed healthcare system. Their worries are real, and while violence is never justified, the passion certainly is. Until people see real signs that President Obama is taking their concerns seriously, these protests are going to continue, regardless of how well-produced the President's own town hall events are. Simply put, the country is nowhere near as left-wing as President Obama obviously is, and he never had a mandate for this type of radical change."

Laura brought in radio talk show host Tammy Bruce and Democratic strategist Tara Dowell to analyze President Obama's town hall meeting on August 14 in Belgrade, Montana. Dowell confidently felt that Obama took on his health care critics today: "I think he did an excellent job. I think this should have been done a lot sooner, and I think he laid out the compelling reasons why we need healthcare reform. Bruce explained that even if Obama was successful today, he has difficult road ahead of him. "What the Obama administration is unable to understand is that conservatives have never really responded like this before. They're [the White House] mistaking this as some kind of organized effort, whereas in fact it is organic, they don't understand how deep it is, and they have no plan B, so the result has been to malign the average American citizen." Laura agreed with Bruce, saying that Obama is having trouble communicating his plan to the American people. "Something is not working here with what they're doing. It's not getting through to the average person out there that this new government program is going to be different from all the other government programs that are inefficient, waste money, and that end up cutting services to people who they promise to take care of."
Impact Segment 
Explaining the White House's unsolicited emails
In attempting to understand why the White House is sending unsolicited emails to average Americans, Laura welcomed Stephen Hayes, a Senior Writer at The Weekly Standard. While Hayes believes that it is not likely the White House is undergoing a specific campaign to obtain email addresses of Americans that are skeptical of health care reform, he was disturbed by Press Secretary Robert Gibbs' reaction to Fox News White House Correspondent Major Garrett's question regarding the matter. "The fact is that Robert Gibbs could have said 'Hey, look, I don't know the answer, I'll get it for you and we can move on.' Laura agreed that not only should have Gibbs reacted in a different way, but that important questions remain regarding the health care bill and gathering of emails by the Obama administration. "There is a data keeping issue involving health care in the bill and then this email collection pops up, so I think there are a number of issues that make people very uncomfortable." Laura also asked Hayes how President Obama can rebrand the healthcare campaign considering a continuing lack of public support for reform. "They know how to put President Obama in front of the teleprompter and in front of crowds, and they know how to attack people who aren't saying things as polished as they should be. That strategy worked for them, but I think they overlearned that lesson; they're trying to apply it now but it just doesn't work."
Unresolved Problems Segment 
Pres. Clinton heckled at left-wing conference
At the far-left Netroots Conference in Pittsburgh, former President Bill Clinton took on a heckler that interrogated Clinton on "Don't Ask, Don't Tell" while also stating that President Obama may need to compromise in healthcare reform. Laura asked Fox News Analyst Dick Morris to share his general reactions to the Netroots Conference on August 13. "The compromises they are talking about are irrelevant to the reasons the elderly oppose this bill. The fundamental problem with this bill that exists in any of the compromises is that you're trying to treat too many people with too few doctors." Laura agreed with Morris's analysis, further adding that "the common sense angle here is that people don't believe you can expand coverage to this extent without having to ration care. That just doesn't make sense to people." Morris also commented on whether Obama is being successful in rebranding the healthcare debate. "No, absolutely not. What he's trying to do is to make the insurance companies and special interests the villains when the insurance companies, the drug companies, and all of the various vendors are in support of the plan because they received their payoffs. There are no special interests opposing this plan, just a couple of ten million mad elderly people."
Personal Story Segment 
The Sarah Palin effect in the health care debate
Because of Sarah Palin's outcry over possible "death panels" in the healthcare reform bill, key Senators are now excluding an end-of-life-care provision from their legislation. Laura was joined by Amanda Carpenter, a reporter for The Washington Times and Mathew Littman, former speechwriter for Joe Biden, to discuss Sarah Palin's influence on the healthcare debate. "I know of no other Republican that has been able to break through the medical debate over this issue. She got President Obama to talk about death panels and Grandma in the same sentence," Carpenter said. Littman, on the other hand, argued that Sarah Palin does not represent a credible voice on the national stage. "I have to tell you I don't read Sarah Palin's Facebook page and I don't follow her on Twitter. And when she's on VH1 dating Flavor Flav, which is what I'm expecting in a couple of years, then I'll follow Sarah Palin. In the meantime, I don't take her seriously." Laura was offended by Littman's characterization of Palin. "I think your little comment about her just now reveals how positively desperate the Democrats look. I'm not surprised that Mathew took it there because she's obviously resonating with Americans on this issue."
Miller Time Segment 
A weekend dose of Dennis
Laura introduced a few of Dennis Miller's greatest moments on his visit to the No Spin Zone. Bill and Miller discussed the controversy surrounding Somali pirates capturing merchant ships off the coast of Africa. "It just cracks me up how we're throwing the word 'pirate' around now and they won't say 'terrorist.' I'm watching Gibbs's daily press conference and he has an eye patch, a parrot on his shoulder, screaming 'Somali want a cracker?' and yet they won't call terrorists 'terrorists.'" Miller moved to President Obama shaking hands and receiving a book from Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez. "Listen, if I were President, I would say that if this guy tries to get anywhere near me with a book, I'd want him tackled, I'd want to duck and cover like the old bomb shelter thing." Finally, Miller commented on rumors that he would be appearing on Dancing with the Stars. "I thought you would be on next year with Barney Frank?" Miller asked.
Unresolved Problems Segment II 
Democrats losing major support
According to a new Fox News poll, the Democrats are losing support in the 2010 Congressional elections. Today, 40% of Americans are planning to vote for Democrats and 40% are planning to vote for Republicans. Democratic strategist Richard Goodstein joined Laura to talk about whether the latest poll is foreboding for Democrats. "It is shocking that it is still 40-40 considering we've got 9.4% unemployment, we've got an economy that is still pretty much cresting up but slightly. This is obviously something that the President inherited, I know people kind of balk at that, but he did," Goodstein. Laura quipped that the falling poll numbers are predominantly based on the name-calling and belittling of American citizens that are protesting over healthcare reform. "We have people organizing. They might not agree with President Obama's agenda, but they are organizing. Why isn't this celebrated? Why is it constantly dismissed as irrelevant by this White House?" Goodstein responded, stating that the town hall protests are signs of desperation by conservatives. "I think what you're seeing with the outrage at the town halls is powerlessness. Their party is not in power and they don't like it," he said.

The best of Reality Check
Finally, Laura introduced a compilation of some of Bill's best Reality Check segments. Some of the memorable "Checks" included Sharon Osborne, a judge on VH1's "Rock of Love: Charm School" tossing a drink onto a contestant, Kathie Lee Gifford breaking a piata on the "Today" show, and Britney Spears addressing Fox News Anchor Megyn Kelly over some racy lyrics in one of Spears's songs. And this was Bill's reaction to a 911 call made by a Texas woman because she didn't have enough shrimp in her shrimp fried rice meal. "Yeah, get the shrimp police out there! Now there's a new dish on the menu: pinhead fried rice."
