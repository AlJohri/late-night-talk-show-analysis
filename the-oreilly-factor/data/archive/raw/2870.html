Talking Points Memo & Top Story 
President Obama and the Jewish vote
"In 2008 Jewish voters supported Barack Obama big time, but now there may be truble in the Jewish precincts. Yesterday the President told the world that he favors Israel pulling back to its 1967 borders; predictably, Israel says that's not going to happen. Although Jewish Americans account for just two percent of the elctorate, they do give a lot of money to Democrats and that might be at risk. The actual negotiations between the Palestinians and Israel will probably not get anywhere no matter what Mr. Obama proposes; there is simply too much money involved in the conflict. Since 1993 the U.S. has committed more than $3.5 billion to the Palestinians, yet most of the people over there remain devoid of services and are dirt poor. Many believe much of the money is stolen. There is no denying that groups like Hamas are able to raise millions of dollars by keeping the conflict with Israel alive, so there is a vested interest in war. Talking Points hates to be cynical, but there is a better chance of Iran joining NATO than any deal between Israel and the Palestinian Authority. That's just the way it is."

The Factor analyzed the Israel situation with pro-Israeli activist Brooke Goldstein and liberal talk radio host Mark Levine. "Barack Obama has dug himself a very deep hole," Goldstein asserted. "In one fell swoop he has completly reversed American foreign policy and he has told the Jewish community that he is going to renege on his promises to Israel. I don't think he understands the roots of the Israeli-Arab conflict." Levine agreed that President Obama has damaged his standing with American Jews. "I'm still going to support Barack Obama because I'm a good liberal, but the President is kind of talking out of both sides of his mouth and is being a little naive. He's looking for a middle position that doesn't exist." The Factor portrayed the conflict as intractable: "Jewish people over the centuries have been chased out of countries and exterminated and now you have a President of the United States saying things no other President has said. I don't see a solution to this, ever."
Personal Story Segment 
Huckabee on a Mitt Romney run
The Factor was joined by Fox News host Mike Huckabee, who put forth an uncomplimentary assessment of Republican front-runner Mitt Romney. "We have some very strong philosophical differences," Huckabee stated. "I felt he had a lack of authenticity on sancity of life and same-sex marriage and guns, issues that really matter to people in the Midwest and South. But we've made peace and he'll be a better president on any day than Barack Obama. If he is the nominee, I'll be out there supporting him." Huckabee assessed the impact of his own decision to drop out of the race. "There is no heir apparent for the people who supported me, all the Republicans have the chance to scramble for those votes."
Fridays with Geraldo Segment 
Strauss-Kahn put on house arrest, granted $1M bail
Geraldo Rivera, who has been looking into the case of former IMF boss Dominique Strauss-Kahn, reported on DSK's checkered past. "His nickname is 'the great seducer,'" Rivera said. "He's a horndog and he has the reputation of being extremely aggressive with women who work at the International Monetary Fund. But he was the leading candidate to replace Sarkozy as President of France. In France they always cut him slack and said his personal life was no issue, and many now believe he was set up by this West African maid. They are asking why would this man, with the world at his fingertips, seek to force himself on a lowly chambermaid? But the evidence against him is very strong."
Factor Follow Up Segment 
Exploiting the Schwarzenegger scandal?
Various women are coming forth to allege that former California Governor Arnold Schwarzenegger had affairs with them in the past. The Factor examined the allegations with entertainment reporter Kim Serafin. "There have been reports and rumors about Arnold plaguing him throughout his career," Serafin said, "so there might be women who now say that there's concrete proof - a child that resulted from one of these relationships - and maybe people will believe me." Serafin endorsed Schwarzenegger's decision to put his career on hold, saying "you don't want to look like you're jumping right back into acting, he needs to focus on his personal life." The Factor predicted that plenty of folks will try to make money off of Schwarzenegger's past indiscretions: "This just seems unbelievably tawdry. If there's a buck to be made people will come out of the woodwork."
At Your Beck and Call Segment 
Glenn Beck's Israel rally
Glenn Beck has announced plans for rally in Israel this summer. He entered the No Spin Zone to explain why. "I personally think that Jews are in real trouble," Beck said. "You have hundreds of thousands of people gathering in squares in the Middle East chanting 'Kill the Jew.' The 'Arab Spring' is a lie - if you look at the words of Hamas and Hezbollah, they're exactly the same as Hitler. The Middle East is on fire, they're surrounding Israel, and our President is giving it away!" Beck put forth a gloomy vision of the Middle East. "In a year I will buy you a steak if Egypt isn't run by a dictator who is worse or equally as bad as Hosni Mubarak." The Factor took issue with Beck's characterization of President Obama: "He is doing the right thing in trying to reach out to the Arab world and trying to moderate thought there."
Back of Book Segment 
Dumbest things of the week
Continuing their their Friday tradition, Arthel Neville and Greg Gutfeld looked back at the week's dumbest people and events. Neville turned the tables and lauded something she described as very smart. "Suffolk University in Boston asked 1,000 people which reporter they trust the most and you, humble correspondent, won convincingly. And Fox News was the most trusted news agency." Gutfeld went with pop singer Katy Perry, who has issued a set of strict demands to concert promoters. "She has a contract rider for her concerts with things she demands, and one of the things is that the limo driver can't even look at her. She also wants a free breakfast." The Factor singled out the Centers for Disease Control, which is using zombies in a new TV spot that stresses emergency preparedness.
